# SOP-0150: Insurance Claim Documentation and Processing

## Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for insurance claim documentation, processing, and customer advocacy to maximize claim success and customer satisfaction.

## Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in insurance claim projects including inspectors, estimators, project managers, and administrative staff.

## Initial Claim Assessment

### Claim Information Gathering
#### Customer Insurance Details
- **Policy information**: Carrier name, policy number, deductible amount
- **Claim number**: Assigned claim number and adjuster contact information
- **Coverage details**: Dwelling coverage limits, additional coverage types
- **Policy effective dates**: Current policy period and renewal information
- **Previous claims**: History of claims on property within 5 years

#### Damage Assessment Documentation
- **Storm date verification**: Confirmed date of loss and weather records
- **Damage scope identification**: All affected areas and components
- **Age and condition**: Pre-existing conditions vs. storm damage
- **Code compliance**: Current code requirements vs. existing installation
- **Access considerations**: Safety and logistical challenges

### Photographic Documentation Standards

#### Comprehensive Photo Requirements
- **Overall property shots**: Four corners showing full structure
- **Roof overview**: Multiple angles showing entire roof system
- **Detailed damage**: Close-up shots of each damaged area
- **Comparison shots**: Damaged vs. undamaged areas for context
- **Interior damage**: Water intrusion, staining, structural damage

#### Photo Organization and Metadata
- **File naming convention**: Property address_Date_Area_Sequence number
- **GPS coordinates**: Embedded location data for verification
- **Timestamp verification**: Accurate date and time stamps
- **Quality standards**: High resolution, clear focus, proper lighting
- **Backup storage**: Multiple storage locations for data protection

## Adjuster Coordination

### Initial Contact Protocol
- **24-hour notification**: Contact adjuster within 24 hours of assignment
- **Joint inspection scheduling**: Coordinate mutual availability
- **Pre-inspection communication**: Share initial assessment and concerns
- **Documentation sharing**: Provide preliminary photos and measurements
- **Scope alignment**: Discuss assessment approach and standards

### Joint Inspection Process
- **Professional presentation**: Company uniforms, identification, business cards
- **Safety coordination**: Mutual safety protocols and equipment sharing
- **Detailed walkthrough**: Systematic inspection of all affected areas
- **Real-time discussion**: Address concerns and questions immediately
- **Documentation sharing**: Exchange photos and measurements during inspection

### Post-Inspection Follow-up
- **Summary confirmation**: Written summary of joint inspection findings
- **Scope agreement**: Document areas of agreement and disagreement
- **Timeline establishment**: Set expectations for estimate submission
- **Supplemental planning**: Identify potential supplemental claim items
- **Communication schedule**: Regular update intervals and contact methods

## Damage Documentation and Reporting

### Written Damage Assessment
#### Detailed Damage Description
- **Component-by-component analysis**: Systematic evaluation by roof section
- **Damage severity classification**: Minor, moderate, severe, total loss
- **Cause determination**: Storm damage vs. normal wear and tear
- **Code compliance issues**: Current code vs. existing installation
- **Health and safety concerns**: Immediate hazards requiring attention

#### Technical Specifications
- **Material identification**: Brand, model, color, and installation date
- **Measurement documentation**: Square footage, linear feet, quantities
- **Installation standards**: Manufacturer and code requirements
- **Replacement specifications**: Like kind and quality standards
- **Upgrade requirements**: Code compliance necessities

### Estimate Preparation

#### Line-Item Detail Requirements
- **Labor components**: Removal, disposal, installation, cleanup
- **Material costs**: Primary materials, accessories, fasteners
- **Equipment charges**: Crane, dumpster, safety equipment
- **Permit and inspection fees**: All required governmental approvals
- **Overhead and profit**: Standard industry markups

#### Pricing Methodology
- **Local market rates**: Current pricing for geographic area
- **Quantity verification**: Accurate measurements and waste factors
- **Quality specifications**: Match existing materials when possible
- **Code upgrade costs**: Separate identification of improvement items
- **Alternative pricing**: Options for different quality levels

## Supplemental Claim Processing

### Supplemental Damage Identification
- **Hidden damage discovery**: Damage revealed during tear-off
- **Code compliance requirements**: Updates required by current standards
- **Unforeseen complications**: Structural issues, material unavailability
- **Additional storm damage**: Damage missed in initial assessment
- **Scope creep prevention**: Clear documentation of original scope

### Supplemental Documentation Process
- **Immediate notification**: Contact adjuster within 4 hours of discovery
- **Photo documentation**: Before and after photos of discovered issues
- **Written justification**: Clear explanation of necessity and scope
- **Cost documentation**: Detailed pricing for additional work
- **Timeline impact**: Schedule adjustments due to supplemental work

### Approval and Authorization
- **Written approval requirement**: All supplemental work pre-approved
- **Customer communication**: Customer informed of process and timeline
- **Progress payments**: Payment schedule adjustment for additional work
- **Final reconciliation**: Complete accounting of all approved items

## Customer Advocacy and Communication

### Customer Education
- **Claims process explanation**: Step-by-step process overview
- **Rights and responsibilities**: Customer obligations and insurance company duties
- **Deductible clarification**: When and how deductible is collected
- **Timeline expectations**: Realistic schedule for claim resolution
- **Quality standards**: Workmanship and material quality guarantees

### Dispute Resolution Support
- **Documentation review**: Thorough review of insurance company assessments
- **Expert consultation**: Independent adjuster or engineer consultations
- **Regulatory compliance**: State insurance regulation adherence
- **Appeal process support**: Assistance with formal complaint procedures
- **Legal referral**: Connection with appropriate legal representation when needed

### Progress Communication
- **Regular status updates**: Weekly progress reports during claim process
- **Issue notification**: Immediate communication of problems or delays
- **Resolution updates**: Progress on dispute resolution efforts
- **Final closure**: Confirmation of claim settlement and project completion

## Quality Assurance and Compliance

### Documentation Review Process
- **Internal review**: Senior estimator review of all documentation
- **Accuracy verification**: Cross-checking of measurements and pricing
- **Completeness check**: Ensuring all required documentation included
- **Compliance validation**: State and local regulation adherence
- **Quality standards**: Company workmanship and service standards

### Record Keeping Requirements
- **File organization**: Systematic filing of all claim documentation
- **Retention schedule**: 7-year retention minimum for all claim files
- **Digital backup**: Electronic copies stored in multiple locations
- **Access control**: Confidentiality and privacy protection measures
- **Audit preparation**: Documentation ready for insurance or regulatory review

### Performance Metrics
- **Claim approval rate**: Target 95% initial approval rate
- **Supplemental success**: Target 90% supplemental approval rate
- **Customer satisfaction**: Target 4.8/5 customer satisfaction score
- **Timeline compliance**: Target 95% on-time completion rate
- **Documentation quality**: Target 100% completeness and accuracy

# SOP-2097: Emergency Roof Repair Procedures

## 1. Purpose
Establish rapid response procedures for emergency roof repairs, ensuring immediate protection of property and occupants while maintaining safety standards and preparing for permanent repairs in post-storm and crisis situations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all emergency response teams, field crews, and project managers involved in urgent roof repairs including storm damage, structural failures, and life-safety situations requiring immediate response.

## 3. Definitions
- **Emergency Repair**: Immediate temporary repairs to prevent further damage or ensure safety
- **Temporary Protection**: Short-term weatherproofing measures until permanent repairs
- **Life Safety Issue**: Conditions presenting immediate danger to building occupants
- **Catastrophic Damage**: Extensive damage requiring immediate comprehensive response
- **Priority Classification**: System for triaging emergency response needs

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Emergency Response Coordinator**: Overall emergency response management and resource allocation
- **Field Emergency Supervisor**: On-site emergency operations, safety oversight, immediate decisions
- **Emergency Repair Technician**: Hands-on emergency repair work, temporary protection installation
- **Safety Officer**: Emergency safety protocols, hazard assessment, worker protection
- **Customer Communications**: Emergency customer contact, status updates, next steps coordination

## 5. Prerequisites
- 24/7 emergency response system activated
- Emergency repair materials and equipment staged
- Emergency response team on standby
- Safety equipment readily available
- Customer emergency contact information current

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Emergency Materials:
- Tarps and temporary covering materials
- Emergency sealants and adhesives
- Temporary structural supports
- Emergency flashing materials
- Quick-setting repair compounds

### Emergency Equipment:
- Emergency lighting and generators
- Basic repair tools and fasteners
- Safety equipment and fall protection
- Communication devices
- Emergency shelter and protection equipment

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Emergency Response Activation
1. **Initial Contact Assessment**
   - Receive emergency call within 15 minutes
   - Assess urgency and safety level
   - Classify emergency priority level
   - Dispatch appropriate response team
   - Establish estimated arrival time

2. **Emergency Priority Classification**
   - **Priority 1 (Life Safety)**: Active structural collapse risk, immediate danger
   - **Priority 2 (Critical Damage)**: Major water intrusion, significant property damage
   - **Priority 3 (Urgent)**: Moderate damage requiring same-day response
   - **Priority 4 (Standard Emergency)**: Minor damage requiring 24-48 hour response

### 7.2 Initial Site Assessment
1. **Safety Assessment**
   - Evaluate immediate safety hazards
   - Check for structural stability
   - Identify electrical hazards
   - Assess environmental dangers
   - Establish safe work areas

2. **Damage Assessment**
   - Document extent of damage with photos
   - Identify immediate threats to property
   - Assess feasibility of temporary repairs
   - Plan emergency response strategy
   - Communicate findings to customer

### 7.3 Emergency Safety Protocols
1. **Site Safety Establishment**
   - Secure work area and prevent unauthorized access
   - Install emergency lighting if needed
   - Establish fall protection systems
   - Set up emergency communication
   - Plan emergency evacuation routes

2. **Hazard Management**
   - Address electrical hazards immediately
   - Remove debris and unstable materials
   - Protect against further structural damage
   - Control water intrusion and drainage
   - Monitor changing conditions

### 7.4 Temporary Protection Procedures
1. **Immediate Water Control**
   - Stop active water intrusion immediately
   - Install temporary tarps and coverings
   - Redirect water flow away from damage
   - Protect interior spaces and contents
   - Establish temporary drainage

2. **Structural Stabilization**
   - Install temporary structural supports
   - Shore up damaged structural elements
   - Prevent further structural movement
   - Protect load-bearing components
   - Plan for permanent structural repair

### 7.5 Florida Emergency Response Considerations
1. **Hurricane and Storm Response**
   - Prepare for continuing severe weather
   - Use hurricane-rated temporary materials
   - Plan for rapid weather deterioration
   - Coordinate with emergency management
   - Document storm-related damage thoroughly

2. **Lightning and Electrical Storm Safety**
   - Monitor lightning activity continuously
   - Suspend work during electrical storms
   - Protect electronic equipment
   - Plan for power outages
   - Use surge protection equipment

3. **Extreme Heat Emergency Protocols**
   - Monitor heat index and worker safety
   - Provide adequate hydration and cooling
   - Adjust work schedules for extreme heat
   - Recognize heat stress symptoms
   - Plan for heat-related emergencies

### 7.6 Temporary Repair Techniques
1. **Membrane and Surface Repairs**
   - Apply emergency patches and sealants
   - Install temporary membrane systems
   - Use quick-setting repair materials
   - Cover damaged areas with protective materials
   - Document temporary repair locations

2. **Flashing and Penetration Repairs**
   - Seal damaged flashing immediately
   - Apply emergency sealants around penetrations
   - Install temporary flashing systems
   - Redirect water flow around damaged areas
   - Mark areas requiring permanent repair

### 7.7 Emergency Documentation
1. **Damage Documentation**
   - Take comprehensive photos of all damage
   - Document pre-existing conditions
   - Record temporary repair measures taken
   - Note areas requiring permanent repair
   - Prepare insurance documentation

2. **Emergency Response Records**
   - Log response times and actions taken
   - Document materials used and costs
   - Record customer communications
   - Note safety issues and resolutions
   - Prepare handoff documentation for permanent repairs

### 7.8 Customer Communication During Emergencies
1. **Immediate Communication**
   - Contact customer within 30 minutes of arrival
   - Explain immediate actions being taken
   - Provide realistic timeline for temporary protection
   - Discuss next steps and permanent repair planning
   - Document all customer communications

2. **Ongoing Updates**
   - Provide regular status updates during work
   - Explain temporary repair limitations
   - Discuss permanent repair recommendations
   - Coordinate with insurance representatives
   - Schedule follow-up permanent repair assessment

### 7.9 Transition to Permanent Repairs
1. **Permanent Repair Planning**
   - Assess extent of permanent repairs needed
   - Prepare detailed repair estimates
   - Coordinate with insurance adjusters
   - Schedule permanent repair work
   - Plan removal of temporary protections

2. **Quality Handoff**
   - Provide detailed documentation to permanent repair team
   - Brief repair team on temporary measures
   - Transfer all relevant photos and documentation
   - Ensure continuity of customer service
   - Monitor temporary repair performance until permanent completion

## 8. Quality Standards
- Response time: Priority 1 within 2 hours, Priority 2 within 4 hours
- Damage control: Stop active water intrusion within 1 hour
- Safety compliance: Zero safety incidents during emergency response
- Customer communication: Contact within 30 minutes of arrival
- Documentation: Complete photo and written documentation

# SOP-2100: Material Handling and Storage on Site

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for proper handling, storage, and protection of roofing materials on job sites to prevent damage, ensure quality, maintain safety, and optimize project efficiency while complying with manufacturer requirements and safety regulations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all field crews, material handlers, project managers, and subcontractors involved in receiving, storing, handling, and protecting roofing materials at job sites for all types of roofing projects.

## 3. Definitions
- **Material Staging**: Organized placement of materials for efficient access and use
- **Environmental Protection**: Protecting materials from weather and site conditions
- **Inventory Management**: Tracking and controlling material quantities and usage
- **Damage Prevention**: Procedures to prevent material damage during handling and storage
- **FIFO Method**: First In, First Out material usage to prevent deterioration

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Site Supervisor**: Overall material management oversight, storage planning, quality control
- **Material Handler**: Physical handling, storage setup, inventory tracking, damage prevention
- **Equipment Operator**: Crane and hoist operations, material transport, placement assistance
- **Quality Inspector**: Material condition verification, storage compliance, damage assessment
- **Safety Coordinator**: Storage safety, handling procedures, hazard prevention

## 5. Prerequisites
- Site preparation and staging area identification completed
- Material delivery schedule coordinated and confirmed
- Storage equipment and protection materials available
- Weather forecast reviewed for storage planning
- Safety equipment and procedures verified

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Storage Materials:
- Weather protection covers and tarps
- Pallets and blocking materials
- Tie-down straps and securing materials
- Desiccant materials for moisture control
- Storage containers and bins

### Handling Equipment:
- Forklifts and material handlers
- Cranes and hoisting equipment
- Hand trucks and dollies
- Lifting slings and rigging hardware
- Material conveyors and hoists

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Pre-Delivery Site Preparation
1. **Storage Area Planning**
   - Identify optimal storage locations on site
   - Plan for easy access and material flow
   - Ensure adequate space for all materials
   - Verify ground conditions and stability
   - Plan for weather protection needs

2. **Site Safety Setup**
   - Establish safe material handling zones
   - Install barriers and warning signs
   - Plan for equipment operation areas
   - Verify overhead clearances and hazards
   - Establish emergency access routes

### 7.2 Material Delivery and Receiving
1. **Delivery Coordination**
   - Coordinate delivery times with site operations
   - Verify material specifications and quantities
   - Inspect materials upon delivery for damage
   - Document any shipping damage immediately
   - Coordinate with delivery personnel for placement

2. **Receiving Documentation**
   - Verify material quantities against delivery receipts
   - Check material specifications and lot numbers
   - Document condition of materials upon receipt
   - Note any discrepancies or damage
   - Update inventory records immediately

### 7.3 Material Storage Systems
1. **Membrane and Sheet Materials**
   - Store rolls vertically when possible
   - Protect from UV exposure and direct sunlight
   - Maintain temperature control per manufacturer requirements
   - Ensure adequate ventilation around stored materials
   - Rotate stock using FIFO method

2. **Insulation Materials**
   - Store on elevated platforms to prevent moisture contact
   - Protect from weather with waterproof covers
   - Maintain packaging integrity until use
   - Prevent compression and damage
   - Monitor for pest intrusion

3. **Fasteners and Hardware**
   - Store in dry, secure containers
   - Organize by type and size for easy access
   - Protect from corrosion and moisture
   - Maintain manufacturer packaging when possible
   - Secure against theft and loss

4. **Flashing and Metal Materials**
   - Store flat to prevent bending and distortion
   - Separate different metals to prevent galvanic corrosion
   - Protect from scratching and surface damage
   - Maintain protective coatings and films
   - Support long pieces adequately

### 7.4 Florida Climate Protection
1. **Heat Protection**
   - Provide shade structures for temperature-sensitive materials
   - Monitor temperature limits for stored materials
   - Use reflective covers to reduce heat absorption
   - Plan storage orientation to minimize solar exposure
   - Implement cooling measures for critical materials

2. **Moisture Protection**
   - Use waterproof covers and enclosures
   - Elevate materials above potential flooding
   - Provide adequate drainage around storage areas
   - Use moisture barriers and desiccants
   - Monitor humidity levels in enclosed storage

3. **Hurricane and Storm Preparation**
   - Secure all materials against high winds
   - Move materials to protected areas when possible
   - Use heavy-duty tie-downs and anchoring systems
   - Plan for rapid material protection
   - Document storm preparation procedures

### 7.5 Material Handling Procedures
1. **Lifting and Moving**
   - Use proper lifting techniques and equipment
   - Plan lifting operations to prevent damage
   - Provide adequate support for flexible materials
   - Use appropriate rigging and lifting points
   - Coordinate team lifting operations

2. **Equipment Operation**
   - Operate all equipment according to manufacturer guidelines
   - Inspect equipment before use
   - Maintain safe operating distances
   - Use proper lifting capacities and techniques
   - Document equipment usage and maintenance

### 7.6 Inventory Management
1. **Material Tracking**
   - Maintain accurate inventory records
   - Track material usage against project requirements
   - Monitor material condition throughout storage
   - Plan material usage sequence for optimal efficiency
   - Document waste and damaged materials

2. **Quality Control**
   - Inspect materials regularly for deterioration
   - Rotate stock to prevent aging issues
   - Remove damaged materials from inventory
   - Document quality issues and trends
   - Coordinate with suppliers for replacement materials

### 7.7 Safety Procedures
1. **Handling Safety**
   - Train all personnel in proper lifting techniques
   - Use mechanical aids to reduce manual handling
   - Provide appropriate PPE for all handling operations
   - Maintain clear walkways and work areas
   - Implement lockout/tagout procedures for equipment

2. **Storage Safety**
   - Secure all stored materials against falling
   - Maintain stable stacking and storage configurations
   - Provide fall protection where needed
   - Install proper lighting for storage areas
   - Establish emergency response procedures

### 7.8 Waste and Damage Management
1. **Damage Assessment**
   - Inspect materials regularly for damage
   - Document damage with photos and descriptions
   - Determine if damaged materials can be used
   - Coordinate with suppliers for replacement
   - Track damage costs and causes

2. **Waste Management**
   - Separate waste materials by type for recycling
   - Properly dispose of hazardous materials
   - Document waste quantities and disposal methods
   - Minimize waste through careful handling
   - Coordinate with waste management vendors

## 8. Quality Standards
- Material condition: Zero preventable damage during storage
- Inventory accuracy: 100% accurate material tracking
- Storage compliance: Full compliance with manufacturer requirements
- Safety performance: Zero material handling incidents
- Efficiency: Optimal material flow and accessibility

## 9. Safety Requirements
- Proper material handling training for all personnel
- Use of appropriate PPE for all handling operations
- Equipment operation safety procedures
- Fall protection for elevated storage and handling
- Emergency response procedures for material incidents

## 10. Environmental Considerations
- Proper disposal and recycling of packaging materials
- Minimization of waste and material damage
- Protection of surrounding environment during storage
- Use of environmentally friendly storage methods
- Compliance with environmental regulations

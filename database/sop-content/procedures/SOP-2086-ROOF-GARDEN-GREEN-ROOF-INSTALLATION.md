# SOP-2086: Roof Garden and Green Roof Installation

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for installing roof gardens and green roof systems, ensuring structural integrity, proper drainage, and long-term sustainability while complying with Florida building codes and environmental regulations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all field crews, structural engineers, project managers, and specialized green roof contractors involved in extensive and intensive green roof installations for commercial and residential projects.

## 3. Definitions
- **Extensive Green Roof**: Lightweight system with shallow growing medium (2-6 inches), typically supporting sedums and grasses
- **Intensive Green Roof**: Heavier system with deeper growing medium (6-24+ inches), supporting diverse plant types including trees
- **Growing Medium**: Specially formulated lightweight soil mixture designed for roof applications
- **Root Barrier**: Membrane system preventing plant roots from penetrating waterproof membrane
- **Bioretention**: System designed to manage stormwater runoff through natural filtration

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Overall coordination, permit compliance, stakeholder communication
- **Structural Engineer**: Load calculations, structural adequacy verification, design approval
- **Green Roof Specialist**: System design, plant selection, installation oversight
- **Waterproofing Technician**: Membrane installation, root barrier application, leak prevention
- **Landscape Specialist**: Plant installation, irrigation setup, maintenance planning

## 5. Prerequisites
- Structural engineering analysis and approval
- Building permits and environmental approvals
- Waterproofing system installation and testing
- Access equipment and safety systems in place
- Plant material procurement and staging

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Materials:
- Waterproof membrane system
- Root barrier membrane
- Drainage layer materials
- Filter fabric
- Growing medium (certified lightweight mix)
- Plant materials (Florida-native preferred)
- Irrigation system components

### Equipment:
- Crane or material hoist systems
- Soil mixing and placement equipment
- Compaction equipment
- Water testing equipment
- Plant transportation containers

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Structural Verification
1. **Load Analysis**
   - Verify structural capacity for saturated weight
   - Calculate live and dead load requirements
   - Assess wind uplift resistance
   - Document engineering approvals
   - Plan for maintenance equipment loads

2. **Deck Preparation**
   - Inspect structural deck for defects
   - Verify proper slope (minimum 1/4" per foot)
   - Install additional drainage if required
   - Ensure proper attachment points
   - Complete waterproofing system

### 7.2 Waterproofing and Protection
1. **Membrane Installation**
   - Install primary waterproof membrane
   - Apply root barrier system
   - Install protection board
   - Test for leaks using flood testing
   - Document all penetrations and seals

2. **Drainage System**
   - Install primary and overflow drains
   - Place drainage layer materials
   - Install filter fabric over drainage
   - Test drainage capacity and flow rates
   - Create inspection access points

### 7.3 Growing Medium Installation
1. **Material Specification**
   - Use certified lightweight growing medium
   - Verify organic content (10-15%)
   - Test pH and nutrient levels
   - Ensure proper drainage characteristics
   - Document material certifications

2. **Placement and Preparation**
   - Transport medium to roof in stages
   - Place and level to specified depths
   - Compact lightly to prevent settling
   - Install irrigation system
   - Test soil moisture and drainage

### 7.4 Plant Installation
1. **Plant Selection (Florida-Specific)**
   - Native and adapted species preferred
   - Drought-tolerant varieties
   - Wind-resistant selections
   - Hurricane-tolerant plants
   - Consider mature size and root systems

2. **Installation Process**
   - Plant during optimal weather conditions
   - Follow proper spacing requirements
   - Ensure adequate root-to-soil contact
   - Apply initial watering and fertilization
   - Install plant protection as needed

### 7.5 Florida Climate Adaptations
1. **Hurricane Preparation**
   - Select wind-resistant plant varieties
   - Secure loose materials and equipment
   - Design drainage for extreme rainfall
   - Plan for post-storm recovery
   - Document emergency procedures

2. **Heat and Humidity Management**
   - Choose heat-tolerant plant species
   - Design adequate irrigation systems
   - Plan for summer stress management
   - Monitor soil temperature and moisture
   - Provide temporary shade during establishment

3. **Stormwater Management**
   - Design for 100-year storm events
   - Install overflow drainage systems
   - Plan for rapid water removal
   - Monitor performance during storms
   - Maintain detention/retention capacity

### 7.6 Irrigation and Maintenance Systems
1. **Irrigation Design**
   - Install efficient drip irrigation
   - Include soil moisture sensors
   - Design for water conservation
   - Plan for seasonal adjustments
   - Include emergency watering capacity

2. **Monitoring Systems**
   - Install structural monitoring equipment
   - Monitor drainage performance
   - Track plant health and establishment
   - Measure stormwater retention
   - Document system performance

## 8. Quality Standards
- Structural load compliance: 100%
- Waterproofing integrity: Zero leaks
- Drainage performance: No standing water >24 hours
- Plant survival rate: >90% after first year
- Stormwater retention: Meet design specifications

## 9. Safety Requirements
- Fall protection for all elevated work
- Crane operation safety protocols
- Heavy lifting and material handling procedures
- Chemical safety for fertilizers and pesticides
- Emergency response for structural issues

## 10. Environmental Considerations

- Stormwater quality improvement
- Urban heat island reduction
- Habitat creation for local wildlife
- Compliance with environmental regulations

# SOP-2120: Site Cleanup and Restoration

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for job site cleanup and restoration activities, ensuring complete debris removal, property protection, customer satisfaction, and environmental compliance while maintaining professional standards and safety protocols.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all field crews, cleanup specialists, project managers, and subcontractors involved in job site cleanup, debris removal, and property restoration for all types of roofing projects.

## 3. Definitions
- **Final Cleanup**: Comprehensive cleaning and restoration at project completion
- **Progressive Cleanup**: Ongoing cleanup throughout project duration
- **Debris Removal**: Systematic removal and disposal of construction waste
- **Property Restoration**: Returning customer property to pre-construction condition
- **Environmental Compliance**: Adherence to waste disposal and environmental regulations

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Cleanup oversight, customer satisfaction, final inspection
- **Cleanup Supervisor**: Cleanup coordination, quality control, team management
- **Cleanup Crew**: Physical cleanup work, debris removal, property protection
- **Equipment Operator**: Equipment cleanup, material loading, transport assistance
- **Quality Inspector**: Final inspection, customer walkthrough, completion verification

## 5. Prerequisites
- Construction work completed and inspected
- Safety equipment and cleanup materials available
- Waste disposal arrangements confirmed
- Customer notification of cleanup schedule
- Weather conditions suitable for cleanup activities

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Cleanup Materials:
- Waste containers and disposal bags
- Cleaning supplies and detergents
- Property protection materials
- Landscaping restoration materials
- Touch-up paints and sealers

### Cleanup Equipment:
- Magnetic sweepers for nail collection
- Blowers and vacuum equipment
- Pressure washers and cleaning equipment
- Hand tools and collection equipment
- Material handling and loading equipment

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Progressive Cleanup During Construction
1. **Daily Cleanup Protocols**
   - Remove debris and waste materials daily
   - Sweep work areas and access routes
   - Maintain clean and safe work environment
   - Protect customer property from damage
   - Secure tools and materials each day

2. **Weekly Comprehensive Cleanup**
   - Conduct thorough site cleanup weekly
   - Remove accumulated debris and waste
   - Clean surrounding areas and walkways
   - Inspect and maintain property protection
   - Address customer concerns about cleanliness

### 7.2 Debris Collection and Sorting
1. **Roofing Material Removal**
   - Collect all old roofing materials systematically
   - Separate materials by type for proper disposal
   - Use tarps and containers to prevent scatter
   - Check for hazardous materials (asbestos, lead)
   - Document quantities for disposal tracking

2. **Construction Waste Management**
   - Separate recyclable materials from waste
   - Collect nails, screws, and metal fasteners
   - Remove packaging materials and containers
   - Dispose of hazardous materials properly
   - Document waste disposal compliance

### 7.3 Magnetic Sweeping for Nails and Fasteners
1. **Systematic Sweeping Pattern**
   - Use magnetic sweepers on all accessible areas
   - Sweep in overlapping patterns for complete coverage
   - Pay special attention to driveways and walkways
   - Check landscaped areas and flower beds
   - Repeat sweeping after equipment removal

2. **Manual Nail Collection**
   - Conduct hand pickup in areas inaccessible to equipment
   - Check gutters and drainage areas
   - Inspect around buildings and structures
   - Remove nails from landscaping and mulch areas
   - Use metal detectors for thorough nail detection

### 7.4 Property Protection and Restoration
1. **Landscape Protection and Restoration**
   - Remove protective coverings carefully
   - Inspect for any damage to plants or grass
   - Replace or restore damaged landscaping
   - Clean and restore outdoor furniture and fixtures
   - Water and care for stressed vegetation

2. **Hardscape Cleaning**
   - Pressure wash driveways, sidewalks, and patios
   - Remove stains and construction debris
   - Clean windows and exterior surfaces
   - Restore outdoor lighting and fixtures
   - Touch up paint and finishes as needed

### 7.5 Florida Climate Cleanup Considerations
1. **Hurricane Season Preparation**
   - Secure all debris and loose materials
   - Complete cleanup before storm seasons
   - Remove potential wind-blown debris
   - Ensure drainage systems are clear
   - Document storm preparedness completion

2. **Heat and Humidity Management**
   - Schedule intensive cleanup during cooler hours
   - Provide adequate hydration for cleanup crews
   - Protect cleaning materials from heat damage
   - Monitor crew safety during extreme heat
   - Adjust cleanup schedules for weather conditions

3. **Rapid Vegetation Growth**
   - Address landscape damage promptly
   - Plant replacement vegetation during optimal seasons
   - Plan for rapid growth and maintenance needs
   - Use native plants for quick establishment
   - Monitor and maintain restored areas

### 7.6 Equipment and Material Removal
1. **Equipment Demobilization**
   - Remove all construction equipment systematically
   - Clean equipment before removal
   - Inspect for any site damage from equipment
   - Restore access routes and staging areas
   - Document equipment removal completion

2. **Material and Tool Collection**
   - Account for all tools and equipment
   - Remove all excess materials from site
   - Clean and organize remaining materials
   - Return unused materials to inventory
   - Document material usage and waste

### 7.7 Interior Cleanup (When Applicable)
1. **Dust and Debris Removal**
   - Remove construction dust from interior spaces
   - Clean all surfaces affected by construction
   - Vacuum HVAC systems and replace filters
   - Clean windows and glass surfaces
   - Restore interior furnishings and equipment

2. **Final Interior Inspection**
   - Check for any water damage or moisture issues
   - Verify proper operation of all systems
   - Test for any air quality issues
   - Address any customer concerns
   - Document interior condition

### 7.8 Final Site Inspection and Customer Walkthrough
1. **Comprehensive Site Inspection**
   - Inspect entire property for cleanup completeness
   - Check all areas for missed debris or damage
   - Verify restoration of all protected areas
   - Test all systems for proper operation
   - Document final site condition

2. **Customer Walkthrough**
   - Conduct detailed walkthrough with customer
   - Address any customer concerns or issues
   - Explain maintenance and care instructions
   - Provide warranty and service information
   - Obtain customer satisfaction confirmation

### 7.9 Waste Disposal and Environmental Compliance
1. **Proper Waste Disposal**
   - Transport waste to approved disposal facilities
   - Separate hazardous materials for special disposal
   - Obtain disposal receipts and documentation
   - Track waste quantities and disposal costs
   - Ensure compliance with local regulations

2. **Environmental Protection**
   - Prevent runoff and environmental contamination
   - Use environmentally friendly cleaning products
   - Protect storm drains and water sources
   - Minimize noise and dust during cleanup
   - Follow all environmental regulations

### 7.10 Documentation and Quality Assurance
1. **Cleanup Documentation**
   - Take before and after photos of all areas
   - Document all cleanup activities completed
   - Record any property damage and repairs
   - Maintain waste disposal documentation
   - Prepare final cleanup completion report

2. **Quality Verification**
   - Verify all cleanup standards met
   - Confirm customer satisfaction with cleanup
   - Address any deficiencies immediately
   - Document quality compliance
   - Update cleanup procedures based on feedback

## 8. Quality Standards
- Debris removal: 100% removal of construction debris
- Nail collection: Zero nails remaining on accessible surfaces
- Property restoration: Complete restoration to pre-construction condition
- Customer satisfaction: Minimum 4.5/5 rating for cleanup
- Environmental compliance: 100% compliance with disposal regulations

## 9. Safety Requirements

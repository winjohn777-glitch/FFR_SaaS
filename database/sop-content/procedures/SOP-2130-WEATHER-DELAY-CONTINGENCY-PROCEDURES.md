# SOP-2130: Weather Delay and Contingency Procedures

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for managing weather delays and implementing contingency plans to maintain project schedules, ensure worker safety, protect materials and work in progress, and maintain customer communication during adverse weather conditions.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all project managers, field supervisors, and crew members involved in weather monitoring, delay management, and contingency planning for all roofing projects during Florida's variable weather conditions.

## 3. Definitions
- **Weather Window**: Period of suitable weather conditions for roofing work
- **Weather Delay**: Work stoppage due to unsafe or unsuitable weather conditions
- **Contingency Plan**: Alternative procedures activated during weather delays
- **Critical Weather Threshold**: Specific weather conditions requiring work cessation
- **Recovery Plan**: Procedures for resuming work after weather delays

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Weather monitoring, contingency planning, customer communication
- **Field Supervisor**: Real-time weather assessment, crew safety, work protection
- **Weather Monitor**: Continuous weather tracking, forecast analysis, alert distribution
- **Crew Leader**: Immediate response to weather changes, material protection, crew safety
- **Customer Service**: Customer communication, schedule updates, expectation management

## 5. Prerequisites
- Weather monitoring systems and alerts established
- Contingency plans developed for project-specific conditions
- Emergency contact procedures activated
- Material protection equipment available
- Crew safety training completed

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Weather Protection Materials:

- Weather monitoring devices and apps

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Weather Monitoring and Forecasting
1. **Continuous Weather Monitoring**
   - Monitor weather conditions every 2 hours during work
   - Use multiple weather sources for accuracy
   - Track radar and satellite imagery
   - Monitor National Weather Service alerts
   - Document weather conditions throughout day

2. **Advanced Weather Planning**
   - Review extended forecasts weekly
   - Plan project phases around weather windows
   - Coordinate critical work during optimal conditions
   - Schedule weather-sensitive activities appropriately
   - Communicate forecast changes to all stakeholders

### 7.2 Critical Weather Thresholds
1. **Wind Speed Limitations**
   - **15 mph sustained**: Cease membrane installation and sheet material work
   - **20 mph sustained**: Cease all elevated work
   - **25 mph sustained**: Secure all materials and evacuate roof
   - **35 mph sustained**: Complete work stoppage and site securing

2. **Precipitation Thresholds**
   - **Light rain/drizzle**: Cease waterproofing work
   - **Moderate rain**: Protect open work areas and materials
   - **Heavy rain**: Complete work stoppage and area securing
   - **Thunderstorms**: Immediate evacuation and electrical safety protocols

3. **Temperature Limitations**
   - **Below 40°F**: Cease adhesive and sealant applications
   - **Above 95°F**: Implement heat stress protocols
   - **Above 100°F**: Consider work suspension during peak hours
   - **Extreme heat index**: Mandatory work suspension protocols

### 7.3 Florida-Specific Weather Considerations
1. **Hurricane and Tropical Storm Protocols**
   - Monitor tropical weather systems continuously
   - Implement 72-hour preparation procedures
   - Secure all materials and equipment
   - Complete temporary protection of work areas
   - Evacuate site and equipment as necessary

2. **Afternoon Thunderstorm Management**
   - Plan morning work for weather-sensitive activities
   - Monitor afternoon storm development
   - Implement rapid protection procedures
   - Plan for daily weather pattern variations
   - Coordinate with customer for schedule adjustments

3. **Lightning Safety Protocols**
   - Cease all work at first sign of lightning
   - Evacuate elevated areas immediately
   - Wait 30 minutes after last lightning before resuming
   - Protect electronic equipment from surges
   - Implement grounding and electrical safety measures

### 7.4 Work Protection Procedures
1. **Immediate Protection Response**
   - Cover open roof areas within 15 minutes of weather alert
   - Secure all loose materials and tools
   - Install temporary drainage systems
   - Protect electrical equipment and systems
   - Document protection measures taken

2. **Extended Weather Protection**
   - Install comprehensive temporary roofing systems
   - Implement long-term drainage solutions
   - Secure equipment for extended periods
   - Plan for material preservation
   - Monitor protected areas regularly

### 7.5 Material and Equipment Protection
1. **Material Securing Procedures**
   - Cover all weather-sensitive materials
   - Secure materials against wind uplift
   - Elevate materials above potential flooding
   - Implement moisture control measures
   - Monitor material condition during delays

2. **Equipment Protection**
   - Secure all equipment against wind and weather
   - Protect electrical systems from moisture
   - Drain and secure pneumatic systems
   - Cover and protect sensitive equipment
   - Document equipment protection measures

### 7.6 Crew Safety and Management
1. **Safety Protocols During Weather**
   - Implement enhanced safety procedures
   - Monitor crew for heat stress or cold exposure
   - Provide appropriate protective equipment
   - Establish safe evacuation procedures
   - Maintain communication with all crew members

2. **Alternative Work Planning**
   - Plan indoor or sheltered work activities
   - Schedule material preparation and maintenance
   - Conduct training and safety meetings
   - Perform equipment maintenance and repairs
   - Plan future work phases and logistics

### 7.7 Customer Communication
1. **Proactive Communication**
   - Notify customers of potential weather delays
   - Explain weather safety requirements
   - Provide updated project timelines
   - Discuss protection measures being taken
   - Maintain regular communication during delays

2. **Delay Management Communication**
   - Notify customers immediately of work delays
   - Explain specific weather conditions causing delays
   - Provide realistic timeline for work resumption
   - Update customers on protection measures
   - Coordinate access and security during delays

### 7.8 Work Resumption Procedures
1. **Post-Weather Assessment**
   - Inspect all work areas for weather damage
   - Check material condition and quality
   - Assess equipment functionality
   - Verify safety systems and protection
   - Document any weather-related issues

2. **Gradual Work Resumption**
   - Resume work gradually as conditions improve
   - Prioritize critical and weather-sensitive work
   - Monitor changing weather conditions
   - Adjust work schedules based on conditions
   - Communicate resumption plans to all stakeholders

### 7.9 Schedule Recovery Planning
1. **Schedule Adjustment Strategies**
   - Extend work hours during favorable weather
   - Add additional crew members when possible
   - Prioritize critical path activities
   - Coordinate with other trades and contractors
   - Implement overtime and weekend work as needed

2. **Resource Reallocation**
   - Reassign crews to weather-protected work
   - Prioritize multiple projects based on weather windows
   - Coordinate equipment and material delivery
   - Adjust project sequence for optimal weather utilization
   - Plan for make-up work during favorable periods

### 7.10 Documentation and Reporting
1. **Weather Delay Documentation**
   - Record all weather delays and durations
   - Document weather conditions and measurements
   - Track protection measures implemented
   - Record crew hours and costs during delays
   - Maintain photos of weather protection measures

2. **Impact Analysis and Reporting**
   - Analyze weather delay impacts on project schedule
   - Calculate costs associated with weather delays
   - Report weather delays to customers and stakeholders
   - Document lessons learned for future projects
   - Update contingency plans based on experience

## 8. Quality Standards
- Weather monitoring: Continuous monitoring during all work hours
- Response time: Implement protection measures within 15 minutes
- Safety compliance: Zero weather-related safety incidents
- Material protection: No weather damage to materials or work
- Communication: Immediate notification of all weather delays

# SOP-2010: Material Procurement and Vendor Management

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for procuring roofing materials, managing vendor relationships, and ensuring quality supply chain operations to support efficient project delivery and cost-effective business operations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in material ordering, vendor selection, quality control, inventory management, and supplier relationship management for residential and commercial roofing projects throughout Florida.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Procurement Manager**: Oversee vendor relationships and material sourcing strategies
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate material requirements and delivery schedules
- **Warehouse Manager**: Manage inventory levels and material quality control
- **Site Supervisor**: Verify material deliveries and job site requirements
- **Accounting Department**: Process purchase orders and vendor payments

### Procurement Systems:
- Material requirements planning (MRP) software
- Vendor management and contact database
- Purchase order processing and tracking systems
- Inventory management and tracking applications

### Communication Tools:

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Verify all materials meet applicable safety standards and certifications
- Ensure proper material handling and storage safety requirements
- Coordinate delivery safety protocols with site operations
- Maintain current safety data sheets (SDS) for all materials
- Address any special handling requirements for hazardous materials

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Material Requirements Planning
1. **Project Analysis and Quantification**:
   - Review project drawings and specifications for material requirements
   - Calculate quantities using standardized measurement and waste factors
   - Identify specialized materials or custom orders requiring lead time
   - Coordinate with project manager on timeline and delivery requirements
   - Account for storage limitations and phased delivery needs

2. **Material Specification Verification**:
   - **Roofing Materials**: Shingles, tiles, membrane systems, metal panels
   - **Underlayment**: Synthetic, felt, ice and water shield products
   - **Flashing**: Step, valley, penetration, and edge flashing materials
   - **Fasteners**: Nails, screws, adhesives appropriate for substrates
   - **Accessories**: Vents, gutters, trim, and specialty components

3. **Quality and Compliance Requirements**:
   - Verify Florida Building Code compliance and approval numbers
   - Confirm wind resistance ratings and hurricane performance
   - Check manufacturer warranties and installation requirements
   - Ensure compatibility between different material systems
   - Verify energy code compliance for new construction projects

### Phase 2: Vendor Selection and Evaluation
1. **Vendor Assessment Criteria**:
   - **Product Quality**: Consistent manufacturing standards and certifications
   - **Delivery Performance**: On-time delivery and schedule reliability
   - **Pricing Competitiveness**: Fair pricing and volume discount structures
   - **Service Quality**: Technical support and problem resolution capabilities
   - **Financial Stability**: Credit worthiness and business continuity

2. **Preferred Vendor Network Management**:
   - Maintain database of qualified suppliers and performance metrics
   - Establish master agreements for pricing and delivery terms
   - Implement vendor scorecard system for performance tracking
   - Conduct regular vendor performance reviews and improvement planning
   - Develop backup supplier relationships for critical materials

3. **New Vendor Qualification Process**:
   - Evaluate financial stability and business references
   - Assess product quality through sample testing and evaluation
   - Verify insurance coverage and contractor licensing requirements
   - Conduct facility inspections for quality control capabilities
   - Establish terms and conditions for purchasing agreements

### Phase 3: Purchase Order Processing
1. **Order Preparation and Authorization**:
   - Create detailed purchase orders with specifications and delivery requirements
   - Obtain required approvals based on dollar amount and authorization levels
   - Verify pricing against current agreements and market conditions
   - Confirm delivery dates and coordinate with project schedules
   - Include special instructions for delivery and site requirements

2. **Order Placement and Confirmation**:
   - Submit purchase orders through approved electronic systems
   - Obtain vendor acknowledgment and delivery confirmation
   - Track order status and communicate updates to project teams
   - Monitor for any changes in delivery schedules or product availability
   - Coordinate with warehouse for receiving and inspection procedures

3. **Expediting and Follow-up**:
   - Monitor critical path materials and expedite when necessary
   - Maintain communication with vendors on delivery status
   - Coordinate alternative sources for urgent or back-ordered items
   - Update project teams on any delivery delays or material changes
   - Document vendor performance for future evaluation purposes

### Phase 4: Delivery and Quality Control
1. **Receiving and Inspection Procedures**:
   - Verify delivery against purchase order quantities and specifications
   - Inspect materials for damage, defects, or quality issues
   - Check for proper labeling, certifications, and compliance markings
   - Document any discrepancies or damage for vendor coordination
   - Accept or reject deliveries based on quality standards

2. **Storage and Inventory Management**:
   - Store materials according to manufacturer requirements and environmental conditions
   - Implement first-in, first-out (FIFO) inventory rotation procedures
   - Maintain accurate inventory records and tracking systems
   - Protect materials from weather damage and theft
   - Coordinate material distribution to job sites as needed

3. **Documentation and Record Keeping**:
   - Maintain complete delivery documentation and receiving reports
   - Track material usage and waste factors for future planning
   - Document vendor performance and quality issues
   - Manage warranty registration and product documentation
   - Update inventory systems with current stock levels and locations

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Order accuracy: 99% correct material delivery against purchase orders
- Delivery performance: 95% on-time delivery within agreed schedule windows
- Material quality: Zero defective materials accepted into inventory
- Cost variance: Materials costs within 2% of budgeted amounts
- Vendor performance: 95% overall vendor scorecard ratings

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Delivery delays**: Expedite orders and coordinate alternative sources
- **Quality problems**: Return defective materials and implement corrective actions
- **Quantity discrepancies**: Verify orders and coordinate shortage resolution
- **Pricing disputes**: Review agreements and negotiate fair resolutions
- **Vendor performance issues**: Implement improvement plans or change suppliers

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document issue details and impact on project schedules
2. Contact vendor immediately to assess situation and options
3. Implement immediate workarounds to minimize project delays
4. Negotiate resolution terms and delivery alternatives
5. Update vendor performance records and improvement plans

# SOP-1002: Guardrail System Installation and Maintenance

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for the proper installation, inspection, and maintenance of guardrail systems to ensure worker safety, OSHA compliance, and fall protection effectiveness on all roofing projects.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all supervisors, safety coordinators, and workers involved in guardrail system installation, use, and maintenance on residential and commercial roofing projects throughout Florida.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Safety Coordinator**: Oversee guardrail system compliance and training
- **Site Supervisor**: Ensure proper installation and daily inspection
- **Lead Installer**: Execute installation procedures and quality checks
- **All Workers**: Follow proper use and maintenance protocols
- **Safety Inspector**: Conduct formal inspections and compliance audits

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Guardrail Components:
- Top rail assemblies (minimum 42 inches high)
- Mid-rail assemblies (19-23 inches above surface)
- Support posts (maximum 8-foot spacing)
- Toe boards (minimum 3.5 inches high when required)
- Connectors, brackets, and fastening hardware

### Installation Tools:
- Torque wrenches calibrated for specific fastener requirements
- Measuring devices and levels for accurate positioning
- Power drills with appropriate bits and attachments
- Safety equipment for installers (harnesses, lanyards)
- Load testing equipment for verification

## 5. Safety Considerations
- All installers must wear fall protection during guardrail installation
- Verify structural capacity before attachment (minimum 200 lbs per post)
- Maintain 6-foot minimum distance from unprotected edges during installation
- Use temporary fall protection until guardrail system is complete
- Conduct immediate inspection after severe weather events

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Site Assessment and Planning
1. **Structural Evaluation**:
   - Review building plans and structural specifications
   - Identify attachment points and verify load capacity
   - Assess roof conditions and surface materials
   - Determine appropriate guardrail system type
   - Plan installation sequence and safety zones

2. **Measurement and Layout**:
   - Mark post locations using chalk lines or temporary markers
   - Verify 8-foot maximum spacing between posts
   - Identify corners, gates, and special connection points
   - Plan for proper rail alignment and elevation
   - Account for roof slope and surface variations

### Phase 2: Component Preparation and Staging
1. **Material Inspection**:
   - Verify all components meet OSHA specifications
   - Check for manufacturer defects or shipping damage
   - Confirm proper hardware and fastener grades
   - Validate load testing certificates and documentation
   - Organize components by installation sequence

2. **Tool Preparation**:
   - Calibrate torque wrenches to manufacturer specifications
   - Test all power tools and safety equipment
   - Verify measuring devices are accurate and functional
   - Prepare temporary storage areas for components
   - Stage materials at strategic locations for efficient access

### Phase 3: Installation Procedures
1. **Post Installation**:
   - Mark exact post locations on roof surface
   - Drill pilot holes according to manufacturer specifications
   - Install posts using appropriate fasteners and torque values
   - Verify post plumb and alignment using levels
   - Apply sealants around penetrations as required

2. **Rail Installation**:
   - Install top rail at 42 inches (±3 inches) above working surface
   - Install mid-rail at 21 inches (±3 inches) above working surface
   - Ensure smooth surface and absence of protruding objects
   - Verify 200-pound load capacity in any direction
   - Install toe boards when materials or tools could fall

3. **Connection and Finishing**:
   - Secure all connections using specified hardware
   - Apply proper torque values and verify tightness
   - Install end caps and safety features
   - Complete final alignment and elevation adjustments
   - Apply protective coatings or treatments as required

### Phase 4: Inspection and Testing
1. **Load Testing Requirements**:
   - Apply 200-pound test load to top rail in outward direction
   - Test at mid-span between posts and at connections
   - Verify no permanent deformation or failure occurs
   - Document test results and component performance
   - Address any deficiencies before system approval

2. **Final Inspection Checklist**:
   - Verify all components are properly installed and secured
   - Check rail heights and spacing compliance
   - Confirm structural integrity and load capacity
   - Inspect for sharp edges, protruding objects, or hazards
   - Validate toe board installation where required

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Installation accuracy: ±1 inch tolerance for rail heights and spacing
- Load capacity: Minimum 200 pounds in any direction
- Structural integrity: Zero permanent deformation under test loads
- Installation time: Maximum 2 hours per 100 linear feet
- Weather resistance: System must withstand 90 mph wind loads

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Insufficient load capacity**: Verify attachment methods and structural support
- **Rail misalignment**: Check post installation and adjust connections
- **Loose connections**: Re-torque fasteners and inspect for damage
- **Weather-related damage**: Inspect after storms and repair as needed
- **Structural interference**: Modify installation to accommodate roof features

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Stop work immediately if safety is compromised
2. Assess the extent of the problem and potential solutions
3. Consult structural engineer if load capacity is questionable
4. Implement repairs using approved materials and methods
5. Re-test system before returning to service

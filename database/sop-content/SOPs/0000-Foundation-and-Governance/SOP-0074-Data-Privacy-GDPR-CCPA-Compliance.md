## BLOCK 1: PURPOSE - Strategic Intent and Business Objectives

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual, including names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, IP addresses, and any other data that can identify a person directly or indirectly.

**Data Subject:** An individual whose personal data is being processed by the organization, including customers, employees, website visitors, and any other persons whose data is collected or processed.

**Data Controller:** The entity that determines the purposes and means of personal data processing, which is Florida First Roofing LLC for most customer and business data processing activities.

**Data Processor:** Third-party entities that process personal data on behalf of the data controller, including cloud service providers, marketing platforms, and other vendors.

**Lawful Basis:** The legal justification for processing personal data under privacy regulations, including consent, contract performance, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests.

**Privacy by Design:** The principle of integrating privacy protection into all business processes, system designs, and operational activities from the earliest stages rather than as an afterthought.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Privacy Compliance Framework Implementation

**Phase 1: Privacy Assessment and Gap Analysis (Weeks 1-4)**
1. Conduct comprehensive data inventory and mapping of all personal data processing activities
2. Assess current privacy practices against GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable regulations
3. Identify privacy compliance gaps and develop remediation plan with priorities
4. Review and update privacy policies, notices, and consent mechanisms
5. Establish privacy governance structure including DPO appointment and committee formation

**Phase 2: Technical and Administrative Controls Implementation (Weeks 5-12)**
1. Implement technical privacy controls including data encryption, access controls, and monitoring
2. Deploy consent management platform for website and marketing communications
3. Configure data subject rights fulfillment processes including automated workflows
4. Establish data retention and deletion procedures with automated enforcement
5. Implement privacy impact assessment procedures for new projects and systems

**Phase 3: Training and Ongoing Compliance (Weeks 13-16)**
1. Conduct comprehensive privacy training for all personnel handling personal data
2. Implement ongoing privacy monitoring and compliance assessment procedures
3. Establish vendor privacy assessment and contract compliance procedures
4. Deploy privacy incident response procedures integrated with security incident management
5. Create privacy compliance reporting and management dashboard

### 5.2 Data Subject Rights Management

**Rights Request Processing Procedures:**
1. Receive and log data subject rights requests through multiple channels (email, phone, web form)
2. Verify identity of data subject making the request using established verification procedures
3. Assess scope and validity of request and determine appropriate response actions
4. Coordinate with relevant departments to gather requested information or implement changes
5. Respond to data subject within required timeframes with requested information or actions

**Specific Rights Implementation:**
- **Right of Access:** Provide complete copy of personal data and processing information
- **Right to Rectification:** Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data promptly
- **Right to Erasure:** Delete personal data when legally required or requested
- **Right to Portability:** Provide personal data in structured, machine-readable format
- **Right to Object:** Stop processing personal data for specific purposes when requested

### 5.3 Privacy Breach Response and Incident Management

**Privacy Breach Detection and Assessment:**
1. Identify potential privacy breaches through security monitoring, employee reports, or external notifications
2. Assess scope, severity, and potential impact of privacy breach on affected data subjects
3. Determine regulatory notification requirements based on breach characteristics and applicable laws
4. Coordinate with security team for technical breach containment and investigation
5. Document all breach response activities and maintain compliance records

**Regulatory Notification and Communication:**
1. Notify relevant data protection authorities within required timeframes (72 hours for GDPR)
2. Communicate with affected data subjects when required by law or when breach poses high risk
3. Coordinate with legal counsel and public relations team for external communications
4. Implement additional privacy controls and monitoring to prevent similar incidents
5. Conduct post-breach review and update privacy procedures based on lessons learned

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

### 3.1 Electronic Signature Legal Framework
**Electronic Signature:** Electronic sound, symbol, or process attached to or logically associated with a record and executed or adopted by a person with intent to sign
**Digital Signature:** Cryptographic mechanism providing authentication, non-repudiation, and data integrity verification
**Electronic Record:** Contract or other record created, generated, sent, communicated, received, or stored by electronic means
**Authentication:** Process of verifying the identity of a person or system attempting to access electronic documents

### 3.2 Security and Verification Terms
**Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Security process requiring multiple verification methods including knowledge, possession, and inherence factors
**Certificate Authority (CA):** Trusted entity issuing digital certificates for public key cryptography
**Public Key Infrastructure (PKI):** Framework managing digital keys and certificates for secure electronic communication
**Audit Trail:** Chronological record of system activities providing documentary evidence of electronic signature processes

### 3.3 Compliance and Legal Terms
**Florida Electronic Signature Act:** State legislation governing electronic signature validity and legal requirements
**Federal E-SIGN Act:** Federal law establishing legal framework for electronic signatures in interstate commerce
**Non-Repudiation:** Assurance that someone cannot deny the validity of their signature on a document
**Legal Validity:** Enforceability and admissibility of electronically signed documents in legal proceedings

## BLOCK 6: STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Standard Electronic Signature Process
**Step 1: Document Preparation and Signature Setup**
- Prepare document using approved templates with signature fields
- Configure signature requirements including signer identification and authentication level
- Set signing order and approval workflow if multiple signatures required
- Add custom fields for date, time, and location capture if applicable
- Review document completeness and accuracy before sending for signature

**Step 2: Signer Authentication and Document Delivery**
- Verify signer email address and contact information accuracy
- Select appropriate authentication method based on document sensitivity
- Send signature request with clear instructions and deadline
- Monitor signature status and send reminders if necessary
- Provide customer support for signature process questions or technical issues

**Step 3: Signature Completion and Document Finalization**
- Verify signature completion and authentication compliance
- Review completed document for accuracy and completeness
- Generate final signed document with embedded audit trail
- Distribute completed document to all parties and store in appropriate systems
- Update CRM or LMS records with signature completion status

### 6.2 High-Security Authentication Procedures
**Step 1: Enhanced Identity Verification**
- Require multi-factor authentication including SMS or phone verification
- Collect additional identification information such as driver's license number
- Implement knowledge-based authentication questions from public records
- Require government-issued ID upload and verification
- Document enhanced authentication completion in audit trail

**Step 2: Advanced Security Implementation**
- Use digital certificates with PKI for cryptographic signature validation
- Implement IP address verification and geolocation tracking
- Require biometric authentication where supported by devices
- Add timestamp and tamper-evident seal to signed documents
- Generate comprehensive audit trail with all security verification details

### 6.3 Quality Assurance and Compliance Verification
**Step 1: Signature Validity Verification**
- Verify all required signature fields completed accurately
- Confirm authentication requirements met for document type
- Review audit trail for completeness and security compliance
- Validate signature integrity and tamper-evident seal
- Check legal compliance with applicable electronic signature laws

**Step 2: Record Management and Storage**
- Store completed documents in appropriate document management system
- Maintain audit trails and authentication records for required retention period
- Update customer and project records with signature completion
- Generate compliance reports for legal and regulatory requirements
- Backup signed documents and audit trails to secure archive storage

# SOP-1010: Ladder Selection, Inspection, and Use Standards

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for the proper selection, inspection, use, and maintenance of ladders to prevent falls and injuries while ensuring OSHA compliance and optimal safety performance on all roofing projects.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel who use ladders in the course of their work, including field crews, supervisors, inspectors, and maintenance staff on residential and commercial roofing projects throughout Florida.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Safety Coordinator**: Develop ladder safety protocols and training programs
- **Site Supervisor**: Ensure proper ladder selection and daily inspections
- **Equipment Manager**: Maintain ladder inventory and inspection schedules
- **All Workers**: Follow proper use procedures and report defects immediately
- **Safety Inspector**: Conduct formal ladder safety audits and compliance reviews

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Ladder Types and Specifications:
- Extension ladders (Class I - 250 lb capacity minimum)
- Step ladders (Type IA - 300 lb capacity for heavy duty)
- Platform ladders for extended work periods
- Specialty ladders for specific roofing applications
- Ladder accessories (stabilizers, standoffs, hooks)

### Inspection Tools:
- Measuring devices for proper angle verification
- Inspection checklists and documentation forms
- Cleaning supplies and maintenance materials
- Identification tags and tracking systems
- Safety equipment for ladder users

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Maintain 4:1 ratio for ladder angle (75 degrees from horizontal)
- Ensure 3-foot extension above roof edge for access
- Never exceed manufacturer's weight capacity rating
- Prohibit ladder use during adverse weather conditions
- Require three-point contact at all times during climbing

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Ladder Selection Criteria
1. **Task Analysis**:
   - Determine required working height and reach
   - Assess duration and frequency of use
   - Evaluate weight of worker plus materials and tools
   - Consider environmental conditions and constraints
   - Identify any special requirements or restrictions

2. **Ladder Type Selection**:
   - **Extension Ladders**: For accessing roofs and elevated work areas
   - **Step Ladders**: For stable platform work below roof level
   - **Platform Ladders**: For extended duration tasks requiring tools
   - **Specialty Ladders**: For unique situations or access requirements

3. **Capacity Verification**:
   - Class I (250 lb): Light duty industrial use
   - Class IA (300 lb): Extra heavy duty industrial use
   - Class II (225 lb): Medium duty commercial use
   - Account for worker weight plus tools and materials

### Phase 2: Pre-Use Inspection Procedures
1. **Visual Inspection Components**:
   - **Rails**: Check for cracks, splits, or damage
   - **Rungs/Steps**: Verify secure attachment and condition
   - **Hardware**: Inspect bolts, rivets, and connecting devices
   - **Moving Parts**: Test locks, hinges, and extension mechanisms
   - **Safety Devices**: Verify operation of spreaders and locks

2. **Functional Testing**:
   - Operate all moving parts through full range of motion
   - Test locking mechanisms for proper engagement
   - Verify ladder stability under load conditions
   - Check for excessive wear or deformation
   - Confirm proper labeling and capacity ratings

3. **Documentation Requirements**:
   - Record inspection date and inspector identification
   - Document any defects or maintenance needs
   - Apply inspection tags or labels as required
   - Update maintenance tracking systems
   - Remove defective ladders from service immediately

### Phase 3: Proper Setup and Positioning
1. **Ground Preparation**:
   - Ensure firm, level, and stable surface
   - Remove debris, ice, or slippery materials
   - Use ladder levelers or pads on uneven surfaces
   - Verify adequate load-bearing capacity of ground
   - Position away from traffic areas and hazards

2. **Angle and Distance Calculation**:
   - Use 4:1 rule: For every 4 feet of height, position base 1 foot from wall
   - Measure from wall to ladder base, not roof edge
   - Verify ladder angle using angle indicator or geometric calculation
   - Ensure top of ladder extends 3 feet above roof edge
   - Secure ladder at top and bottom when required

3. **Electrical Safety Considerations**:
   - Maintain minimum 10-foot clearance from power lines
   - Use non-conductive fiberglass ladders near electrical hazards
   - Inspect for overhead wires before positioning
   - Coordinate with utility companies when necessary
   - Train workers on electrical hazard recognition

### Phase 4: Safe Use Techniques
1. **Climbing Procedures**:
   - Maintain three-point contact (two hands, one foot or two feet, one hand)
   - Face ladder while climbing up or down
   - Keep body centered between side rails
   - Avoid overreaching beyond belt buckle line
   - Carry tools in tool belt or haul up separately

2. **Working from Ladders**:
   - Position work within easy reach to avoid overreaching
   - Move ladder rather than leaning to reach work areas
   - Avoid sudden movements or excessive force applications
   - Use ladder accessories for stability and safety
   - Never use top two rungs of step ladders as steps

3. **Multiple Worker Considerations**:
   - Only one person on ladder at a time unless specifically designed for multiple users
   - Coordinate movement and activities to prevent conflicts
   - Establish communication protocols for team ladder use
   - Assign spotter when conditions warrant additional safety measures

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Ladder slipping**: Check surface conditions and ladder feet condition
- **Insufficient reach**: Verify proper extension and positioning
- **Instability**: Check setup angle and ground conditions
- **Hardware problems**: Inspect and maintain moving parts regularly
- **Weight capacity concerns**: Verify load calculations and ladder rating

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Stop work immediately if safety is compromised
2. Identify root cause of problem through systematic inspection
3. Implement corrective measures using approved materials and methods
4. Re-inspect ladder before returning to service
5. Document problem and resolution for trend analysis

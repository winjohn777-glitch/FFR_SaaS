# SOP-4001: Initial Customer Contact and Lead Management

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for managing initial customer contact, lead qualification, and conversion processes to ensure consistent customer experience, maximum conversion rates, and effective relationship building from first contact through project completion.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in customer contact activities, including customer service representatives, sales team members, office administrators, and field personnel interacting with customers and prospects throughout Florida.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Customer Service Manager**: Oversee customer contact procedures and team performance
- **Customer Service Representatives**: Execute initial contact and lead qualification procedures
- **Sales Team**: Convert qualified leads and manage customer relationships
- **Office Administrator**: Support contact management and documentation systems
- **Project Manager**: Maintain customer communication throughout project execution

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Communication Systems:
- Multi-line telephone system with call routing and recording capabilities
- Customer relationship management (CRM) software with lead tracking
- Email management system with templates and automation
- Mobile communication devices for field personnel
- Video conferencing capabilities for remote consultations

### Documentation Tools:
- Digital forms and lead qualification questionnaires
- Customer database with history and preference tracking
- Document management system for customer communications
- Electronic signature and contract management platforms

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Ensure customer safety during property visits and consultations
- Maintain data privacy and confidentiality of customer information
- Follow safety protocols for customer property access and assessment
- Implement emergency contact procedures for customer safety issues
- Maintain current insurance coverage for customer interaction activities

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Initial Contact Response and Qualification
1. **Immediate Response Protocols**:
   - **Phone Calls**: Answer within 3 rings with professional greeting
   - **Email Inquiries**: Send automated acknowledgment within 5 minutes
   - **Web Form Submissions**: Immediate SMS confirmation and email response
   - **Emergency Calls**: Transfer to 24/7 emergency hotline immediately
   - **Social Media Inquiries**: Respond within 2 hours during business hours

2. **Customer Information Collection**:
   - Full customer name and preferred contact information
   - Property address with accurate directions and access notes
   - Type of roofing issue, service needed, or project scope
   - Urgency level and timeline expectations
   - Insurance claim status and carrier information
   - Preferred communication methods and contact times
   - Budget range and financing considerations

3. **Lead Source Documentation**:
   - Marketing campaign or referral source identification
   - Website page or content that generated the inquiry
   - Previous customer referral or relationship information
   - Geographic area and service territory verification
   - Competitor information or previous service provider details

### Phase 2: Needs Assessment and Service Classification
1. **Service Type Determination**:
   - **Emergency Services** (0-2 hour response): Active leaks, storm damage, safety hazards
   - **Urgent Services** (24-48 hour response): Minor leaks, loose materials, post-storm inspections
   - **Standard Services** (3-7 day response): Routine maintenance, aesthetic improvements, preventive work
   - **New Construction** (project-based timeline): New installations, major renovations
   - **Commercial Projects** (specialized handling): Large-scale commercial and industrial projects

2. **Customer Qualification Criteria**:
   - Property ownership verification and decision-making authority
   - Financial capability and payment method preferences
   - Timeline expectations and flexibility for scheduling
   - Geographic location within service territory
   - Project scope alignment with company capabilities

3. **Priority Level Assignment**:
   - **Hot Leads**: Immediate need, qualified buyer, ready to proceed
   - **Warm Leads**: Near-term need, qualified, requires follow-up
   - **Cold Leads**: Future need, may require nurturing and education
   - **Referrals**: High-priority processing with expedited service
   - **Repeat Customers**: Priority handling with historical context

### Phase 3: Appointment Scheduling and Preparation
1. **Scheduling Coordination**:
   - Use integrated calendar system with real-time availability
   - Confirm customer availability and preferred time windows
   - Schedule appropriate personnel based on service type and complexity
   - Allow adequate time for thorough assessment and consultation
   - Coordinate with existing project schedules and resource availability

2. **Pre-Visit Preparation**:
   - Review customer property history and previous service records
   - Research property details through public records and mapping systems
   - Check weather forecast and plan for weather-dependent activities
   - Prepare appropriate assessment tools and documentation materials
   - Brief assigned personnel on customer expectations and special requirements

3. **Customer Communication and Confirmation**:
   - Send appointment confirmation via email and SMS
   - Provide detailed information about the visit process and timeline
   - Include contact information for assigned personnel
   - Offer preparation instructions for customer property access
   - Confirm emergency contact procedures and scheduling flexibility

### Phase 4: Follow-up and Relationship Development
1. **Immediate Follow-up Requirements**:
   - 24-hour appointment confirmation call or text message
   - Day-of reminder 2 hours before scheduled appointment
   - Post-visit follow-up within 4 hours of completion
   - Proposal delivery and explanation within 24 hours
   - Regular status updates throughout decision-making process

2. **Long-term Relationship Management**:
   - Maintain regular contact with prospects through nurturing campaigns
   - Provide educational content and seasonal maintenance reminders
   - Track customer lifecycle and identify opportunities for additional services
   - Implement referral programs and customer loyalty initiatives
   - Monitor customer satisfaction and address concerns proactively

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Response time compliance: 95% adherence to established response timeframes
- Lead qualification accuracy: 90% accurate assessment of customer needs and qualifications
- Conversion rate: 25% minimum conversion from qualified leads to signed contracts
- Customer satisfaction: 4.5/5 rating for initial contact experience
- Follow-up compliance: 100% completion of required follow-up activities

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **High call volume**: Implement call queue management and overflow procedures
- **Lead qualification challenges**: Enhance training and provide additional qualification tools
- **Scheduling conflicts**: Maintain flexible scheduling and backup personnel
- **Customer communication problems**: Verify contact information and preferred methods
- **System technical issues**: Implement backup procedures and rapid technical support

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document issue details and impact on customer experience
2. Implement immediate workarounds to maintain service quality
3. Analyze root causes and systemic issues
4. Develop permanent solutions and process improvements
5. Update training and procedures based on lessons learned

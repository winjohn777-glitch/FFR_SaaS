## 1. PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for monitoring and ensuring compliance with federal regulations affecting Florida First Roofing LLC operations, including OSHA safety standards, EPA environmental regulations, DOT transportation requirements, and federal labor laws to minimize legal risk and maintain operational legitimacy.

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to:
- All company operations and personnel
- Compliance and legal affairs staff
- Department heads and supervisors
- Safety and environmental coordinators
- External compliance consultants
- Regulatory agency interactions

## 3. DEFINITIONS

**Federal Regulatory Compliance**: Adherence to all applicable federal laws, regulations, and standards
**Regulatory Monitoring**: Systematic tracking and assessment of regulatory compliance status
**Compliance Gap**: Difference between current practices and regulatory requirements
**Regulatory Update**: Changes or modifications to existing federal regulations
**Compliance Audit**: Systematic examination of regulatory compliance effectiveness

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Compliance Manager
- Oversee federal regulatory compliance monitoring program
- Coordinate with regulatory agencies and external counsel
- Monitor regulatory changes and implementation requirements
- Report compliance status to senior management

### 4.2 Department Heads
- Ensure departmental compliance with applicable federal regulations
- Implement regulatory compliance procedures within departments
- Report compliance issues and violations promptly
- Support compliance training and awareness initiatives

### 4.3 Safety Coordinator
- Monitor OSHA compliance and safety regulations
- Coordinate safety training and certification requirements
- Investigate safety incidents and regulatory implications
- Maintain safety documentation and records

### 4.4 Environmental Coordinator
- Monitor EPA environmental regulations and requirements
- Coordinate environmental compliance and reporting
- Manage hazardous material handling and disposal
- Maintain environmental permits and documentation

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Federal Regulatory Framework Monitoring

#### 5.1.1 Key Federal Agencies and Regulations
1. **Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)**
   - Construction safety standards (29 CFR 1926)
   - Fall protection requirements
   - Respiratory protection standards
   - Hazard communication requirements
   - Personal protective equipment regulations

2. **Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)**
   - Clean Air Act compliance
   - Clean Water Act requirements
   - Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA)
   - Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA)
   - Asbestos abatement regulations

3. **Department of Transportation (DOT)**
   - Commercial vehicle safety regulations
   - Hazardous materials transportation
   - Driver qualification requirements
   - Vehicle inspection and maintenance
   - Hours of service regulations

4. **Department of Labor (DOL)**
   - Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
   - Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
   - Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act
   - Migrant and Seasonal Agricultural Worker Protection Act
   - Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA)

#### 5.1.2 Regulatory Monitoring System
1. **Regulatory Tracking Database**
   - Federal Register monitoring
   - Agency website surveillance

   - Industry association notifications
   - Legal counsel advisory updates

2. **Compliance Status Tracking**
   - Current compliance assessment

   - Corrective action planning

### 5.5 Labor and Employment Compliance

#### 5.5.1 Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) Compliance

   - Benefits continuation requirements

# SOP-8230: Miami-Dade County Roofing Operations

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for conducting roofing operations in Miami-Dade County, Florida, ensuring compliance with the most stringent building codes in the nation, international airport coordination, seaport operations, high-rise urban density requirements, and protocols for Florida First Roofing LLC's certified roofing services in Florida's largest county.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel conducting roofing operations, inspections, repairs, and installations within Miami-Dade County boundaries, including Miami, Miami Beach, Hialeah, Homestead, Coral Gables, Miami Gardens, Doral, Aventura, Kendall, and 30+ other municipalities.

## 3. Definitions
- **Miami-Dade County Building Code**: Strictest building code in the nation post-Hurricane Andrew
- **High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)**: Entire county designation requiring enhanced standards
- **Miami International Airport (MIA)**: Major international aviation hub
- **PortMiami**: Cruise capital and major cargo port
- **International Business District**: Global financial and trade center

## 4. Responsibilities
- **County Operations Manager**: Overall compliance with Miami-Dade requirements
- **International Operations Coordinator**: Airport, port, and international business coordination
- **High-Rise Construction Manager**: Urban density and skyscraper coordination
- **Hurricane Zone Specialist**: HVHZ compliance and emergency response
- **Multicultural Liaison**: Community coordination in diverse populations

## 5. Prerequisites
- Active contractor license with Miami-Dade County and applicable municipalities
- International airport security clearances
- Current liability insurance meeting urban requirements ($10M minimum)
- High-rise construction certifications and crane operator licenses
- Multilingual capabilities for diverse communities

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### County-Approved Materials:
- Miami-Dade County approved products only (NOA required)
- Hurricane impact-resistant materials for 175+ mph winds
- High-rise fire-rated materials for urban density
- Saltwater corrosion-resistant materials for coastal/marine environment
- International trade compliant materials and certifications

### Equipment Requirements:
- High-rise construction cranes and equipment
- Hurricane-rated staging and temporary protection systems
- International airport-compliant equipment (height and security)
- Urban noise-controlled equipment for 24/7 operations
- Multi-story debris management and recycling systems

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Miami-Dade Building Code Compliance
1. **Strictest Code Requirements in Nation**
   - Use only Miami-Dade County approved products (NOA system)
   - Apply 175+ mph wind speed design criteria county-wide
   - Implement large missile impact testing requirements
   - Follow enhanced structural attachment standards
   - Document complete NOA compliance for all materials

2. **Permit Application Procedures**
   - Navigate complex multi-jurisdictional permit systems
   - Submit detailed structural calculations and plans
   - Pay substantial permit fees ($150+ base + $0.20 per square foot)
   - Schedule multiple inspection phases
   - Coordinate with building official reviews

3. **Notice of Acceptance (NOA) Requirements**
   - Verify all materials have current Miami-Dade NOA
   - Maintain NOA documentation on-site
   - Follow NOA installation requirements exactly
   - Document NOA compliance throughout project
   - Coordinate with NOA testing and certification

### 7.2 High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) Requirements
1. **County-Wide HVHZ Designation**

   - Use missile impact-resistant materials for all projects

2. **Hurricane Preparedness and Response**

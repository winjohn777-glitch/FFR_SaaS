# SOP-1088: Accident Investigation and Root Cause Analysis

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for investigating accidents, incidents, and near-misses in Florida First Roofing LLC operations, conducting thorough root cause analysis, and implementing effective corrective actions to prevent recurrence while ensuring compliance with OSHA reporting requirements and Florida-specific regulations.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all incidents involving:
- Work-related injuries and illnesses
- Property damage and equipment failures
- Environmental releases and spills
- Near-miss events and close calls
- Vehicle accidents and transportation incidents
- Customer property damage
- Regulatory violations and citations
- Security and violence incidents

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Accident**: An unplanned event that results in injury, illness, property damage, or environmental harm.

**Incident**: Any unplanned event that has the potential to cause injury, illness, or damage.

**Near-Miss**: An event that could have resulted in injury, illness, or damage but did not.

**Root Cause**: The fundamental reason for an occurrence that, if corrected, would prevent recurrence.

**Contributing Factor**: An element that increases the likelihood of an incident occurring.

**Corrective Action**: Measures taken to eliminate the cause of a detected nonconformity or incident.

## 5. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

### 5.1 Severity Classification
**Fatality:**
- Death resulting from work-related incident
- OSHA reporting within 8 hours
- Comprehensive investigation required
- External agency coordination

**Serious Injury:**
- Hospitalization or major medical treatment
- OSHA reporting within 24 hours
- Detailed investigation required
- Management notification

**Minor Injury:**
- First aid treatment only
- Documentation required
- Basic investigation
- Trend analysis inclusion

**Near-Miss:**
- No injury or damage occurred
- Learning opportunity
- Quick investigation
- Preventive action focus

### 5.2 Type Classification
**Personal Injury:**
- Employee injuries and illnesses
- Contractor and visitor injuries
- Occupational health incidents
- Cumulative trauma disorders

**Property Damage:**
- Equipment damage or failure
- Facility damage
- Customer property damage
- Environmental damage

**Process-Related:**
- Quality failures
- System breakdowns
- Procedural violations
- Training deficiencies

## 6. IMMEDIATE RESPONSE PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Emergency Response
1. **Ensure Safety**
   - Provide medical assistance
   - Secure the incident scene
   - Eliminate ongoing hazards
   - Evacuate if necessary

2. **Notification Process**
   - Call 911 for emergencies
   - Notify supervisor immediately
   - Contact safety manager
   - Report to regulatory agencies

3. **Scene Preservation**
   - Secure physical evidence
   - Photograph scene conditions
   - Identify and protect witnesses
   - Maintain chain of custody

## 7. INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

### 7.1 Investigation Team Formation
**Team Composition:**
- Safety manager or designee (team leader)
- Immediate supervisor
- Employee representative
- Subject matter expert (when needed)
- External specialist (for complex incidents)

**Team Selection Criteria:**
- Knowledge of work processes
- Investigation training and experience
- Objectivity and impartiality
- Availability for timely investigation

### 7.2 Investigation Timeline
**Immediate (0-24 hours):**
- Emergency response and stabilization
- Initial scene documentation
- Witness identification and separation
- Preliminary cause assessment

**Short-term (1-7 days):**
- Detailed investigation activities
- Evidence collection and analysis
- Witness interviews
- Preliminary findings development

**Final (7-30 days):**
- Root cause analysis completion
- Corrective action development
- Report preparation and review
- Implementation planning

**Documentary Evidence:**

- Maintenance records
- Inspection reports

**Testimonial Evidence:**

- Expert opinions
- Supervisor observations

## 8. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS METHODS

### 8.1 Five Why Analysis
```
Five Why Example:
Why did the fall occur?
- Worker fell from ladder

Why did worker fall from ladder?
- Ladder slipped on wet surface

Why was ladder on wet surface?
- Rain made surface slippery

Why was work performed in rain?
- Schedule pressure to complete job

Why was there schedule pressure?
- Poor weather planning and backup procedures
```

### 8.2 Fishbone Diagram Analysis
**Categories for Analysis:**
- People (training, experience, fatigue)
- Equipment (condition, suitability, maintenance)
- Environment (weather, lighting, noise)
- Materials (quality, availability, storage)
- Methods (procedures, supervision, communication)
- Management (policies, resources, culture)

### 8.3 Fault Tree Analysis
- Top event identification
- Immediate cause identification
- Contributing factor analysis
- Systematic cause breakdown
- Probability assessment

### 8.4 Change Analysis
- What changed from normal conditions?
- When did the change occur?
- Who was involved in the change?
- Where did the change take place?
- Why was the change made?
- How was the change implemented?

# SOP-1021: Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) Requirements

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) selection, installation, testing, and maintenance to prevent electrical shock hazards and ensure electrical safety compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1926.404 standards in roofing operations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel using electrical equipment and tools on construction sites, temporary electrical installations, and any location where GFCI protection is required throughout Florida First Roofing operations.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Safety Officer**: Oversee GFCI compliance and electrical safety training
- **Electrical Supervisor**: Ensure proper GFCI installation and functionality
- **Equipment Operator**: Use GFCI-protected equipment and report issues
- **Site Supervisor**: Verify GFCI availability and compliance on job sites
- **Maintenance Personnel**: Test, maintain, and repair GFCI devices

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### GFCI Protection Devices:
- Portable GFCI outlets and adapters for temporary use
- GFCI circuit breakers for permanent electrical panel installation
- GFCI extension cords and power strips for tool protection
- GFCI testers and verification equipment
- Weatherproof GFCI devices for outdoor construction use

### Testing Equipment:
- GFCI circuit analyzers and testing devices
- Digital multimeters for electrical verification
- Receptacle testers for proper wiring verification
- Voltage detectors and electrical safety equipment
- Documentation forms and testing record sheets

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Test all GFCI devices before each use to ensure proper operation
- Never bypass or disable GFCI protection devices
- Replace damaged or non-functional GFCI devices immediately
- Use GFCI protection for all electrical tools and equipment on construction sites
- Maintain dry conditions around electrical equipment and GFCI devices
- Implement lockout/tagout procedures for electrical maintenance

- [ ] Verify GFCI devices are installed and operational

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: GFCI Requirements and Selection
1. **OSHA GFCI Requirements Identification**:
   - **Construction Sites**: All 120V receptacles in temporary installations
   - **Wet Locations**: Any electrical equipment in damp or wet conditions
   - **Outdoor Use**: All temporary electrical equipment used outdoors
   - **Tool Protection**: Power tools not covered by Assured Equipment Grounding Program
   - **Extension Cords**: All temporary extension cords on construction sites

2. **GFCI Device Selection and Specification**:
   - **Portable GFCI Outlets**: 15A and 20A ratings for temporary use
   - **GFCI Circuit Breakers**: Permanent panel installation for circuits
   - **GFCI Extension Cords**: Job site rated with weather protection
   - **GFCI Adapters**: Plug-in protection for existing non-GFCI outlets
   - **Weather Rating**: NEMA 3R or better for outdoor construction use

3. **Installation Planning and Coordination**:
   - Assess electrical load requirements and GFCI capacity
   - Plan GFCI locations for accessibility and protection
   - Coordinate with electrical systems and temporary power
   - Plan for testing access and maintenance procedures
   - Establish documentation and record-keeping procedures

### Phase 2: GFCI Installation and Setup
1. **Temporary GFCI Installation**:
   - **Portable Units**: Connect portable GFCI devices to approved circuits
   - **Extension Cord Setup**: Use only GFCI-protected extension cords
   - **Adapter Installation**: Install GFCI adapters at point of use
   - **Weather Protection**: Ensure weatherproof installation for outdoor use
   - **Accessibility**: Position for easy testing and emergency disconnection

2. **Permanent GFCI Installation**:
   - Install GFCI circuit breakers in electrical panels per NEC requirements
   - Wire GFCI devices according to manufacturer instructions
   - Verify proper line and load connections for downstream protection
   - Test installation with appropriate testing equipment
   - Label GFCI-protected circuits and outlets clearly

3. **System Integration and Verification**:
   - Verify GFCI compatibility with electrical system grounding
   - Test GFCI function with connected loads and equipment
   - Check for nuisance tripping and resolve compatibility issues
   - Document GFCI installation locations and specifications
   - Train personnel on GFCI location and testing procedures

### Phase 3: GFCI Testing and Maintenance
1. **Daily Testing Procedures**:
   - **Test Button Function**: Press TEST button to verify GFCI trips
   - **Reset Verification**: Press RESET button to restore power
   - **Outlet Testing**: Use receptacle tester to verify proper wiring
   - **Load Testing**: Test GFCI with typical connected equipment
   - **Documentation**: Record daily test results and any issues

2. **Weekly Comprehensive Testing**:
   - Use GFCI circuit analyzer for detailed function testing
   - Verify trip time meets OSHA requirements (4-6 milliamps, 25 milliseconds)
   - Test GFCI under various load conditions
   - Inspect GFCI devices for physical damage or wear
   - Document comprehensive test results and maintenance needs

3. **Monthly Maintenance and Inspection**:
   - Clean GFCI devices and check for corrosion or damage
   - Verify electrical connections and terminations
   - Test GFCI devices with calibrated testing equipment
   - Check weatherproof integrity and sealing
   - Update maintenance records and schedule repairs

### Phase 4: Troubleshooting and Problem Resolution
1. **Common GFCI Problems and Solutions**:
   - **Nuisance Tripping**: Check for moisture, damaged equipment, or wiring issues
   - **Failure to Trip**: Replace defective GFCI device immediately
   - **Failure to Reset**: Check for continued ground fault or device failure
   - **No Power**: Verify upstream power and proper installation
   - **Intermittent Operation**: Replace device and check electrical connections

2. **Emergency Procedures and Response**:
   - Immediately disconnect power if GFCI fails to provide protection
   - Implement alternative GFCI protection or cease electrical work
   - Remove defective GFCI devices from service and tag as defective
   - Coordinate emergency replacement of critical GFCI protection
   - Document incidents and implement corrective measures

3. **Repair and Replacement Procedures**:
   - Remove defective GFCI devices from service immediately
   - Replace with equivalent or better rated GFCI devices
   - Test new installations before returning to service
   - Document replacement dates and device specifications
   - Update maintenance schedules and testing procedures

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Testing frequency: Daily testing of all GFCI devices before use
- Response time: GFCI trip time 4-6 milliamps within 25 milliseconds
- Replacement time: Immediate replacement of defective GFCI devices
- Documentation completeness: 100% complete testing and maintenance records
- Personnel training: 100% training compliance for GFCI use and testing

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **GFCI won't reset**: Check for continued ground fault or replace device
- **Frequent tripping**: Inspect equipment for moisture or insulation damage
- **No GFCI protection**: Install appropriate GFCI device before equipment use
- **Testing equipment failure**: Use backup testers and coordinate calibration
- **Weather-related problems**: Implement enhanced weather protection measures

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Stop electrical work immediately when GFCI problems are identified
2. Implement alternative GFCI protection or work suspension
3. Test and verify GFCI function before resuming electrical operations
4. Document all GFCI issues and corrective actions taken
5. Update procedures to prevent similar problems in the future

# SOP-1030: PPE Assessment and Selection Procedures

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes comprehensive protocols for assessing personal protective equipment (PPE) needs, selecting appropriate equipment, and ensuring proper usage throughout Florida First Roofing LLC operations, maintaining compliance with OSHA standards and ensuring maximum worker safety and health protection.

### Scope
- PPE hazard assessment and equipment selection procedures
- Equipment specification and quality standards
- Training and competency verification for PPE usage
- Maintenance, inspection, and replacement protocols
- Documentation and compliance verification procedures
- Integration with overall safety management systems

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Workplace Hazard Assessment
```
Hazard Identification Process:
□ Physical hazard assessment (impact, penetration, crush)
□ Chemical exposure evaluation (materials, solvents, adhesives)
□ Environmental hazard analysis (heat, cold, UV, weather)
□ Electrical hazard assessment and protection requirements
□ Fall hazard evaluation and protection system requirements

Assessment Documentation:
- Detailed hazard inventory and location mapping
- Risk level assessment and protection requirement determination
- PPE specification and performance requirement development
- Training requirement identification and competency standards
- Monitoring and reassessment schedule establishment
```

### 5.2 PPE Selection Criteria
```
Head Protection:
- ANSI/ISEA Z89.1 Type I or Type II classification
- Class E electrical protection for electrical work
- Impact and penetration resistance appropriate for hazards
- Comfort and fit for extended wear periods
- Weather resistance for Florida climate conditions

Eye and Face Protection:
- ANSI/ISEA Z87.1 impact and optical clarity standards
- Side protection for debris and projectile hazards
- UV protection for outdoor work and sun exposure
- Anti-fog and scratch-resistant coatings
- Prescription lens compatibility and options

Fall Protection:
- ANSI/ISEA Z359 personal fall protection systems
- Full-body harness with appropriate D-ring configurations
- Self-retracting lifeline (SRL) or personal fall limiter (PFL)
- Energy absorber and shock-absorbing components
- Compatibility with roof work and positioning requirements
```

### 5.3 Equipment Inspection and Maintenance
```
Daily Inspection Requirements:
□ Visual inspection for damage, wear, or deterioration
□ Functional testing of moving parts and connections
□ Cleanliness and hygiene verification
□ Proper fit and adjustment confirmation
□ Documentation of inspection results and findings

Maintenance Procedures:
- Cleaning and sanitization according to manufacturer instructions
- Storage in appropriate conditions to prevent damage
- Repair or replacement decision based on damage assessment
- Maintenance record keeping and lifecycle tracking
- Quality control and performance verification
```

### 5.4 Training and Competency Verification
```
Training Program Elements:
- Hazard recognition and PPE requirement understanding
- Proper selection and equipment specification knowledge
- Correct usage, adjustment, and maintenance procedures
- Inspection and problem identification techniques
- Emergency procedures and equipment failure response

Competency Assessment:
- Written examination covering PPE knowledge and requirements
- Practical demonstration of proper usage and inspection
- Problem identification and resolution testing
- Emergency response and procedure implementation
- Ongoing performance monitoring and evaluation
```

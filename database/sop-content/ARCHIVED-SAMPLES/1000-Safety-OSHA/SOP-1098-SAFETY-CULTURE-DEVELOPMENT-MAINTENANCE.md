# SOP-1098: Safety Culture Development and Maintenance

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for developing, implementing, and maintaining a positive safety culture within Florida First Roofing LLC operations, ensuring that safety becomes an integral part of organizational values, behaviors, and practices while addressing Florida's unique operational challenges and cultural diversity.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all safety culture development activities including:
- Organizational safety culture assessment
- Leadership safety behavior modeling
- Employee engagement and participation programs
- Safety communication and awareness initiatives
- Recognition and incentive programs
- Training and competency development
- Cultural integration for diverse workforce
- Continuous improvement and sustainability

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Safety Culture**: The shared values, beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors regarding safety that exist within an organization.

**Safety Climate**: The perceived state of safety in the workplace at a specific time, as measured through employee surveys and assessments.

**Psychological Safety**: The belief that one can speak up about safety concerns without fear of negative consequences.

**Safety Leadership**: The process of influencing others to work safely and create a safe work environment.

**Behavioral Safety**: A process that focuses on what people do, analyzes why they do it, and applies research-supported intervention strategies.

**Cultural Transformation**: The systematic process of changing organizational culture to achieve desired safety outcomes.

### 5.3 Culture Maturity Levels
**Level 1 - Reactive:**
- Safety managed by compliance
- Focus on accidents after occurrence
- Blame-oriented approach
- Limited employee engagement

**Level 2 - Dependent:**
- Safety managed by procedures
- Supervisor-driven safety
- Rule-based compliance
- Basic employee participation

**Level 3 - Independent:**
- Personal safety responsibility
- Self-initiated safety behaviors
- Proactive hazard identification
- Improved safety performance

**Level 4 - Interdependent:**
- Team-based safety commitment
- Collective responsibility
- Continuous improvement focus
- Sustainable safety excellence

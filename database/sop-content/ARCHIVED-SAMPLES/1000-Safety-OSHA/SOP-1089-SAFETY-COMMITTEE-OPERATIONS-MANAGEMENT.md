# SOP-1089: Safety Committee Operations and Management

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for the formation, operation, and management of safety committees within Florida First Roofing LLC, ensuring effective worker participation in safety programs, regulatory compliance, and continuous improvement of workplace safety and health conditions in accordance with OSHA guidelines and Florida-specific requirements.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all safety committee activities including:
- Corporate safety committee operations
- Job site safety committees
- Joint labor-management safety committees
- Specialized safety committees (crane, confined space, etc.)
- Safety committee member training and development
- Meeting procedures and documentation
- Follow-up and corrective action implementation
- Integration with overall safety management system

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Safety Committee**: A group of employees and management representatives working together to identify, evaluate, and recommend solutions for workplace safety and health issues.

**Joint Committee**: A safety committee with equal representation from management and employees.

**Committee Charter**: A document defining the committee's purpose, authority, structure, and operating procedures.

**Quorum**: The minimum number of committee members required to conduct official business.

**Action Item**: A specific task or recommendation requiring follow-up and completion tracking.

**Safety Inspection**: Systematic examination of workplace conditions conducted by committee members.

## 3. REGULATORY REFERENCES

- OSHA 29 CFR 1926.95 - Employee participation requirements

## 4. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### Executive Management
- Provide safety committee authority and resources
- Approve committee recommendations
- Ensure management participation
- Support committee activities and decisions

### Safety Manager
- Serve as committee advisor and facilitator
- Provide technical expertise and guidance
- Coordinate with regulatory agencies
- Monitor committee effectiveness

### Committee Chairperson
- Lead committee meetings and activities
- Coordinate member participation
- Ensure meeting objectives are met
- Report to management and employees

### Committee Members
- Actively participate in meetings and activities
- Represent constituent groups
- Conduct safety inspections
- Follow up on assigned action items

### Committee Secretary
- Prepare meeting agendas
- Record meeting minutes
- Distribute communications
- Maintain committee records

## 5. COMMITTEE STRUCTURE AND COMPOSITION

### 5.1 Corporate Safety Committee
**Composition:**
- Safety Manager (Chairperson)
- Operations Manager
- Human Resources Representative
- Employee Representatives (3-5 members)
- Union Representative (if applicable)

**Membership Criteria:**
- Management and employee representation
- Various departments and job classifications
- Different experience levels and perspectives
- Voluntary participation encouraged

### 5.2 Job Site Safety Committees
**Composition:**
- Site Supervisor (Co-chair)
- Lead Worker (Co-chair)
- Employee Representatives (2-3 members)
- Subcontractor Representative (when applicable)

**Activation Criteria:**
- Projects lasting more than 6 months
- Projects with 20 or more workers
- High-hazard operations
- Customer requirements

### 5.3 Specialized Committees
**Crane Safety Committee:**
- Certified crane operators
- Qualified riggers
- Maintenance personnel
- Safety representatives

**Confined Space Committee:**
- Entry supervisors
- Attendants and entrants
- Equipment specialists
- Emergency response personnel

### 5.4 Florida-Specific Considerations
- Hurricane preparedness committee
- Heat stress prevention committee
- Lightning safety committee
- Environmental compliance committee

# SOP-1086: Workplace Violence Prevention

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for preventing workplace violence, ensuring employee safety, and maintaining a secure work environment for all Florida First Roofing LLC operations, addressing both traditional workplace violence and unique risks associated with residential and commercial roofing work environments.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC personnel and work environments including:
- Office and administrative facilities
- Job sites and customer properties
- Vehicle operations and transportation
- Remote work locations
- Client interactions and service calls
- Vendor and contractor relationships
- Emergency response situations
- After-hours and weekend operations

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Workplace Violence**: Any act or threat of physical violence, harassment, intimidation, or other threatening disruptive behavior in the workplace.

**Threat Assessment**: A systematic process to evaluate the credibility and seriousness of a threat.

**Behavioral Indicators**: Observable actions or statements that may suggest potential for violence.

**De-escalation**: Techniques used to reduce tension and prevent escalation of conflicts.

**Safe Room**: A designated secure location for personnel to retreat during violent incidents.

**Zero Tolerance Policy**: Immediate disciplinary action for any act of workplace violence.

## 5. RISK ASSESSMENT AND IDENTIFICATION

### 5.1 Workplace Risk Factors
```
High-Risk Situations:
1. Customer disputes over billing or work quality
2. Termination or disciplinary actions
3. Domestic violence spilling into workplace
4. Working alone at remote locations
5. Handling cash or valuable materials
6. Late-night or early-morning operations
```

### 5.2 Individual Risk Factors
**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Verbal threats or intimidation
- Aggressive or hostile behavior
- Substance abuse problems
- Depression or mood changes
- Obsession with weapons or violence
- History of violence or criminal behavior

**Personal Stressors:**
- Financial difficulties
- Relationship problems
- Legal troubles
- Health issues
- Work performance problems

### 5.3 Environmental Risk Factors
**Physical Environment:**
- Isolated work locations
- Poor lighting or visibility
- Multiple exit accessibility
- Communication availability
- Proximity to high-crime areas

**Organizational Factors:**
- High-stress work environment
- Poor management practices
- Inadequate staffing levels
- Lack of employee support systems

### 5.4 Florida-Specific Considerations
- Hurricane stress and displacement
- Heat-related stress factors
- Transient population interactions
- Tourism-related security issues
- Drug trafficking corridor concerns

## 7. THREAT ASSESSMENT PROCEDURES

### 7.1 Initial Assessment
```
Assessment Process:
1. Gather all available information
2. Interview witnesses and involved parties
3. Review personnel records and history
4. Consult with mental health professionals
5. Coordinate with law enforcement if needed
6. Document all findings and recommendations
```

### 7.2 Risk Classification
**Low Risk:**
- Vague or indirect threats
- No history of violence
- Adequate coping mechanisms
- Support systems available

**Medium Risk:**
- More specific threats
- Some concerning behaviors
- Limited coping skills
- Escalating stressors

**High Risk:**
- Direct and specific threats
- History of violence
- Access to weapons
- Detailed planning evident

### 7.3 Response Actions
**Low Risk Response:**
- Increased monitoring
- Employee assistance referral
- Supervisor notification
- Documentation of incidents

**Medium Risk Response:**
- Law enforcement consultation
- Security measures enhancement
- Possible work restrictions
- Professional intervention

**High Risk Response:**
- Immediate law enforcement involvement
- Employee removal from workplace
- Enhanced security measures
- Legal protective orders

## 8. INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURES

### 8.1 Immediate Response
1. **Ensure Safety**
   - Remove from danger immediately
   - Call 911 if violence occurring
   - Seek medical attention for injuries
   - Secure the incident area

2. **Notification Process**
   - Alert management immediately
   - Contact law enforcement
   - Notify employee assistance program
   - Inform insurance carriers

3. **Documentation**
   - Record all incident details
   - Collect witness statements
   - Preserve physical evidence
   - Photograph injuries or damage

### 8.2 Emergency Procedures
**Active Violence Situation:**
- Run: Evacuate if possible
- Hide: Find secure location if escape not possible
- Fight: As last resort, incapacitate the threat

**Bomb Threat Response:**
- Do not hang up on caller
- Gather as much information as possible
- Evacuate building if credible threat
- Contact law enforcement immediately

### 8.3 Post-Incident Procedures
- Provide crisis counseling services
- Conduct thorough investigation
- Implement corrective measures
- Monitor affected employees
- Review and update policies

## 9. DE-ESCALATION TECHNIQUES

### 9.1 Verbal De-escalation
**Communication Strategies:**
- Remain calm and speak slowly
- Use non-threatening body language
- Listen actively and empathetically
- Avoid arguing or being defensive
- Acknowledge the person's feelings

**Phrases to Use:**
- "I can see you're upset"
- "Help me understand the problem"
- "Let's work together to solve this"
- "I want to help you"

**Phrases to Avoid:**
- "Calm down"
- "That's not my problem"
- "You need to leave"
- "I don't care"

### 9.2 Physical De-escalation
- Maintain safe distance (1.5 times arm's length)
- Keep hands visible and open
- Stand at an angle rather than face-to-face
- Have an escape route planned
- Remove or secure potential weapons

### 9.3 Environmental Modifications
- Move to a more private location
- Remove audience when possible
- Eliminate distractions
- Ensure adequate personal space
- Provide comfortable seating

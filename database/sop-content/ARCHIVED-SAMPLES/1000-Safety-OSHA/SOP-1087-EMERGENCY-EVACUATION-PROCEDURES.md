# SOP-1087: Emergency Evacuation Procedures

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive emergency evacuation procedures for all Florida First Roofing LLC facilities and job sites, ensuring the safe and orderly evacuation of personnel during emergencies while addressing Florida's unique hazards including hurricanes, severe weather, flooding, and other natural disasters.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC locations including:
- Corporate offices and administrative buildings
- Warehouse and storage facilities
- Equipment yards and maintenance shops
- Job sites and construction areas
- Remote work locations
- Temporary facilities and trailers
- Vehicle operations during emergencies
- Customer facilities during service calls

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Emergency Evacuation**: The immediate and rapid movement of people away from a threat or actual occurrence of a hazard.

**Assembly Point**: A designated safe location where evacuees gather after leaving a building or area.

**Evacuation Warden**: A trained employee responsible for ensuring complete evacuation of assigned areas.

**Shelter-in-Place**: Protective action involving taking refuge in a small interior room with no or few windows and sealing it.

**Emergency Action Plan**: A written document required by OSHA describing actions employees should take during emergencies.

**Accountability System**: A method to verify that all personnel have been evacuated safely.

## 5. EMERGENCY TYPES AND PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Fire Emergencies
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Activate fire alarm system
2. Call 911 and report fire location
3. Begin immediate evacuation
4. Close doors behind you
5. Use stairs, never elevators
6. Report to assembly points

**Evacuation Route Selection:**
- Use nearest safe exit
- Avoid smoke-filled areas
- Feel doors before opening
- Stay low if smoke present

### 5.2 Severe Weather (Hurricanes, Tornadoes)
**Hurricane Procedures:**
- Monitor weather forecasts continuously
- Implement 72-hour preparation phase
- Secure all equipment and materials
- Evacuate to inland safe areas
- Follow mandatory evacuation orders

**Tornado Procedures:**
- Move to interior rooms on lowest floors
- Avoid windows and glass
- Take protective positions
- Wait for all-clear signals

### 5.3 Flooding Emergencies
**Flash Flood Response:**
- Move to higher ground immediately
- Avoid walking or driving through water
- Monitor emergency broadcasts
- Evacuate low-lying areas promptly

**Storm Surge Evacuation:**
- Follow coastal evacuation routes
- Leave early before roads flood
- Move inland and to higher elevation
- Do not return until authorities approve

### 5.4 Hazardous Material Incidents
**Chemical Spill/Release:**
- Evacuate upwind and uphill
- Maintain minimum 1000-foot distance
- Avoid low-lying areas
- Wait for hazmat team clearance

**Gas Leak Procedures:**
- Evacuate immediately
- Do not use electrical devices
- Ventilate area if safe to do so
- Wait for utility company clearance

### 5.5 Florida-Specific Emergencies
**Lightning Safety:**
- Seek shelter in substantial buildings
- Avoid open areas and tall objects
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder
- Use 30-30 rule for safety

**Heat Emergency:**
- Move to air-conditioned areas
- Provide cooling measures
- Monitor for heat-related illness
- Transport to medical facilities

## 6. EVACUATION PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Alarm Systems
**Fire Alarm Signals:**
- Continuous horn/bell - General evacuation
- Intermittent tone - Shelter in place
- Voice announcement - Specific instructions
- Strobe lights - Visual notification

**Communication Methods:**
- Public address systems
- Two-way radios
- Cell phone notifications
- Emergency notification systems

### 6.2 Evacuation Routes
```
Route Planning Requirements:
1. Multiple exit routes from each area
2. Clearly marked exit signs
3. Emergency lighting systems
4. Route maps posted prominently
5. Regular route inspections
6. Alternative route planning
```

**Primary Routes:**
- Main exits and stairwells
- Direct routes to assembly points
- Well-lit and marked pathways
- Regular maintenance and testing

**Secondary Routes:**
- Alternative exits for blocked routes
- Emergency stairs and exits
- Roof access routes (if applicable)
- Ground-level exits from basements

### 6.3 Assembly Points
**Selection Criteria:**
- Safe distance from buildings
- Away from emergency vehicle routes
- Protection from weather when possible
- Adequate space for all personnel
- Good communication capabilities

**Assembly Point Management:**
- Designated assembly point coordinators
- Accountability check procedures
- Communication with emergency coordinator
- Relocation procedures if necessary

## 10. SHELTER-IN-PLACE PROCEDURES

### 10.1 When to Shelter
- Hazardous material releases
- Chemical emergencies
- Severe weather warnings
- Security threats

### 10.2 Shelter Procedures
1. **Room Selection**
   - Interior rooms with few windows
   - Upper floors for flooding
   - Lower floors for wind events
   - Away from hazmat release

2. **Room Sealing**
   - Close and lock doors and windows
   - Seal gaps with plastic and tape
   - Turn off ventilation systems
   - Seal vents and openings

3. **Supplies and Equipment**
   - Battery-powered radio
   - First aid supplies
   - Water and snacks
   - Flashlights and batteries

### 10.3 Duration Management
- Monitor emergency broadcasts
- Maintain communication logs
- Ration supplies appropriately
- Plan for extended duration

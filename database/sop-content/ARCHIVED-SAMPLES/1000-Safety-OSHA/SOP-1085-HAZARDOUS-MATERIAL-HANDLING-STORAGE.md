# SOP-1085: Hazardous Material Handling and Storage

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for safe handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous materials used in roofing operations, ensuring compliance with OSHA Hazard Communication Standard 29 CFR 1910.1200 and protecting workers, the environment, and the public from chemical hazards specific to Florida's climate and regulatory environment.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC operations involving hazardous materials including:
- Roofing adhesives and solvents
- Primers and sealants
- Asbestos-containing materials (ACM)
- Lead-based paint materials
- Protective coatings and paints
- Cleaning chemicals and degreasers
- Fuel and petroleum products
- Refrigerants and propellants

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Hazardous Material**: Any substance that poses a threat to human health, safety, or the environment.

**Safety Data Sheet (SDS)**: A document providing information about chemical hazards, safe handling procedures, and emergency measures.

**Hazard Communication**: The process of informing workers about chemical hazards through labels, SDSs, and training.

**Exposure Limit**: The maximum concentration of a chemical to which workers may be exposed.

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**: Equipment worn to minimize exposure to hazards.

**Incompatible Materials**: Chemicals that may react dangerously when stored or mixed together.

## 7. HANDLING PROCEDURES

### 7.1 General Handling Guidelines
1. **Pre-Work Preparation**
   - Review SDS information
   - Select appropriate PPE
   - Verify ventilation adequacy
   - Prepare emergency equipment

2. **Safe Handling Practices**
   - Use proper lifting techniques
   - Avoid skin contact
   - Prevent inhalation exposure
   - Minimize container opening time

3. **Transfer Procedures**
   - Use appropriate transfer equipment
   - Ground and bond containers
   - Control static electricity
   - Monitor for leaks and spills

### 7.2 Specific Material Handling

## 10. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### 10.1 Spill Response
1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Ensure personnel safety
   - Contain spill if safe to do so
   - Evacuate affected area
   - Notify emergency contacts

2. **Cleanup Procedures**
   - Use appropriate cleanup materials
   - Follow SDS recommendations
   - Dispose of waste properly
   - Decontaminate affected areas

### 10.2 Exposure Response
**Inhalation Exposure:**
- Move to fresh air immediately
- Monitor vital signs
- Seek medical attention
- Document exposure details

**Skin Contact:**
- Remove contaminated clothing
- Flush with water for 15 minutes
- Seek medical attention
- Save contaminated materials

**Eye Contact:**
- Flush with water for 15 minutes
- Do not rub eyes
- Seek immediate medical attention
- Continue flushing en route

### 10.3 Fire Response
- Evacuate area immediately
- Call fire department
- Use appropriate extinguishing agents
- Monitor for toxic fumes

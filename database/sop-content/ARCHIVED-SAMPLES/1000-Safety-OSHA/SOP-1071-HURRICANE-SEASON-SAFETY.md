# SOP-1071: Hurricane Season Safety Protocols

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive safety protocols for Florida First Roofing LLC operations during hurricane season (June 1 - November 30). This procedure ensures employee safety, equipment protection, and business continuity during severe weather events while maintaining emergency response capabilities.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC operations during:
- Hurricane season preparation and monitoring
- Tropical storm and hurricane warnings
- Post-storm emergency response operations
- Equipment and material securing procedures
- Employee evacuation and safety protocols
- Business continuity and recovery operations

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Hurricane Season**: June 1 through November 30 annually in the Atlantic Basin
- **Tropical Storm**: Sustained winds of 39-73 mph
- **Hurricane**: Sustained winds of 74 mph or greater
- **Storm Surge**: Rise in sea level caused by hurricane winds
- **Hurricane Watch**: Hurricane conditions possible within 48 hours
- **Hurricane Warning**: Hurricane conditions expected within 36 hours
- **Saffir-Simpson Scale**: Hurricane intensity scale from Category 1-5

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Hurricane Season Preparation

#### 5.1.1 Pre-Season Planning (May 1-31)
1. **Emergency Plan Review**
   - Update hurricane response procedures
   - Review employee contact information
   - Verify emergency supply inventories
   - Test communication systems and equipment

2. **Training and Drills**
   - Conduct hurricane preparedness training for all employees
   - Practice evacuation procedures
   - Review equipment securing techniques
   - Test emergency communication protocols

3. **Supply and Equipment Preparation**
   - Stock emergency supplies for minimum 72-hour operations
   - Inspect and test generators and backup power systems
   - Verify fuel storage levels and procurement procedures
   - Prepare equipment securing materials and tie-downs

4. **Facility Preparation**
   - Inspect building integrity and storm shutters
   - Clear drainage systems and secure outdoor equipment
   - Backup computer systems and important documents
   - Install temporary storm protection as needed

#### 5.1.2 Ongoing Monitoring (June 1 - November 30)
1. **Daily Weather Monitoring**
   - Check National Hurricane Center forecasts twice daily
   - Monitor tropical development in Atlantic Basin
   - Review local weather service warnings and watches
   - Communicate weather updates to all personnel

2. **72-Hour Preparation Trigger**
   - Activate when tropical system may impact Florida within 72 hours
   - Begin equipment and material securing procedures
   - Alert all employees of potential storm impacts
   - Review and update emergency contact lists

### 5.2 Storm Approach Procedures

#### 5.2.1 48-Hour Warning Protocols
1. **Work Site Securing**
   - Remove or secure all loose materials from rooftops
   - Install temporary tie-downs on work in progress
   - Drain and secure water containers and equipment
   - Document current project status with photographs

2. **Equipment Protection**
   - Move equipment to secure storage or higher ground
   - Fuel all vehicles and equipment
   - Charge all electronic devices and communication equipment
   - Secure ladders, scaffolding, and temporary structures

3. **Employee Notification**
   - Issue formal storm warning notification to all employees
   - Provide evacuation zone information and routes
   - Confirm employee contact information and locations
   - Establish check-in procedures during and after storm

#### 5.2.2 24-Hour Warning Protocols
1. **Final Site Preparation**
   - Complete all equipment securing and material protection
   - Install storm shutters and building protection
   - Shut off utilities as appropriate
   - Post emergency contact information at all locations

2. **Employee Safety Measures**
   - Release all employees to prepare personal properties
   - Provide evacuation assistance to employees in flood zones
   - Establish emergency shelter locations for stranded employees
   - Confirm emergency contact and check-in procedures

### 5.3 During Storm Operations

#### 5.3.1 Shelter-in-Place Procedures
1. **Safety Protocols**
   - Remain in safe location until all-clear is given
   - Monitor weather conditions and emergency broadcasts
   - Maintain communication with emergency coordinator
   - Avoid windows and stay away from storm-exposed areas

2. **Communication Procedures**
   - Check in with emergency coordinator every 4 hours
   - Report any emergency situations immediately
   - Monitor company communication channels
   - Assist with emergency response as conditions allow

#### 5.3.2 Emergency Response Capabilities
1. **Limited Emergency Services**
   - Provide emergency tarping for critical customer needs
   - Assist with life-safety emergency situations
   - Coordinate with local emergency management agencies
   - Document emergency response activities

2. **Safety Restrictions**
   - No rooftop work during storm conditions
   - No work with sustained winds above 25 mph
   - No electrical work during storm conditions
   - All work must have safety supervisor present

### 5.4 Post-Storm Response

#### 5.4.1 Immediate Assessment (0-24 hours)
1. **Employee Accountability**
   - Conduct employee welfare check and roll call
   - Provide assistance to employees with storm damage
   - Document any employee injuries or emergencies
   - Establish temporary employee housing if needed

2. **Facility Assessment**
   - Inspect all company facilities for damage
   - Check utilities and communication systems
   - Assess equipment and vehicle condition
   - Document damage with photographs for insurance

3. **Safety Assessment**
   - Identify immediate safety hazards
   - Clear debris from access routes
   - Check structural integrity of buildings
   - Establish safe work areas for operations

#### 5.4.2 Recovery Operations (24-72 hours)
1. **Emergency Response Services**
   - Prioritize life-safety emergency calls
   - Provide emergency tarping and water intrusion prevention
   - Assist with structural stabilization as needed
   - Coordinate with insurance adjusters and emergency services

2. **Resource Management**
   - Assess material and supply needs
   - Coordinate with suppliers for emergency deliveries
   - Manage fuel and generator operations
   - Establish temporary work areas and staging locations

3. **Customer Communication**
   - Contact customers to assess their needs and damage
   - Provide realistic timelines for emergency services
   - Coordinate with insurance companies and adjusters
   - Document customer requests and service priorities

#### 5.4.3 Full Recovery Operations (72+ hours)
1. **Normal Operations Restoration**
   - Gradually increase workforce and service capacity
   - Resume normal project schedules as conditions allow
   - Complete damage assessments and insurance claims
   - Implement lessons learned from storm response

2. **Quality Control**
   - Inspect all emergency repairs for quality and safety
   - Schedule permanent repairs and improvements
   - Document response effectiveness and areas for improvement
   - Update procedures based on storm experience

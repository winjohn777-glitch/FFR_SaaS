## BLOCK 1: PURPOSE - Strategic Intent and Business Objectives

To establish comprehensive first aid and medical emergency response procedures ensuring immediate, effective medical care for injured or ill personnel at Florida First Roofing LLC work sites. This SOP provides framework for emergency medical response, injury treatment, and coordination with emergency medical services while maintaining compliance with OSHA first aid requirements and industry safety standards.

**Strategic Business Objectives:**
- Provide immediate, life-saving medical care for workplace injuries and medical emergencies
- Ensure rapid coordination with emergency medical services for serious injuries
- Minimize injury severity through prompt, appropriate first aid intervention
- Maintain compliance with OSHA first aid and medical service requirements
- Reduce workers' compensation costs through effective emergency medical response
- Support injured worker recovery and successful return to work

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**First Aid:** Immediate medical care provided to an injured or ill person before professional medical help arrives, designed to preserve life, prevent worsening of condition, and promote recovery.

**Medical Emergency:** A serious injury or sudden illness requiring immediate medical attention to prevent death or serious disability.

**Automated External Defibrillator (AED):** A portable device that automatically diagnoses life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias and can treat them through electrical defibrillation.

**Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR):** An emergency procedure combining chest compressions and rescue breathing to maintain blood circulation and oxygenation during cardiac arrest.

**Emergency Medical Services (EMS):** Professional emergency medical care providers including paramedics, emergency medical technicians, and hospital emergency departments.

**Trauma:** Physical injury caused by external force or violence, often requiring immediate medical intervention to prevent death or disability.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Medical Emergency Response Activation

**Immediate Response to Medical Emergency (First 60 Seconds):**
1. Ensure scene safety and prevent additional injuries to responders and others
2. Assess injured person's consciousness and breathing status immediately
3. Call 911 immediately for any serious injury or life-threatening condition
4. Begin appropriate first aid treatment based on injury assessment
5. Assign personnel to guide emergency medical services to exact location

**Emergency Assessment and Prioritization:**
1. Check for responsiveness using verbal and physical stimulation
2. Assess airway, breathing, and circulation (ABCs) for life-threatening conditions
3. Control severe bleeding using direct pressure and elevation
4. Treat for shock by elevating legs and maintaining body temperature
5. Monitor vital signs and consciousness level continuously until EMS arrival

**Communication and Coordination:**
1. Notify company medical response coordinator and management immediately
2. Provide clear, accurate information about injury type and severity
3. Coordinate work site access for emergency medical service vehicles
4. Clear unnecessary personnel from emergency scene while maintaining assistance
5. Document all emergency response actions and timeline of events

### 5.2 Specific Injury Treatment Protocols

**Fall Injury Response:**
1. Do not move injured person unless in immediate danger from additional hazards
2. Assume spinal injury and maintain head and neck immobilization
3. Check for consciousness, breathing, and circulation without moving victim
4. Treat visible injuries including bleeding control and shock prevention
5. Keep victim warm and conscious while awaiting emergency medical service arrival

**Heat Illness Treatment:**
1. Move victim to cool, shaded area or air-conditioned space immediately
2. Remove excess clothing and begin active cooling with water and fans
3. Monitor consciousness level and provide water if victim is conscious and alert
4. Call 911 for heat stroke symptoms including altered mental status
5. Continue cooling measures and monitor vital signs until medical help arrives

**Cardiac Emergency Response:**
1. Check for responsiveness and normal breathing immediately
2. Call 911 and request AED if victim is unresponsive with no normal breathing
3. Begin CPR with chest compressions if no pulse detected
4. Use AED according to device instructions as soon as available
5. Continue CPR and AED use until emergency medical services take over

### 5.3 Post-Emergency Procedures and Follow-up

**Immediate Post-Emergency Actions:**
1. Ensure injured worker receives appropriate medical evaluation and treatment
2. Complete detailed incident report including witness statements and photographs
3. Notify workers' compensation insurance carrier within required timeframes
4. Coordinate with family members and provide support during medical treatment
5. Conduct immediate investigation to identify contributing factors and prevention measures

**Return to Work Coordination:**
1. Obtain medical clearance from treating physician before allowing return to work
2. Coordinate light duty or modified work assignments based on medical restrictions
3. Monitor injured worker's recovery and provide necessary support and accommodations
4. Update safety procedures and training based on incident investigation findings
5. Conduct incident review with all personnel to share lessons learned and prevention strategies

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Responder Safety During Medical Emergencies
**Scene Safety Assessment:**
- Ensure emergency responders are not exposed to same hazards that caused injury
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment during medical response
- Implement universal precautions for all blood and body fluid exposures
- Maintain safe distances from electrical hazards and unstable structures
- Coordinate with emergency services for hazardous scene management

**Infection Control and Disease Prevention:**
- Use disposable gloves and face masks when providing first aid care
- Proper disposal of contaminated medical supplies and equipment
- Hand washing and sanitization following all medical emergency responses
- Hepatitis B vaccination for personnel with regular first aid responsibilities
- Documentation and medical follow-up for any occupational disease exposures

### 8.2 Psychological and Emotional Support
**Trauma Response and Support:**
- Recognize psychological impact of serious injuries on witnesses and coworkers
- Provide immediate emotional support and debriefing following traumatic incidents
- Coordinate with employee assistance programs for counseling and support services
- Monitor personnel for signs of post-traumatic stress and delayed emotional reactions
- Implement return-to-work support for personnel affected by workplace traumatic incidents

**Family and Communication Support:**
- Sensitive notification of family members regarding workplace injuries
- Coordination with hospitals and medical providers for family communication
- Transportation assistance for family members during medical emergencies
- Regular updates and communication during extended medical treatment
- Support for funeral arrangements and family assistance for fatality incidents

## BLOCK 14: APPENDICES - Supporting Information and Cross-References

### 14.1 Emergency Medical Response Quick Reference Guide
**Primary Assessment (ABCDE):**
- **Airway:** Check for clear airway and remove obstructions
- **Breathing:** Assess breathing rate, depth, and effectiveness
- **Circulation:** Check pulse and control severe bleeding
- **Disability:** Assess spinal injury and neurological function
- **Exposure:** Examine for additional injuries while maintaining warmth

**CPR and AED Quick Steps:**
1. Check responsiveness and breathing
2. Call 911 and request AED
3. Begin chest compressions (30 compressions, 2 breaths)
4. Use AED when available and follow voice prompts
5. Continue CPR until EMS arrives

### 14.2 Common Roofing Injury Treatment Guidelines
**Fall Injury Protocol:**
- Assume spinal injury and immobilize head and neck
- Do not move unless immediate danger present
- Treat for shock and monitor consciousness
- Call 911 for any fall from height greater than 6 feet

**Heat Illness Treatment:**
- Move to cool environment immediately
- Remove excess clothing and begin active cooling
- Provide water if conscious and alert
- Call 911 for altered mental status or high body temperature

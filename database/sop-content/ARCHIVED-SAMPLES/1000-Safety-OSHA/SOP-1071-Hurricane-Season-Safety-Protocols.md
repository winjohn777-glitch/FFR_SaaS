## BLOCK 1: PURPOSE - Strategic Intent and Business Objectives

To establish comprehensive hurricane season safety protocols ensuring the protection of all Florida First Roofing LLC personnel, equipment, and facilities during tropical weather events while maintaining emergency response capabilities for customers. This SOP provides framework for pre-storm preparation, storm response, and post-storm recovery operations specific to Florida's hurricane-prone environment.

**Strategic Business Objectives:**
- Ensure complete safety and protection of all employees and their families during hurricane events
- Protect company equipment, vehicles, and facilities from storm damage
- Maintain emergency response capability to assist customers with urgent roofing needs
- Ensure business continuity and rapid resumption of operations following storms
- Comply with all emergency management and safety regulations during hurricane season
- Support community recovery efforts and emergency response coordination

## BLOCK 2: SCOPE - Applicability and Coverage

This SOP applies to all hurricane preparedness and response activities within Florida First Roofing LLC during the Atlantic Hurricane Season (June 1 - November 30):

### 2.1 Geographic Coverage and Risk Assessment
**Hurricane Risk Zones:**
- Coastal areas with direct hurricane landfall and storm surge threats
- Inland areas subject to high winds, tornadoes, and flooding from hurricane systems

### 2.2 Personnel and Operational Coverage
**All Company Personnel:**
- Field crews and roofing installation teams

**Business Operations:**

- Customer emergency services and post-storm roofing repairs
- Communication systems and emergency contact procedures

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Hurricane Season:** The period from June 1 through November 30 when tropical cyclone activity is most likely in the Atlantic Basin, requiring enhanced weather monitoring and emergency preparedness.

**Storm Track:** The projected path of a tropical storm or hurricane as forecasted by the National Hurricane Center, used for evacuation and preparation decision-making.

**Cone of Uncertainty:** The forecast track area showing the probable path of the storm center, with increasing uncertainty at longer forecast periods.

**Storm Surge:** The abnormal rise of water generated by a storm's winds, often the most dangerous aspect of hurricanes for coastal areas.

**Evacuation Zone:** Geographic areas designated by emergency management officials for mandatory or voluntary evacuation based on storm surge and wind risk.

**Emergency Operations Center (EOC):** The designated facility for coordinating company emergency response and maintaining communication during hurricane events.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Pre-Season Preparation and Planning

**Annual Hurricane Preparedness Review (May 1-31):**
1. Review and update all hurricane emergency procedures and contact information
2. Inspect and test emergency equipment including generators, radios, and safety supplies
3. Coordinate with insurance providers to verify coverage and claim procedures
4. Update evacuation plans and routes for all personnel and their families
5. Conduct hurricane preparedness training and tabletop exercises with all staff

**Facility and Equipment Preparation:**
1. Identify and prepare secure storage locations for equipment and vehicles
2. Install hurricane shutters or plywood protection for all company facilities
3. Secure outdoor equipment, signage, and materials that could become projectiles
4. Test backup power systems and communication equipment
5. Create detailed inventory of all equipment and vehicles for insurance purposes

**Personnel Preparedness Support:**
1. Provide hurricane preparedness information and checklists to all employees
2. Offer evacuation assistance for employees without adequate transportation
3. Coordinate group purchasing of emergency supplies for cost savings
4. Establish employee assistance fund for hurricane preparation and recovery
5. Verify emergency contact information and communication preferences for all personnel

### 5.2 Storm Approach and Response Procedures

**72-Hour Storm Watch Activation:**
1. Begin continuous monitoring of storm track and intensity forecasts
2. Issue first alert to all personnel regarding potential storm impact
3. Begin securing outdoor equipment and materials at all locations
4. Review evacuation routes and shelter options with employees
5. Coordinate with customers regarding project delays and securing procedures

**48-Hour Hurricane Warning Response:**
1. Activate company Emergency Operations Center and communication protocols
2. Begin evacuation procedures for employees in mandatory evacuation zones
3. Secure all company facilities with storm shutters and protective measures
4. Evacuate vehicles and equipment to secure inland locations
5. Suspend all field operations and secure active job sites

**24-Hour Final Preparation:**
1. Complete evacuation of all personnel from threatened areas
2. Final securing of facilities and equipment protection measures
3. Activate emergency communication systems and check-in procedures
4. Coordinate with local emergency management for any special assistance
5. Implement business continuity procedures and customer communication

### 5.3 Post-Storm Assessment and Recovery

**Immediate Post-Storm Response (First 24 Hours):**
1. Conduct personnel safety and welfare checks for all employees and families
2. Assess damage to company facilities, equipment, and vehicles
3. Coordinate with emergency services for personnel in need of assistance
4. Begin emergency customer service for urgent roofing repairs
5. Document all storm-related damage for insurance and business purposes

**Recovery and Restoration Operations:**
1. Conduct detailed safety inspections before allowing personnel to return to work
2. Coordinate with utility companies for power and communication restoration
3. Begin damage repair and restoration of company facilities and equipment
4. Resume customer service operations and emergency repair services
5. Support community recovery efforts and volunteer coordination

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Personnel Safety During Hurricane Events
**Life Safety Priorities:**
- Evacuation takes precedence over property protection and business operations
- No personnel should remain in mandatory evacuation zones during storm events
- Emergency shelter assistance provided for employees without adequate shelter options
- Medical needs assessment and coordination for employees with special requirements
- Family safety planning assistance and coordination for all personnel

**Post-Storm Safety Hazards:**
- Downed power lines and electrical hazards from storm damage
- Structural damage and building collapse risks
- Flooding and water contamination hazards
- Hazardous material releases and environmental contamination
- Traffic hazards from debris and damaged infrastructure

### 8.2 Business and Property Protection
**Equipment and Vehicle Protection:**
- Elevation or evacuation of vehicles and equipment from flood-prone areas
- Securing of loose materials and equipment that could become projectiles
- Fuel storage protection and emergency fuel supply management
- Important document and record protection through backup and evacuation
- Insurance documentation and claim preparation for potential losses

**Facility Protection Measures:**
- Storm shutter installation or plywood boarding for all windows and openings
- Roof protection and securing of roofing materials and equipment
- Drainage system clearing and flood prevention measures
- Emergency power system testing and fuel supply verification
- Inventory protection through elevation and waterproof storage

# SOP-1090: Wildlife Hazard Awareness (Florida-Specific)

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for identifying, preventing, and responding to wildlife hazards specific to Florida roofing operations. This procedure ensures employee safety while working in environments where encounters with venomous snakes, alligators, aggressive birds, insects, and other Florida wildlife are possible.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC operations where wildlife encounters may occur:
- Rooftop and exterior building work
- Attic and crawl space inspections
- Ground-level site preparation and cleanup
- Equipment storage and staging areas
- Emergency response operations
- Rural and suburban construction sites

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Venomous Species**: Animals capable of injecting toxins through bites or stings
- **Aggressive Wildlife**: Animals that may attack when threatened or protecting territory
- **Protected Species**: Wildlife protected under state or federal law
- **Nesting Season**: Breeding periods when wildlife is most territorial and aggressive
- **Wildlife Corridor**: Natural pathways used by animals for movement and migration
- **Habitat Disturbance**: Human activities that disrupt natural wildlife environments

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Florida Wildlife Hazard Identification

#### 5.1.1 Venomous Snakes
1. **Species Identification**
   - **Eastern Diamondback Rattlesnake**: Large, diamond pattern, rattle
   - **Timber Rattlesnake**: Banded pattern, rattle, aggressive when threatened
   - **Copperhead**: Copper-colored head, hourglass body pattern
   - **Cottonmouth/Water Moccasin**: Dark coloration, white mouth interior
   - **Coral Snake**: Red, yellow, black bands (red touches yellow)
   - **Pygmy Rattlesnake**: Small size, fine rattle, aggressive nature

2. **Habitat Preferences**
   - Brush piles and debris around buildings
   - Rock walls and foundation crevices
   - Roof overhangs and attic spaces
   - Water sources and drainage areas
   - Tall grass and overgrown vegetation

3. **Seasonal Considerations**
   - Most active in warmer months (April-October)
   - Seek shelter in buildings during cooler weather
   - Increased activity during mating season (spring/fall)
   - More aggressive when protecting young

#### 5.1.2 Alligators
1. **Risk Locations**
   - Properties near water bodies (lakes, ponds, canals)
   - Retention ponds in residential developments
   - Golf course water hazards
   - Storm water management systems
   - Coastal and wetland areas

2. **Behavioral Patterns**
   - More active during warm weather
   - Increased aggression during mating season (April-June)
   - Territorial behavior near nests and young
   - Can move quickly on land for short distances

#### 5.1.3 Dangerous Birds
1. **Species of Concern**
   - **Mockingbirds**: Extremely territorial during nesting
   - **Red-winged Blackbirds**: Aggressive near nesting areas
   - **Hawks and Eagles**: Large raptors with sharp talons
   - **Owls**: Nocturnal, silent attacks possible
   - **Crows**: Group attacks when threatened

2. **Nesting Seasons**
   - Most species: March through August
   - Multiple broods possible for some species
   - Extreme aggression when protecting eggs/young
   - Territory defense up to 50 feet from nest

#### 5.1.4 Venomous Spiders and Insects
1. **Spider Species**
   - **Black Widow**: Shiny black with red hourglass marking
   - **Brown Recluse**: Brown with violin-shaped marking
   - **Southern Black Widow**: Similar to black widow, less common

2. **Dangerous Insects**
   - **Fire Ants**: Aggressive colonies, painful stings
   - **Wasps and Yellow Jackets**: Nest in roof areas, multiple stings
   - **Hornets**: Large, aggressive, painful stings
   - **Bees**: Generally docile unless threatened

#### 5.1.5 Other Wildlife Hazards
1. **Bats**
   - Rabies transmission risk
   - Large colonies in attics and roof spaces
   - Federal protection under Endangered Species Act
   - Professional removal required

2. **Wild Boar**
   - Increasing population in Florida
   - Extremely dangerous when cornered
   - Sharp tusks and aggressive behavior
   - Unpredictable movement patterns

### 5.2 Prevention and Avoidance Strategies

#### 5.2.1 Site Preparation
1. **Pre-Work Inspection**
   - Visual survey of entire work area
   - Check for animal signs (tracks, droppings, nests)
   - Inspect brush piles and debris areas
   - Look for holes, dens, and hiding places

2. **Area Clearing**
   - Remove brush and debris from work areas
   - Trim vegetation around building perimeters
   - Clear pathways and escape routes
   - Install temporary barriers as needed

3. **Equipment Staging**
   - Position equipment away from wildlife habitat
   - Secure food and drink containers
   - Maintain clean work areas free of attractants
   - Store materials in enclosed containers

#### 5.2.2 Personal Protective Equipment
1. **Standard Protection**
   - Long pants and closed-toe shoes/boots
   - Gloves for handling materials and debris
   - Long-sleeved shirts in high-risk areas
   - Safety glasses and hard hats

2. **Enhanced Protection**
   - Snake-proof gaiters for ground work
   - Heavy-duty gloves for attic work
   - Puncture-resistant footwear
   - Headlamps for visibility in dark areas

#### 5.2.3 Work Practice Controls
1. **Movement and Positioning**
   - Make noise when approaching work areas
   - Use flashlights in dark spaces before entering
   - Work in pairs when possible
   - Maintain clear escape routes

2. **Material Handling**
   - Shake out stored materials before use
   - Use tools to move debris and materials
   - Avoid reaching into blind areas
   - Check equipment before use

### 5.3 Emergency Response Procedures

#### 5.3.1 Snake Bite Response
1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Keep victim calm and still
   - Remove jewelry and tight clothing near bite
   - Position bite below heart level if possible
   - Mark swelling progression on skin

2. **Medical Response**
   - Call 911 immediately for venomous snake bites
   - Transport to nearest hospital with antivenom
   - Do NOT cut, suck, or apply ice to bite
   - Document snake description for medical team

3. **Prohibited Actions**
   - Do not attempt to catch or kill snake
   - No tourniquets or ice application
   - No cutting or sucking of wound
   - No alcohol or caffeine consumption

#### 5.3.2 Large Animal Encounters
1. **Alligator Encounters**
   - Back away slowly and maintain eye contact
   - Run in straight line if attack is imminent
   - Seek immediate medical attention for any bite
   - Report encounter to Florida Fish and Wildlife

2. **Wild Boar Encounters**
   - Make yourself appear large and back away slowly
   - Climb onto equipment or structures if available
   - Make loud noises to deter approach
   - Call animal control for aggressive animals

#### 5.3.3 Insect Sting Response
1. **Multiple Stings**
   - Remove victim from area immediately
   - Remove stingers by scraping, not pinching
   - Apply ice and seek medical attention
   - Monitor for allergic reaction symptoms

2. **Allergic Reactions**
   - Administer epinephrine if available and trained
   - Call 911 immediately
   - Monitor airway and breathing
   - Be prepared to perform CPR

### 5.4 Professional Wildlife Removal

#### 5.4.1 When to Call Professionals
1. **Mandatory Professional Removal**
   - Protected species (bats, certain birds, reptiles)
   - Large alligators (4 feet or longer)
   - Venomous snake removal
   - Large animal nests or dens

2. **Recommended Professional Services**
   - Multiple wasp or bee colonies
   - Aggressive bird nesting situations
   - Unknown species identification
   - Recurring wildlife problems

#### 5.4.2 Coordination Procedures
1. **Service Selection**
   - Verify proper licensing and insurance
   - Confirm expertise with specific species
   - Check references and certifications
   - Ensure compliance with regulations

2. **Work Coordination**
   - Schedule removal before work begins
   - Coordinate access and safety requirements
   - Document removal activities
   - Verify area is safe before resuming work

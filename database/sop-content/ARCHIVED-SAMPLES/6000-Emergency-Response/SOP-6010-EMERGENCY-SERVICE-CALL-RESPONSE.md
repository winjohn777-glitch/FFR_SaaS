# SOP-6010: Emergency Service Call Response Procedures

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes comprehensive protocols for responding to emergency roofing service calls in Florida, ensuring rapid response capabilities, customer safety, proper documentation, and regulatory compliance during critical roofing emergencies and post-storm situations.

### Scope
- Emergency call intake and prioritization procedures
- Rapid response team deployment and coordination
- Safety assessment and emergency stabilization techniques
- Customer communication and expectation management
- Insurance coordination and documentation requirements
- Regulatory compliance and permit management for emergency work

## 4. Procedure Overview

### Emergency Call Response Process
1. Emergency call intake and threat assessment
2. Response team dispatch and arrival coordination
3. Safety assessment and hazard identification
4. Emergency stabilization and temporary protection
5. Documentation and follow-up service coordination

### Response Prioritization
1. Life safety and structural integrity threats (immediate response)
2. Active water intrusion and property damage (1-2 hour response)
3. Potential damage prevention and stabilization (4-6 hour response)
4. Assessment and permanent repair coordination (24-48 hour response)

### Post-Emergency Service
1. Permanent repair estimation and planning
2. Insurance claim support and documentation
3. Permit application and regulatory compliance
4. Quality follow-up and customer satisfaction verification

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Emergency Call Intake Protocol
```
Call Assessment Checklist:
□ Customer contact information and location
□ Nature and severity of roofing emergency
□ Safety hazards and immediate threats
□ Water intrusion or structural damage present
□ Accessibility and equipment requirements
□ Insurance carrier information
□ Preferred response timeframe and expectations

Priority Classification:
Priority 1 (Immediate): Life safety threats, structural collapse risk
Priority 2 (Urgent): Active water intrusion, significant property damage
Priority 3 (High): Potential damage, preventive stabilization needed
Priority 4 (Standard): Assessment, planning, non-urgent repairs
```

### 5.2 Response Team Dispatch
```
Team Composition:
- Team Leader (certified and experienced)
- Minimum 2 technicians (safety and efficiency)
- Emergency equipment and materials
- Safety equipment and personal protection
- Documentation and communication tools

Equipment Requirements:
- Emergency tarps and temporary covering materials
- Basic hand tools and fastening supplies
- Safety equipment and fall protection
- Ladders and access equipment appropriate for conditions
- Digital camera and documentation materials
```

### 5.3 On-Site Safety Assessment
```
Safety Evaluation Protocol:
1. Initial visual assessment from ground level
2. Structural integrity evaluation and stability check
3. Electrical hazard identification and protection
4. Fall hazard assessment and protection planning
5. Weather condition evaluation and work feasibility
6. Access route planning and safety implementation

Documentation Requirements:
- Photo documentation of damage and hazards
- Written assessment of safety conditions
- Temporary protection recommendations
- Permanent repair scope and recommendations
```

### 5.4 Emergency Stabilization Procedures
```
Temporary Protection Methods:
- Emergency tarping and weather protection
- Structural support and stabilization
- Water diversion and drainage protection
- Debris removal and area securing
- Access prevention and safety barriers

Quality Standards:
- Secure attachment preventing wind displacement
- Adequate coverage preventing water intrusion
- Professional appearance and customer confidence
- Minimal additional damage during installation
- Clear explanation and customer education
```

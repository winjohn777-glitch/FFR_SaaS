# SOP-6201: Post-Storm Damage Assessment and Emergency Repairs

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive post-storm damage assessment and emergency repair procedures for Florida First Roofing LLC to provide rapid, accurate damage evaluation, prioritize emergency repairs, and coordinate with insurance carriers for expedited claims processing following major storm events.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all post-storm operations following hurricanes, tornadoes, severe thunderstorms, and other weather-related damage events affecting customer properties throughout Florida First Roofing LLC service areas, including emergency response, damage documentation, and temporary repairs.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Storm Response Coordinator**: Oversee all post-storm assessment operations and emergency repair coordination
- **Lead Damage Assessors**: Conduct comprehensive property damage evaluations and documentation
- **Emergency Repair Teams**: Perform critical temporary repairs and water damage prevention
- **Insurance Liaison**: Coordinate with adjusters and manage claims documentation
- **Customer Service Manager**: Manage customer communications and emergency service requests
- **Safety Inspector**: Ensure all assessment and repair activities meet safety standards

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Assessment Technology:
- Thermal imaging cameras for hidden damage detection
- Professional digital cameras with macro and wide-angle capabilities
- Drones with 4K video and thermal imaging capabilities
- Moisture meters and structural damage detection equipment
- Mobile assessment software with real-time documentation capabilities

### Emergency Repair Supplies:
- Emergency tarping systems and water barrier materials
- Temporary structural support systems and bracing materials
- Emergency sealants and waterproofing compounds
- Portable generators and emergency lighting systems
- Safety equipment for hazardous damage assessment

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Conduct comprehensive safety assessment before entering damaged structures
- Verify electrical system safety and implement lockout/tagout procedures
- Assess structural integrity and implement appropriate safety measures
- Use proper PPE for contaminated water and debris exposure
- Coordinate with utility companies for power line and gas line safety
- Maintain fall protection protocols on damaged roofing systems

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Initial Response and Triage (0-4 Hours Post-Storm)
1. **Emergency Call Management**:
   - Activate storm response call center with expanded capacity
   - Implement emergency triage system for prioritizing damage reports
   - Document emergency calls with location, damage type, and urgency level
   - Coordinate with emergency services for life-threatening situations
   - Establish preliminary response schedules based on damage severity

2. **Area Assessment and Route Planning**:
   - Deploy aerial reconnaissance using drone systems
   - Assess road conditions and identify access limitations
   - Coordinate with local authorities for area access and safety information
   - Develop optimal routing for assessment teams based on priority and access
   - Establish staging areas for equipment and emergency supplies

### Phase 2: Comprehensive Damage Assessment (4-24 Hours)
1. **Systematic Property Evaluation**:
   - Conduct exterior inspection using systematic grid approach
   - Document all visible damage using photographic and video evidence
   - Perform thermal imaging assessment for hidden moisture and structural damage
   - Measure and quantify damage using professional assessment tools
   - Complete detailed damage assessment forms with GPS coordinates

2. **Advanced Damage Documentation**:
   - **Roofing System Assessment**: Document shingle/tile damage, flashing failures, structural impact
   - **Water Intrusion Evaluation**: Identify all water entry points and moisture penetration
   - **Structural Damage Assessment**: Evaluate framing, decking, and support system integrity
   - **Interior Impact Documentation**: Assess ceiling, wall, and flooring damage from water intrusion
   - **Secondary Damage Identification**: Document wind-blown debris impact and related damage

3. **Emergency Repair Prioritization**:
   - Classify damage severity using standardized emergency rating system
   - Identify immediate water intrusion prevention requirements
   - Assess structural safety and temporary support needs
   - Prioritize repairs based on life safety, property protection, and habitability
   - Coordinate emergency repairs with comprehensive restoration planning

### Phase 3: Emergency Repair Implementation (24-72 Hours)
1. **Critical Water Damage Prevention**:
   - Install emergency tarping systems using hurricane-rated materials
   - Apply temporary sealants and emergency waterproofing measures
   - Install temporary drainage systems to redirect water flow
   - Implement emergency structural support for compromised roof systems
   - Coordinate with water damage restoration specialists for interior protection

2. **Temporary Structural Repairs**:
   - Install temporary structural bracing for damaged roof framing
   - Secure loose roofing materials and debris removal
   - Implement temporary weatherproofing for exposed areas
   - Install emergency electrical safety measures and GFCI protection
   - Coordinate with utility companies for safe temporary power restoration

### Phase 4: Insurance Coordination and Documentation (24-96 Hours)
1. **Comprehensive Claims Documentation**:
   - Prepare detailed damage assessment reports with photographic evidence
   - Complete insurance carrier-specific documentation requirements
   - Coordinate adjuster inspections and provide technical expertise
   - Prepare preliminary repair estimates with emergency and permanent costs
   - Document emergency repair costs and temporary protection measures

2. **Adjuster Coordination and Support**:
   - Schedule adjuster inspections and provide site access coordination
   - Present technical damage assessment findings and repair recommendations
   - Provide expert testimony on damage causation and repair requirements
   - Negotiate emergency repair authorizations and temporary protection coverage
   - Coordinate with public adjusters when retained by property owners

### Phase 5: Permanent Repair Planning and Coordination (3-7 Days)
1. **Comprehensive Repair Planning**:
   - Develop detailed repair specifications based on assessment findings
   - Coordinate with insurance carriers for repair authorization and coverage
   - Prepare material lists and coordinate supplier delivery schedules
   - Develop project timelines based on material availability and crew scheduling
   - Coordinate with customers on repair scope and temporary living arrangements

2. **Quality Assurance and Follow-up**:
   - Verify emergency repairs are maintaining property protection
   - Monitor weather conditions and adjust temporary protection as needed
   - Coordinate with customers on repair progress and timeline updates
   - Implement quality control measures for all emergency and permanent repairs
   - Document lessons learned and process improvements for future storms

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Damage assessment accuracy: 95% correlation with final adjuster evaluations
- Emergency repair effectiveness: 100% water intrusion prevention success rate
- Response time: Initial assessment within 24 hours of safe access conditions
- Documentation completeness: 100% of required insurance documentation submitted
- Customer satisfaction: 4.8/5 rating for emergency response and communication

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Access limitations due to debris**: Coordinate with debris removal services and local authorities
- **Assessment equipment failures**: Maintain backup equipment and alternative documentation methods
- **Insurance adjuster delays**: Proceed with emergency repairs and document for later reimbursement
- **Material shortage for emergency repairs**: Maintain strategic emergency supply inventory
- **Customer communication challenges**: Implement multiple communication channels and regular updates

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Implement backup procedures and alternative assessment methods
2. Coordinate with emergency services and local authorities for assistance
3. Escalate critical issues to Storm Response Coordinator immediately
4. Document all challenges and implement process improvements
5. Maintain customer communication and manage expectations effectively

# SOP-6001: Storm Tracking and Decision Making Procedures

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes systematic protocols for tracking tropical weather systems, hurricanes, and severe storms affecting Florida operations, and provides clear decision-making frameworks for operational adjustments, safety measures, and business continuity during storm events.

### Scope
- Real-time weather monitoring and storm tracking procedures
- Decision-making criteria for operational modifications
- Communication protocols for storm-related decisions
- Safety threshold establishment and implementation
- Resource allocation and protection decision frameworks
- Post-storm assessment and operational resumption criteria

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Weather Monitoring Protocol
```
Daily Monitoring Schedule:
6:00 AM: Initial weather assessment and 24-hour forecast review
12:00 PM: Midday conditions update and afternoon forecast
6:00 PM: Evening assessment and overnight forecast review
During Storm Threats: Hourly monitoring and assessment

Information Sources:
- National Hurricane Center (primary)
- Florida Division of Emergency Management
- Local National Weather Service offices
- Commercial weather monitoring services
```

### 5.2 Decision-Making Criteria
```
Wind Speed Thresholds:
- 25-35 mph: Heightened monitoring, safety briefings
- 35-45 mph: Non-essential work cessation, equipment securing
- 45+ mph: Complete work stoppage, site evacuation
- Hurricane conditions: Full emergency protocol activation

Additional Factors:
- Lightning activity and electrical storm presence
- Heavy rainfall and flooding potential
- Storm surge and coastal flooding risks
- Tornado watch/warning conditions
```

### 5.3 Communication Protocols
```
Internal Communication:
1. Management team notification (immediate)
2. Field supervisor alerts (within 30 minutes)
3. Employee notification (within 1 hour)
4. Administrative team briefing (within 2 hours)

External Communication:
1. Customer notifications (within 2 hours)
2. Vendor and supplier alerts (within 4 hours)
3. Insurance carrier notification (as required)
4. Regulatory reporting (as mandated)
```

### 5.4 Operational Decision Matrix
```
Threat Level 1 (Monitoring):
- Continue normal operations with enhanced monitoring
- Brief crews on weather awareness and safety
- Prepare equipment and materials for potential securing
- Update emergency contact information and procedures

Threat Level 2 (Preparation):
- Cease non-essential outdoor work activities
- Secure loose materials and equipment
- Brief customers on potential service impacts
- Activate enhanced communication protocols

Threat Level 3 (Implementation):
- Stop all outdoor work activities immediately
- Implement complete equipment securing procedures
- Evacuate personnel from high-risk areas
- Activate full emergency response protocols
```

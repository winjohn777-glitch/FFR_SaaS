## 1. PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for business continuity planning and implementation to ensure Florida First Roofing LLC maintains essential operations during crisis events, minimizes service disruptions, and enables rapid recovery while protecting employees, customers, and business assets.

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to:
- All management and supervisory personnel
- Department heads and team leaders
- Emergency response coordinators
- IT and systems administrators
- All employees during crisis situations
- Key vendors and contractors

## 3. DEFINITIONS

**Business Continuity Plan (BCP)**: Comprehensive strategy for maintaining essential business functions during disruptions
**Critical Business Functions**: Operations essential for company survival and customer safety
**Recovery Time Objective (RTO)**: Target time for restoring operations after disruption
**Recovery Point Objective (RPO)**: Maximum acceptable data loss during disruption
**Alternate Work Site**: Secondary location for continuing operations during emergencies

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Chief Operating Officer
- Oversee business continuity planning and implementation
- Authorize activation of continuity plans
- Coordinate with senior leadership on strategic decisions
- Monitor plan effectiveness and performance

### 4.2 Business Continuity Coordinator
- Manage day-to-day continuity planning activities
- Coordinate with department heads on functional plans
- Maintain continuity documentation and procedures
- Conduct training and testing exercises

### 4.3 Department Heads
- Develop department-specific continuity plans
- Identify critical functions and resources
- Train staff on continuity procedures
- Implement assigned continuity actions

### 4.4 IT Manager
- Maintain technology infrastructure redundancy
- Implement data backup and recovery systems
- Coordinate telecommunications continuity
- Support remote work capabilities

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Business Continuity Plan Development

#### 5.1.1 Business Impact Analysis
1. **Critical Function Identification**
   - Customer safety and emergency response
   - Active project completion and safety
   - Employee safety and welfare
   - Financial and accounting operations
   - Vendor and supplier relationships

2. **Impact Assessment**
   - Revenue loss calculations
   - Customer satisfaction impact
   - Regulatory compliance requirements
   - Reputation and market position effects
   - Recovery cost estimations

3. **Resource Requirements Analysis**
   - Essential personnel identification
   - Critical equipment and materials
   - Technology and communication needs
   - Facility and workspace requirements
   - Financial resources and cash flow

#### 5.1.2 Risk Assessment and Prioritization
1. **Threat Identification**
   - Natural disasters (hurricanes, floods, tornadoes)
   - Technology failures and cyber attacks
   - Supply chain disruptions
   - Personnel unavailability
   - Economic and market disruptions

2. **Vulnerability Analysis**
   - Geographic risk factors in Florida
   - Technology dependency vulnerabilities
   - Single points of failure identification
   - Resource concentration risks
   - Communication system weaknesses

### 5.2 Continuity Strategy Implementation

#### 5.2.1 Alternate Operations Setup
1. **Backup Facility Establishment**
   - Secondary office space identification
   - Equipment and technology deployment
   - Communication system setup
   - Access and security arrangements
   - Utility and service connections

2. **Remote Work Capabilities**
   - Home office equipment distribution
   - VPN and secure access setup
   - Cloud-based system implementation
   - Mobile communication tools
   - Document and file sharing platforms

#### 5.2.2 Critical Process Continuity
1. **Customer Service Continuity**
   - Emergency hotline establishment
   - Customer communication protocols
   - Service delivery alternatives
   - Project timeline adjustments
   - Quality maintenance standards

2. **Financial Operations Continuity**
   - Banking and payment processing
   - Payroll and benefits administration
   - Accounts receivable and payable
   - Insurance claim processing
   - Cash flow management

### 5.3 Florida-Specific Business Continuity Considerations

#### 5.3.1 Hurricane Season Preparedness
1. **Pre-Season Planning**
   - Annual plan review and updates
   - Equipment and supply stockpiling
   - Personnel cross-training completion
   - Communication system testing
   - Vendor and contractor coordination

2. **Storm Event Implementation**
   - Staged activation based on storm track
   - Personnel safety prioritization
   - Equipment and asset protection
   - Customer project securing
   - Supply chain management

#### 5.3.2 Post-Disaster Recovery
1. **Immediate Assessment (0-24 hours)**
   - Personnel safety verification
   - Facility damage assessment
   - Equipment and asset inventory
   - Communication system restoration
   - Customer safety verification

2. **Short-term Recovery (1-7 days)**
   - Essential operations restoration
   - Customer service resumption
   - Supply chain reestablishment
   - Workforce mobilization
   - Financial operations restart

3. **Long-term Recovery (1 week+)**
   - Full operations restoration
   - Facility repair and reconstruction
   - Market position reestablishment
   - Lessons learned integration
   - Plan improvement implementation

#### 5.4.1 Data Protection and Backup
1. **Data Backup Systems**

   - Offsite storage implementation
   - Cloud backup redundancy

2. **System Redundancy**
   - Multiple internet connections
   - Backup power systems
   - Alternative communication methods

#### 5.4.2 IT Infrastructure Continuity
1. **Network Continuity**
   - VPN access for remote work

   - Mobile hotspot networks

2. **Application and Software Continuity**
   - Cloud-based application access
   - Software licensing management

### 5.5 Supply Chain and Vendor Continuity

#### 5.5.1 Vendor Relationship Management
1. **Critical Vendor Identification**

   - Subcontractor networks
   - Professional service providers
   - Emergency service vendors

2. **Alternative Vendor Development**
   - Backup supplier relationships

1. **Emergency Stock Management**

## 6. CONTINUITY PLAN ACTIVATION

### 6.1 Activation Triggers

- Major facility damage or destruction
- Significant personnel unavailability
- Technology system failures
- Supply chain disruptions

### 6.2 Activation Procedures
1. **Initial Assessment**
   - Situation evaluation and scope determination
   - Impact analysis and priority setting
   - Resource availability assessment
   - Timeline and duration estimation

2. **Team Mobilization**
   - Emergency response team activation
   - Department head notification
   - Key personnel contact and assembly
   - Vendor and contractor notification

3. **Plan Implementation**
   - Execute departmental continuity procedures
   - Activate alternate operations facilities
   - Implement communication protocols
   - Monitor progress and effectiveness

## 1. PURPOSE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for managing media relations and public communications during crisis situations to ensure accurate information dissemination, protect Florida First Roofing LLC's reputation, and maintain public trust while complying with legal and regulatory requirements.

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to:
- Senior management team
- Designated company spokespersons
- Marketing and communications staff
- Crisis response team members
- All employees regarding media contact
- External public relations consultants

## 3. DEFINITIONS

**Media Relations**: Management of company interactions with news media outlets
**Public Communication**: Information shared with general public through various channels
**Spokesperson**: Designated individual authorized to speak on behalf of the company
**Media Kit**: Comprehensive package of company information for media distribution
**News Release**: Official statement issued to media organizations
**Crisis Messaging**: Strategic communication designed for emergency situations

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Chief Executive Officer
- Serve as primary company spokesperson for major crises
- Approve all strategic messaging decisions
- Authorize media engagement strategies
- Oversee overall communication direction

### 4.2 Chief Marketing Officer
- Coordinate all media relations activities
- Manage public communication strategies
- Oversee spokesperson training and preparation
- Monitor media coverage and public sentiment

### 4.3 Designated Spokespersons
- Deliver approved messages to media
- Represent company at press conferences
- Respond to media inquiries professionally
- Maintain message consistency and accuracy

### 4.4 All Employees
- Refer all media inquiries to designated spokespersons
- Avoid unauthorized statements to media
- Support official communication efforts
- Maintain confidentiality requirements

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Media Relations Preparation

#### 5.1.1 Spokesperson Designation and Training
1. **Primary Spokesperson Selection**
   - CEO for major corporate crises
   - Department heads for technical issues
   - Legal counsel for regulatory matters
   - Safety director for accident-related incidents

2. **Backup Spokesperson Assignment**
   - Train multiple spokespersons for availability
   - Establish clear hierarchy and authority
   - Provide specialized training for each role
   - Maintain current contact information

3. **Media Training Requirements**
   - Annual media interview training
   - Crisis communication workshops
   - Message delivery practice sessions
   - On-camera presentation training

#### 5.1.2 Media Contact Database
1. **Local Media Contacts**
   - Television news stations
   - Newspaper reporters and editors
   - Radio station news departments
   - Online news platforms
   - Trade publication contacts

2. **Contact Information Management**
   - Maintain current reporter contact lists
   - Update media contact databases regularly
   - Track preferred communication methods
   - Document beat coverage areas

### 5.2 Crisis Media Response Protocols

#### 5.2.1 Immediate Response (0-2 hours)
1. **Media Inquiry Assessment**
   - Evaluate inquiry urgency and scope
   - Determine appropriate spokesperson
   - Assess information requirements
   - Plan response timeline

2. **Initial Response Actions**
   - Acknowledge media inquiry receipt
   - Provide estimated response timeframe
   - Gather necessary information
   - Prepare initial statement if required

#### 5.2.2 Information Gathering and Verification
1. **Fact Collection Process**
   - Gather accurate incident information
   - Verify technical details with experts
   - Confirm timeline and scope
   - Document all relevant facts

2. **Legal and Compliance Review**
   - Review statements with legal counsel
   - Ensure regulatory compliance
   - Confirm accuracy of all claims
   - Approve message content

### 5.3 Public Communication Strategy Implementation

#### 5.3.1 Message Development
1. **Core Message Creation**
   - Develop clear, concise key messages
   - Address primary public concerns
   - Include relevant safety information
   - Maintain consistent messaging tone

2. **Audience-Specific Messaging**
   - Tailor messages for different media outlets
   - Adapt content for various audiences
   - Consider cultural and regional factors
   - Address specific stakeholder concerns

#### 5.3.2 Communication Channel Selection
1. **Traditional Media**
   - Press releases and statements
   - Press conferences and briefings
   - Media interviews and availability
   - Editorial board meetings

2. **Digital Communication Platforms**
   - Company website updates
   - Social media announcements
   - Email newsletters
   - Online video statements

### 5.4 Florida-Specific Media Relations Considerations

#### 5.4.1 Hurricane Season Communications
1. **Pre-Season Media Preparation**
   - Establish relationships with weather reporters
   - Prepare hurricane response statements
   - Coordinate with emergency management officials
   - Develop storm preparation messaging

2. **Storm Event Media Management**
   - Provide regular safety updates
   - Share preparation and response activities
   - Coordinate with local emergency officials
   - Maintain continuous communication availability

#### 5.4.2 Local Media Relationship Management
1. **Community Media Engagement**
   - Build relationships with local reporters
   - Participate in community events
   - Provide educational information
   - Support local journalism efforts

2. **Industry Media Relations**
   - Engage with roofing trade publications
   - Participate in industry conferences
   - Share expertise and best practices
   - Build thought leadership presence

### 5.5 Press Conference and Media Event Management

#### 5.5.1 Press Conference Planning
1. **Event Logistics**
   - Select appropriate venue and timing
   - Arrange necessary equipment and materials
   - Prepare visual aids and demonstrations
   - Coordinate security and safety measures

2. **Content Preparation**
   - Develop opening statements
   - Prepare answers for anticipated questions
   - Create supporting materials and handouts
   - Practice delivery and timing

#### 5.5.2 Media Event Execution
1. **Event Management**
   - Control access and attendance
   - Facilitate question and answer sessions
   - Manage time and agenda adherence
   - Ensure accurate information delivery

2. **Follow-up Activities**
   - Provide additional information as promised
   - Correct any inaccuracies promptly
   - Monitor media coverage and response
   - Evaluate event effectiveness

## 6. MEDIA MONITORING AND RESPONSE

### 6.1 Media Coverage Monitoring
- Track print and digital news coverage
- Monitor social media mentions and discussions
- Analyze public sentiment and reactions
- Identify trending topics and concerns

### 6.2 Rapid Response Protocols
- Correct inaccurate information immediately
- Address negative coverage professionally
- Provide additional context when necessary
- Engage in constructive dialogue

## 7. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORD KEEPING

### 7.1 Media Interaction Documentation
- Record all media contacts and interactions
- Maintain transcripts of interviews and statements
- Document questions asked and answers provided
- Track follow-up commitments and actions

### 7.2 Coverage Analysis
- Collect and analyze all media coverage
- Assess message effectiveness and accuracy
- Monitor public perception changes
- Document lessons learned

## 8. CRISIS-SPECIFIC MESSAGING FRAMEWORKS

### 8.1 Safety-Related Incidents
- Prioritize human safety and welfare
- Express concern for affected parties
- Emphasize company safety commitment
- Provide accurate incident information

### 8.2 Operational Disruptions
- Acknowledge service impacts honestly
- Provide restoration timelines
- Explain mitigation measures
- Maintain customer focus

### 8.3 Regulatory or Legal Issues
- Defer to legal counsel guidance
- Maintain compliance focus
- Avoid speculation or assumptions
- Emphasize cooperation with authorities

# SOP-2079: Ridge and Hip Installation Procedures

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for ridge and hip installation on all Florida First Roofing LLC projects, ensuring proper ventilation, weather protection, structural integrity, and aesthetic appearance while addressing Florida's challenging weather conditions including high winds, driving rain, and hurricane impacts.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all ridge and hip installation activities including:
- Ridge cap and hip cap installations
- Ridge ventilation systems
- Hip starter installation
- Ridge board and hip rafter systems
- Snow guards and ice dam protection
- Architectural ridge systems
- Ventilation integration systems
- Weather sealing procedures

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Ridge**: The horizontal line at the top of a roof where two roof planes meet.

**Hip**: The external angle formed where two sloping roof planes meet.

**Ridge Cap**: The covering material installed over the ridge to provide weather protection.

**Hip Cap**: The covering material installed over hips to provide weather protection.

**Ridge Vent**: A ventilation device installed along the ridge to allow air circulation.

**Hip Starter**: The first piece of hip cap material installed at the bottom of a hip.

**Snow Guard**: A device installed to prevent snow and ice from sliding off the roof.

**Ridge Board**: The structural member at the ridge that supports the rafters.

## 6. RIDGE CAP INSTALLATION

### 6.1 Ridge Preparation
**Substrate Preparation:**
1. **Ridge Line Verification**
   - Check ridge alignment
   - Verify straightness
   - Measure ridge length
   - Mark installation points

2. **Surface Preparation**
   - Clean ridge area thoroughly
   - Remove debris and dust
   - Check for proper ventilation
   - Verify substrate condition

**Ventilation Installation:**
- Install ridge vents if required
- Secure ventilation systems
- Verify air flow paths
- Test ventilation function

### 6.2 Material Preparation
**Ridge Cap Cutting:**
- Measure ridge length accurately
- Cut ridge caps to proper length
- Allow for end overlaps
- Prepare end treatments

**Fastener Selection:**
- Use manufacturer-specified fasteners
- Select proper length
- Choose corrosion-resistant materials
- Verify wind resistance ratings

### 6.3 Installation Procedures
**Starting Point Selection:**
- Begin at end opposite prevailing wind
- Start at hip intersection if present
- Plan overlap sequence
- Mark starting position

**Ridge Cap Installation:**
1. **First Cap Installation**
   - Position cap properly
   - Check alignment
   - Install fasteners per schedule
   - Verify weather seal

2. **Subsequent Caps**
   - Maintain proper overlap
   - Check alignment continuously
   - Install fasteners consistently
   - Ensure weather protection

3. **End Treatment**
   - Install end caps or closures
   - Seal end joints properly
   - Provide weather protection
   - Check for water infiltration

### 6.4 Fastening Requirements
**Standard Applications:**
- Follow manufacturer specifications
- Use proper fastener spacing
- Penetrate substrate adequately
- Avoid over-driving fasteners

**High-Wind Applications:**
- Increase fastener density
- Use structural fasteners
- Provide enhanced attachment

### 6.5 Weather Sealing

- Use appropriate sealants
- Apply at proper temperature

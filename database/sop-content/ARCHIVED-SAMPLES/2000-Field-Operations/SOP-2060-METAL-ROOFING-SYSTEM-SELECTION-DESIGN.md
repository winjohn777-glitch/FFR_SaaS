# SOP-2060: Metal Roofing System Selection and Design

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for metal roofing system selection, design specification, performance evaluation, and installation planning to ensure optimal performance, hurricane resistance, and compliance with Florida Building Code requirements.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in metal roofing system design, material selection, structural evaluation, and installation planning for residential and commercial projects throughout Florida's hurricane-prone regions.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Design Engineer**: System design and structural analysis
- **Metal Roofing Specialist**: Material selection and installation method specification
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate design requirements and client specifications
- **Structural Engineer**: Load analysis and framing adequacy assessment
- **Code Compliance Officer**: Building code verification and permit coordination

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: System Selection and Performance Analysis
1. **Metal Roofing System Types Evaluation**:
   - **Standing Seam Systems**: Structural vs. architectural applications
   - **Exposed Fastener Systems**: Ribbed panels and corrugated options
   - **Metal Shingle Systems**: Residential applications and aesthetic options
   - **Flat Seam Systems**: Low-slope and commercial applications
   - **Specialty Systems**: Curved panels and architectural features

2. **Material Selection Criteria**:
   - **Aluminum**: Corrosion resistance, coastal applications, weight advantages
   - **Steel**: Galvanized, Galvalume, painted systems, strength characteristics
   - **Copper**: Premium applications, longevity, patina development
   - **Zinc**: Architectural applications, self-healing properties
   - **Stainless Steel**: Extreme environment applications, maintenance considerations

3. **Performance Requirements Assessment**:
   - Wind uplift resistance per Miami-Dade hurricane standards
   - Thermal movement and expansion joint requirements
   - Fire resistance ratings and code compliance
   - Energy efficiency and cool roof requirements
   - Noise control and acoustic performance considerations

### Phase 2: Structural Design and Load Analysis
1. **Dead Load Analysis**:
   - Calculate metal roofing system weight (1-3 lbs/sq ft typical)
   - Evaluate framing adequacy for concentrated loads
   - Assess snow load requirements where applicable
   - Consider equipment loads and maintenance access requirements
   - Document structural reinforcement needs

2. **Wind Load Calculations**:
   - Apply ASCE 7 wind load standards for Florida hurricane zones
   - Calculate uplift forces for different roof zones
   - Design fastener patterns and spacing requirements
   - Evaluate edge and corner loading conditions
   - Specify enhanced attachment for HVHZ requirements

3. **Thermal Analysis and Movement Design**:
   - Calculate thermal expansion and contraction rates
   - Design expansion joints and movement accommodation
   - Plan for differential movement between materials
   - Specify thermal break requirements and details
   - Design restraint systems for thermal stress management

### Phase 3: System Design and Detail Development
1. **Panel Layout and Configuration**:
   - Determine panel run lengths and joint locations
   - Design ridge, hip, and eave conditions
   - Plan for penetrations and equipment mounting
   - Specify valley and drainage systems
   - Design transition details to other roofing materials

2. **Fastening System Design**:
   - **Concealed Fasteners**: Clip systems and attachment methods
   - **Exposed Fasteners**: Screw patterns and sealing requirements
   - **Structural Attachment**: Purlin spacing and load transfer
   - Specify fastener materials and corrosion resistance
   - Design for thermal movement accommodation

3. **Weatherproofing and Sealing Systems**:
   - Design seam configurations and sealing methods
   - Specify sealant types and application procedures
   - Plan for ice dam prevention and drainage
   - Design gutter and downspout integration
   - Specify underlayment and vapor barrier systems

### Phase 4: Compliance and Installation Planning
1. **Building Code Compliance Verification**:
   - Verify Florida Building Code Chapter 15 metal roofing requirements
   - Confirm wind resistance testing and approval documentation
   - Check fire resistance ratings and separation requirements
   - Verify energy code compliance for cool roof requirements
   - Document accessibility requirements for maintenance

2. **Hurricane Resistance Compliance**:
   - Verify Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA) approval
   - Confirm wind uplift testing compliance (up to 180+ mph)
   - Document impact resistance testing for debris simulation
   - Specify enhanced fastening for HVHZ applications
   - Plan for quality assurance inspection requirements

3. **Installation Sequence and Logistics Planning**:
   - Develop installation timeline and crew requirements
   - Plan material delivery and staging procedures
   - Coordinate with other trades and building systems
   - Specify quality control checkpoints and inspection procedures
   - Plan for weather protection during installation

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Thermal movement problems**: Review expansion joint design and accommodation
- **Wind uplift failures**: Verify fastener patterns and structural attachment
- **Seam failures**: Assess sealing methods and thermal stress factors
- **Noise issues**: Evaluate substrate attachment and insulation systems
- **Corrosion concerns**: Review material compatibility and galvanic action

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Analyze performance issue and identify root causes
2. Review design calculations and manufacturer specifications
3. Consult with structural engineer for load-related issues
4. Coordinate with manufacturer for technical support
5. Implement design modifications and update documentation

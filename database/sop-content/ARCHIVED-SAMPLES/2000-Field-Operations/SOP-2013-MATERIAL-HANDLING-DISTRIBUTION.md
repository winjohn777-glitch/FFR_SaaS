# SOP-2013: Material Handling and Distribution

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for safe and efficient material handling, distribution, and positioning to ensure worker safety, prevent material damage, optimize workflow efficiency, and maintain quality standards throughout the installation process.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in material handling, distribution, positioning, and movement at job sites, including ground crew, installation teams, and equipment operators throughout Florida First Roofing projects.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Materials Handler**: Execute safe material handling and distribution procedures
- **Site Supervisor**: Coordinate material flow and crew assignments
- **Equipment Operator**: Operate lifting and moving equipment safely
- **Installation Crew**: Handle materials properly during installation
- **Safety Officer**: Monitor handling safety and compliance procedures

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Material Handling Equipment:
- Conveyor systems and material hoists for roof access
- Forklifts, telehandlers, and boom lifts for heavy materials
- Hand trucks, dollies, and manual handling equipment
- Crane and rigging equipment for large material placement
- Material carts and distribution containers

### Safety Equipment:
- Personal protective equipment (hard hats, gloves, safety glasses)
- Fall protection systems and harnesses
- Lifting belts and back support equipment
- Communication devices and signal equipment
- Emergency equipment and first aid supplies

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Verify proper lifting techniques and weight limitations for personnel
- Ensure equipment inspection and safe operation procedures
- Implement fall protection for elevated material handling
- Maintain clear pathways and prevent trip hazards
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment at all times
- Coordinate with other trades to prevent conflicts and accidents

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Material Handling Planning and Preparation
1. **Distribution Planning and Scheduling**:
   - Review daily installation requirements and material needs
   - Plan material flow sequence and timing coordination
   - Assess weather conditions and handling constraints
   - Coordinate with installation crews for material positioning
   - Plan for equipment availability and crew assignments

2. **Equipment Preparation and Safety Checks**:
   - Inspect material handling equipment for safe operation
   - Verify equipment capacity and load limitations
   - Check safety systems and emergency controls
   - Ensure operator certification and training compliance
   - Prepare rigging and lifting accessories

3. **Pathway and Access Preparation**:
   - Clear and prepare material movement routes
   - Establish safe zones and traffic control measures
   - Verify ground conditions and stability for equipment
   - Plan for utilities and overhead hazard avoidance
   - Set up staging areas and temporary storage locations

### Phase 2: Ground-Level Material Handling
1. **Manual Handling Procedures**:
   - **Lifting Techniques**: Proper body mechanics and team lifting
   - **Weight Limits**: Maximum 50 lbs per person, team lift for heavier items
   - **Grip and Stability**: Secure grip and stable footing
   - **Communication**: Clear verbal communication during team lifts
   - **Rest Periods**: Regular breaks to prevent fatigue and injury

2. **Mechanical Handling Equipment Operation**:
   - **Forklift Operations**: Certified operators and proper load securing
   - **Conveyor Systems**: Material flow control and safety monitoring
   - **Hand Trucks and Dollies**: Proper loading and pathway navigation
   - **Telehandler Operations**: Boom positioning and load stability
   - **Equipment Coordination**: Traffic control and communication protocols

3. **Material Preparation and Organization**:
   - Sort materials by installation sequence and location
   - Bundle materials for efficient handling and transport
   - Label materials for easy identification and placement
   - Protect materials during handling and movement
   - Coordinate with installation schedule and crew needs

### Phase 3: Roof-Level Material Distribution
1. **Roof Access and Hoisting Operations**:
   - **Material Hoists**: Certified operation and load management
   - **Conveyor Systems**: Continuous material flow and safety monitoring
   - **Crane Operations**: Rigging and lifting procedures
   - **Manual Carrying**: Safe pathways and load distribution
   - **Boom Lift Operations**: Certified operators and safety protocols

2. **Roof Staging and Positioning**:
   - Position materials for efficient installation workflow
   - Distribute weight to prevent roof overloading
   - Maintain clear pathways for personnel movement
   - Protect materials from wind and weather exposure
   - Coordinate with safety systems and fall protection

3. **Installation Support and Just-in-Time Delivery**:
   - Coordinate material delivery with installation progress
   - Maintain adequate material inventory without over-stocking
   - Support multiple crews with coordinated material flow
   - Manage material quality and protection during distribution
   - Coordinate waste removal and cleanup operations

### Phase 4: Specialized Material Handling
1. **Heavy Material Handling (Tiles, Equipment)**:
   - Plan lifting procedures and equipment requirements
   - Use multiple personnel and mechanical assistance
   - Coordinate rigging and crane operations
   - Verify roof load capacity and distribution
   - Implement enhanced safety measures and spotters

2. **Fragile Material Handling (Membrane, Glass)**:
   - Use specialized handling equipment and techniques
   - Minimize handling frequency and distance
   - Protect materials from damage during movement
   - Use cushioned supports and protective wrapping
   - Coordinate with installation teams for immediate use

3. **Hazardous Material Handling (Chemicals, Solvents)**:
   - Follow manufacturer safety data sheets (SDS)
   - Use appropriate personal protective equipment
   - Implement spill prevention and response procedures
   - Maintain separation from incompatible materials
   - Coordinate with safety personnel and emergency procedures

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Handling damage rate: Maximum 0.5% material damage during handling
- Safety compliance: 100% compliance with lifting and handling procedures
- Efficiency metrics: Material availability within 15 minutes of request
- Equipment utilization: Optimal use of handling equipment and personnel
- Workflow coordination: Smooth material flow without installation delays

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Equipment failures**: Implement backup procedures and alternative methods
- **Weather delays**: Secure materials and adjust handling procedures
- **Personnel injuries**: Follow emergency procedures and medical response
- **Material damage**: Assess damage and coordinate replacement procedures
- **Workflow bottlenecks**: Adjust distribution patterns and crew coordination

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Stop operations immediately if safety issues are identified
2. Assess situation and implement emergency or backup procedures
3. Coordinate with supervision for resource allocation and support
4. Document incidents and implement corrective measures
5. Resume operations with enhanced safety and quality measures

# SOP-2012: On-Site Material Storage and Protection

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for proper on-site material storage, weather protection, security, and inventory management to prevent damage, ensure material quality, and optimize job site efficiency and safety.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in material storage, protection, and management at job sites, temporary storage locations, and staging areas throughout Florida First Roofing projects.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Site Supervisor**: Oversee storage area management and material protection
- **Materials Coordinator**: Plan storage layout and inventory tracking
- **Installation Crew**: Maintain storage organization and material handling
- **Security Officer**: Implement theft prevention and site security measures
- **Safety Coordinator**: Ensure storage safety and access compliance

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Storage Infrastructure:
- Weather-resistant storage containers and temporary buildings
- Pallets, dunnage, and material support systems
- Tarps, covers, and weather protection materials
- Fencing, barriers, and security equipment
- Material handling equipment (forklifts, dollies, carts)

### Environmental Control:
- Ventilation systems for enclosed storage areas
- Dehumidification equipment for moisture-sensitive materials
- Temperature monitoring and control systems
- Drainage systems and water management
- Lighting systems for visibility and security

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Maintain clear access routes and emergency egress paths
- Implement proper material stacking and stability requirements
- Ensure adequate lighting for safe material handling
- Plan for fire prevention and emergency response procedures
- Use appropriate fall protection for elevated storage access
- Implement traffic control for material handling equipment

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Storage Area Planning and Preparation
1. **Site Assessment and Layout Planning**:
   - Evaluate available space and ground conditions
   - Plan storage locations for efficient material flow
   - Consider proximity to work areas and access requirements
   - Assess drainage patterns and water management needs
   - Plan for future deliveries and storage expansion

2. **Storage Infrastructure Setup**:
   - Install temporary storage buildings or containers
   - Prepare level foundations and proper drainage
   - Set up pallets, racks, and material support systems
   - Install lighting, ventilation, and environmental controls
   - Establish security measures and access controls

3. **Organization and Inventory Systems**:
   - Design storage layout for material types and sizes
   - Implement inventory tracking and labeling systems
   - Establish material rotation and FIFO procedures
   - Plan for hazardous material separation and special storage
   - Set up documentation and record-keeping systems

### Phase 2: Material-Specific Storage Requirements
1. **Roofing Shingles and Tiles**:
   - **Storage Surface**: Level, dry pallets or platforms
   - **Stacking**: Maximum 5 bundles high for shingles, appropriate support for tiles
   - **Weather Protection**: Complete coverage from rain and UV exposure
   - **Ventilation**: Adequate air circulation to prevent moisture buildup
   - **Handling**: Minimize movement and avoid damage during storage

2. **Metal Roofing Materials**:
   - **Support Systems**: Continuous support to prevent oil canning
   - **Protection**: Cover from moisture and prevent galvanic corrosion
   - **Separation**: Use dividers between different materials
   - **Handling**: Proper lifting techniques and edge protection
   - **Environment**: Protect from salt air in coastal areas

3. **Membrane and Waterproofing Materials**:
   - **Temperature Control**: Store within manufacturer temperature ranges
   - **UV Protection**: Complete protection from direct sunlight
   - **Ventilation**: Adequate air circulation for roll goods
   - **Chemical Storage**: Separate incompatible materials and solvents
   - **Handling**: Vertical storage for rolls, flat storage for sheets

### Phase 3: Environmental Protection and Quality Preservation
1. **Weather Protection Systems**:
   - **Rain Protection**: Waterproof covers and proper drainage
   - **Wind Protection**: Secure covers and tie-down systems
   - **UV Protection**: Opaque covers for light-sensitive materials
   - **Temperature Control**: Insulation and ventilation for extreme temperatures
   - **Humidity Control**: Dehumidification for moisture-sensitive materials

2. **Moisture and Humidity Management**:
   - Implement proper drainage around storage areas
   - Use moisture barriers between materials and ground contact
   - Monitor humidity levels and implement control measures
   - Ensure adequate ventilation to prevent condensation
   - Protect materials from ground moisture and splash-back

3. **Temperature Control and Thermal Protection**:
   - Monitor temperature ranges per material specifications
   - Provide insulation for extreme temperature protection
   - Implement cooling measures for hot weather conditions
   - Plan for thermal expansion accommodation in storage systems
   - Protect temperature-sensitive adhesives and sealants

### Phase 4: Security and Inventory Management
1. **Theft Prevention and Security Measures**:
   - Install perimeter fencing and access controls
   - Implement lighting systems for visibility and deterrence
   - Use security cameras and alarm systems where appropriate
   - Establish material check-in/check-out procedures
   - Coordinate with security personnel and patrol services

2. **Inventory Tracking and Management**:
   - **Receiving**: Update inventory upon material delivery
   - **Distribution**: Track material movement to work areas
   - **Usage**: Monitor consumption rates and waste tracking
   - **Rotation**: Implement first-in, first-out material usage
   - **Auditing**: Regular inventory counts and reconciliation

3. **Access Control and Distribution**:
   - Establish authorized personnel access lists
   - Implement material requisition and approval procedures
   - Control key and access code distribution
   - Schedule material distribution to minimize storage time
   - Coordinate with installation crews for just-in-time delivery

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Weather protection: 100% protection from moisture and UV exposure
- Organization efficiency: Materials accessible within 5 minutes average
- Security compliance: Zero theft incidents and 100% accountability
- Environmental conditions: Maintain storage within manufacturer specifications
- Inventory accuracy: 99% accuracy in material tracking and counts

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Weather damage**: Assess damage extent and coordinate replacement
- **Theft or vandalism**: Document losses and enhance security measures
- **Storage capacity exceeded**: Coordinate additional storage or accelerated usage
- **Environmental control failures**: Implement emergency protection measures
- **Access problems**: Redesign layout and improve material flow

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Assess issue severity and immediate protection requirements
2. Implement emergency measures to prevent further damage
3. Document problems and coordinate with management
4. Arrange for damaged material replacement or repair
5. Update storage procedures to prevent recurrence

# SOP-2011: Material Delivery and Acceptance Procedures

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for receiving, inspecting, documenting, and accepting material deliveries to ensure quality control, inventory accuracy, and proper documentation for warranty and quality assurance purposes.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in material receiving, inspection, documentation, and acceptance at warehouse facilities, job sites, and temporary storage locations throughout Florida First Roofing operations.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Receiving Supervisor**: Oversee delivery acceptance and quality verification
- **Warehouse Personnel**: Conduct material inspection and documentation
- **Site Supervisor**: Coordinate job site deliveries and storage
- **Quality Control Inspector**: Verify material compliance and specifications
- **Procurement Manager**: Resolve delivery discrepancies and vendor issues

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Inspection Equipment:
- Digital cameras for documentation and damage recording
- Measuring tools for quantity verification and dimensional checking
- Barcode scanners and inventory tracking devices
- Moisture meters and environmental monitoring equipment
- Weight scales and measurement verification tools

### Documentation Tools:
- Delivery receipt forms and inspection checklists
- Mobile devices for real-time data entry and communication
- Material tracking software and inventory management systems
- Quality control documentation and reporting tools
- Communication devices for vendor and office coordination

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Verify proper material handling and lifting techniques
- Ensure safe unloading procedures and equipment operation
- Implement traffic control for delivery vehicles at job sites
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment during inspections
- Maintain clear access routes and emergency egress paths
- Verify driver and delivery personnel safety compliance

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Pre-Delivery Preparation and Coordination
1. **Delivery Schedule Verification**:
   - Confirm delivery appointment and expected arrival time
   - Verify delivery location and site access requirements
   - Review material specifications against purchase order
   - Prepare receiving personnel and inspection equipment
   - Coordinate with site operations for delivery timing

2. **Storage Area Preparation**:
   - Clear and prepare designated storage areas
   - Verify storage capacity and material handling capabilities
   - Ensure weather protection and security measures
   - Prepare material handling equipment (forklifts, dollies)
   - Establish traffic control and safety zones

3. **Documentation Preparation**:
   - Retrieve purchase orders and material specifications
   - Prepare inspection checklists and quality control forms
   - Set up inventory tracking systems and scanning equipment
   - Prepare camera equipment for documentation
   - Review vendor quality standards and acceptance criteria

### Phase 2: Delivery Reception and Initial Inspection
1. **Vehicle and Driver Verification**:
   - Verify delivery vehicle identification and authorization
   - Check driver credentials and delivery documentation
   - Inspect vehicle condition and material protection measures
   - Document delivery time, weather conditions, and personnel
   - Establish communication with delivery personnel

2. **Initial Material Assessment**:
   - Compare delivery against purchase order quantities
   - Verify material types, specifications, and part numbers
   - Check packaging condition and protection adequacy
   - Document any obvious damage or discrepancies
   - Photograph delivery vehicle and initial material condition

3. **Unloading Supervision and Safety**:
   - Direct safe unloading procedures and material handling
   - Monitor for damage during unloading operations
   - Verify proper material handling and protection
   - Document unloading process and any incidents
   - Ensure compliance with site safety requirements

### Phase 3: Detailed Inspection and Quality Verification
1. **Quantity Verification and Documentation**:
   - **Count Verification**: Physical count against delivery documentation
   - **Weight Verification**: Spot-check weights for bulk materials
   - **Measurement Verification**: Check dimensions and specifications
   - **Package Integrity**: Inspect packaging for damage or tampering
   - **Shortage Documentation**: Record any quantity discrepancies

2. **Quality Control Inspection**:
   - **Visual Inspection**: Check for defects, damage, or quality issues
   - **Specification Compliance**: Verify materials meet purchase order specs
   - **Certification Review**: Check quality certificates and test reports
   - **Color/Finish Verification**: Confirm aesthetic specifications
   - **Manufacturer Documentation**: Verify proper labeling and identification

3. **Sample Testing and Verification** (When Required):
   - Collect representative samples for testing
   - Conduct field tests for moisture content and basic properties
   - Verify manufacturer test certificates and compliance documentation
   - Document sample collection and testing procedures
   - Coordinate laboratory testing for critical materials

### Phase 4: Acceptance Decision and Documentation
1. **Acceptance Criteria Evaluation**:
   - **Complete Delivery**: All items per purchase order received
   - **Quality Standards**: Materials meet specified quality requirements
   - **Damage Assessment**: No significant damage affecting performance
   - **Documentation Complete**: All required certificates and paperwork
   - **Vendor Compliance**: Delivery meets contractual requirements

2. **Acceptance or Rejection Documentation**:
   - **Accepted Materials**: Sign delivery receipts and update inventory
   - **Rejected Materials**: Document reasons and coordinate return
   - **Partial Acceptance**: Accept conforming materials, reject defective items
   - **Conditional Acceptance**: Accept with vendor correction requirements
   - **Quality Hold**: Place materials on hold pending resolution

3. **Communication and Record Keeping**:
   - Notify procurement of delivery status and any issues
   - Update inventory management systems in real-time
   - Distribute delivery documentation to appropriate departments
   - File quality control documentation and inspection records
   - Coordinate vendor communication for any discrepancies

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Delivery completeness: 99% accuracy between delivered and ordered quantities
- Damage rate: Maximum 1% damage during delivery and handling
- Documentation accuracy: 100% complete delivery documentation
- Inspection thoroughness: 100% material inspection per quality standards
- Response time: Same-day resolution of delivery discrepancies

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Quantity shortages**: Verify count accuracy and coordinate partial shipment
- **Quality defects**: Document defects and coordinate vendor replacement
- **Delivery damage**: Assess damage extent and determine acceptability
- **Documentation problems**: Contact vendor for missing or incorrect paperwork
- **Schedule conflicts**: Coordinate alternative delivery timing

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document issue details with photographs and measurements
2. Contact vendor immediately to report discrepancies
3. Implement temporary storage or protection measures
4. Coordinate replacement or corrective action timeline
5. Update quality records and vendor performance tracking

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Site Assessment:** A comprehensive evaluation of the existing roofing system, structural conditions, building envelope integration, and environmental factors affecting roofing project planning and implementation.

**Pre-Construction Survey:** A detailed inspection and measurement process conducted before project commencement to verify project scope, identify potential complications, and ensure accurate material estimation and timeline development.

**Structural Integrity Evaluation:** Professional assessment of roof deck, framing, and support structure adequacy to support proposed roofing system loads and installation activities.

**Building Envelope Analysis:** Comprehensive evaluation of how the roofing system integrates with walls, windows, doors, and other building components to ensure weather protection and energy efficiency.

**Hazard Identification Matrix:** Systematic evaluation and documentation of safety hazards, fall protection requirements, and risk mitigation strategies for project planning and worker protection.

**Material Compatibility Assessment:** Technical evaluation ensuring proposed roofing materials are appropriate for structural conditions, climate requirements, and building code compliance.

**Regulatory Compliance Verification:** Comprehensive review of building codes, permit requirements, zoning restrictions, and HOA regulations affecting project implementation and approval.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Pre-Assessment Planning and Preparation
**Step 1: Project Information Gathering and Initial Planning**
1. Review customer request and gather initial project scope and requirements information
2. Obtain property information including address, building age, and previous roofing history
3. Research building permits and zoning requirements through local building department
4. Review HOA requirements and architectural guidelines if applicable
5. Schedule assessment appointment with customer and coordinate access requirements

**Step 2: Assessment Team Assignment and Resource Preparation**
1. Assign qualified assessment team based on project complexity and requirements
2. Prepare assessment equipment including measurement tools, safety equipment, and documentation materials
3. Load digital assessment forms and CRM system mobile applications
4. Verify team member certifications and competency for assigned assessment activities
5. Coordinate weather conditions and reschedule if necessary for safety and accuracy

**Step 3: Safety Planning and Equipment Preparation**
1. Conduct preliminary safety assessment based on available property information
2. Prepare appropriate fall protection equipment and safety gear for team members
3. Identify potential hazards and develop preliminary safety procedures
4. Prepare emergency contact information and rescue equipment as needed
5. Verify insurance coverage and liability protection for assessment activities

### 5.2 On-Site Assessment Implementation
**Step 4: Initial Site Evaluation and Safety Setup**
1. Conduct walk-around inspection to identify immediate safety hazards and access routes
2. Establish safety perimeter and install appropriate fall protection systems
3. Document site conditions with digital photography from multiple angles
4. Verify property boundaries and identify utility locations and restrictions
5. Confirm customer presence and review assessment scope and timeline

**Step 5: Structural Assessment and Condition Evaluation**
1. Measure roof dimensions accurately using laser measurement tools and manual verification
2. Assess roof deck condition through visual inspection and moisture detection
3. Evaluate structural framing adequacy and identify any sagging or damage
4. Inspect attic space for proper ventilation, insulation, and structural conditions
5. Document all findings with detailed photography and written descriptions

**Step 6: Building Envelope Integration Assessment**
1. Inspect roof-to-wall connections and flashing integration points
2. Evaluate chimney, vent, and penetration conditions and sealing adequacy
3. Assess gutter and drainage system capacity and integration requirements
4. Review window and door proximity and weather protection requirements
5. Identify potential moisture intrusion points and mitigation requirements

**Step 7: Material and System Evaluation**
1. Assess existing roofing material condition and remaining service life
2. Identify compatibility requirements for new material installation
3. Evaluate substrate preparation requirements and potential complications
4. Assess access requirements for material delivery and installation logistics
5. Document special requirements for historic buildings or unique architectural features

### 5.3 Assessment Documentation and Reporting
**Step 8: Comprehensive Documentation Completion**
1. Complete standardized assessment forms with all required measurements and observations
2. Organize digital photography with clear labeling and cross-reference to written findings
3. Upload assessment data to CRM system for immediate office access and verification
4. Verify accuracy of all measurements and calculations before leaving site
5. Obtain customer signature on assessment completion form and provide preliminary timeline

**Step 9: Analysis and Recommendation Development**
1. Analyze assessment findings and develop comprehensive project recommendations
2. Calculate accurate material requirements and prepare detailed material specifications
3. Identify permit requirements and coordinate application preparation and submission
4. Develop project timeline and resource allocation requirements
5. Prepare cost estimation and pricing based on comprehensive assessment findings

**Step 10: Customer Communication and Project Planning**
1. Schedule follow-up meeting with customer to review assessment findings and recommendations
2. Present comprehensive assessment report with detailed documentation and recommendations
3. Review project timeline, material selections, and pricing with customer approval
4. Coordinate permit applications and regulatory approval processes
5. Schedule project implementation and coordinate with production team for seamless transition

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Assessment Safety Hazards
**Fall Protection Requirements:**
- All personnel working on elevated surfaces must use appropriate fall protection systems
- Roof access limited to qualified personnel with current fall protection training
- Ladder safety procedures strictly enforced with proper setup and three-point contact
- Weather conditions monitored continuously with work suspension for unsafe conditions
- Emergency rescue procedures and equipment available for immediate response

**Environmental and Structural Hazards:**
- Electrical hazards from overhead power lines and building electrical systems
- Structural instability from damaged or deteriorated roof framing
- Hazardous materials exposure from asbestos or lead-containing materials
- Wildlife encounters including bees, wasps, and other pest hazards
- Slip and fall hazards from wet, icy, or debris-covered surfaces

### 8.2 Risk Mitigation Strategies
**Safety Equipment and Procedures:**
- Personal protective equipment required for all assessment team members
- Fall protection systems inspected daily and properly maintained
- Emergency communication devices available for immediate assistance
- First aid equipment and trained personnel available on complex assessments
- Safety briefings conducted before each assessment with hazard-specific procedures

**Customer and Property Protection:**
- Property protection measures implemented to prevent damage during assessment
- Clear communication with customer regarding assessment activities and timeline
- Insurance verification and liability protection confirmed before assessment begins
- Documentation of existing property conditions to prevent false damage claims
- Restoration procedures established for any minor damage occurring during assessment

### 8.3 Emergency Response Procedures
**Medical Emergency Response:**
- Emergency services contact information readily available and verified
- Assessment team trained in basic first aid and emergency response procedures
- Emergency evacuation routes identified and communicated to team members
- Customer notification procedures for emergency situations and service interruption
- Company notification and support coordination for emergency response

**Equipment Failure and Weather Emergency:**
- Backup measurement equipment available for critical assessment functions
- Weather monitoring and assessment postponement procedures for safety
- Communication procedures for schedule changes and customer notification
- Emergency shelter and evacuation procedures for severe weather events
- Equipment recovery and site security procedures for emergency situations

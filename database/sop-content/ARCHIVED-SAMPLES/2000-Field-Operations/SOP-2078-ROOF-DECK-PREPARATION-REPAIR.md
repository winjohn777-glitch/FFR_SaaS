# SOP-2078: Roof Deck Preparation and Repair

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for roof deck preparation and repair on all Florida First Roofing LLC projects, ensuring structural integrity, proper substrate conditions, and long-term roofing system performance while addressing Florida's challenging weather conditions including high winds, moisture exposure, and hurricane impacts.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all roof deck preparation and repair activities including:
- Plywood and OSB deck systems
- Concrete deck preparation
- Metal deck systems
- Structural deck repairs
- Deck fastening and attachment
- Moisture damage repair
- Structural reinforcement
- Deck surface preparation

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Roof Deck**: The structural surface onto which the roofing system is applied.

**Substrate**: The surface that supports the roofing system components.

**Sheathing**: Structural panels that form the roof deck surface.

**Decking**: The structural material that spans between roof framing members.

**Fastening Schedule**: The specified pattern and spacing of fasteners.

**Deflection**: The amount a structural member bends under load.

**Span Rating**: The maximum spacing between supports for structural panels.

**Delamination**: Separation of layers within engineered wood products.

## 6. WOOD DECK PREPARATION AND REPAIR

### 6.1 Panel Selection and Specifications
**Plywood Systems:**
- Minimum 15/32" CDX for structural applications
- Exterior grade adhesives
- APA performance ratings
- Span rating compliance

**OSB Systems:**
- Minimum 7/16" structural grade
- Moisture-resistant adhesives
- APA performance ratings
- Proper edge sealing

**Engineered Systems:**
- I-joist compatible panels
- Proper span ratings
- Edge support requirements
- Fastening specifications

### 6.2 Structural Repair Procedures
**Partial Panel Replacement:**
1. **Damage Assessment**
   - Mark damaged areas clearly
   - Determine repair boundaries
   - Check framing condition
   - Plan access requirements

2. **Removal Procedures**
   - Remove roofing materials carefully
   - Cut out damaged sections
   - Clean framing surfaces
   - Inspect for additional damage

3. **Framing Repair**
   - Replace damaged framing members
   - Add blocking where needed
   - Ensure proper bearing
   - Verify structural adequacy

4. **Panel Installation**
   - Cut panels to proper size
   - Install with proper gaps
   - Use specified fasteners
   - Maintain structural continuity

**Full Panel Replacement:**
- Remove entire panels carefully
- Check all framing members
- Install new panels properly
- Verify edge support

### 6.3 Fastening Requirements
**Fastener Selection:**
- Ring shank nails for high winds
- Structural screws for enhanced holding
- Corrosion-resistant fasteners
- Proper length specifications

**Fastening Patterns:**
1. **Standard Applications**
   - 6" o.c. at panel edges
   - 12" o.c. in field
   - Penetrate framing minimum 1-1/4"
   - Use proper fastener spacing

2. **High-Wind Applications**
   - 4" o.c. at panel edges
   - 6" o.c. in field
   - Ring shank nails required
   - Enhanced edge fastening

**Florida High-Wind Requirements:**
- Follow Miami-Dade approvals
- Use enhanced fastening schedules
- Install structural fasteners
- Provide continuous edge support

### 6.4 Panel Edge Treatment
**Edge Support:**
- Provide solid blocking
- Use panel clips where appropriate
- Maintain proper gaps
- Ensure structural continuity

**Gap Requirements:**
- 1/8" gap between panel ends
- 1/16" gap between panel edges
- Larger gaps in high humidity areas
- Account for thermal expansion

### 6.5 Surface Preparation
**Cleaning Requirements:**
- Remove all debris and dust
- Clean oil and contamination
- Repair surface defects
- Prime exposed wood

**Surface Smoothness:**
- Sand high spots
- Fill low areas
- Ensure uniform surface
- Check with straightedge

## 12. COMMON PROBLEMS AND SOLUTIONS

### 12.1 Installation Problems
**Inadequate Fastening:**
- Cause: Wrong fastener selection
- Solution: Replace with proper fasteners
- Prevention: Verify specifications

**Improper Gaps:**
- Cause: Inadequate planning
- Solution: Adjust panel spacing
- Prevention: Follow gap requirements

**Surface Irregularities:**
- Cause: Poor installation technique
- Solution: Sand and refinish
- Prevention: Use proper tools

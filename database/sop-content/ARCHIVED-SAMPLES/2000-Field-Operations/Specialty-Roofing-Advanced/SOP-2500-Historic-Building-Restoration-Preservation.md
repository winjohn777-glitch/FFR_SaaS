# SOP-2500: Historic Building Restoration and Preservation

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive historic building restoration and preservation procedures for Florida First Roofing LLC to provide specialized roofing services for historic properties, ensure compliance with preservation standards, maintain architectural integrity, and coordinate with preservation authorities and regulatory agencies.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all roofing work on properties listed on the National Register of Historic Places, local historic districts, structures over 50 years old requiring preservation consideration, and any building designated as having historic or architectural significance throughout Florida.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Historic Preservation Specialist**: Oversee all historic building projects and regulatory compliance
- **Restoration Project Manager**: Manage complex historic restoration projects and coordination
- **Historic Materials Specialist**: Source and specify authentic materials and techniques
- **Preservation Architect Liaison**: Coordinate with preservation architects and consultants
- **Regulatory Compliance Coordinator**: Manage permits and regulatory approval processes
- **Master Craftsperson**: Lead specialized restoration techniques and training

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Specialized Historic Materials:
- Authentic period roofing materials (slate, clay tiles, wood shingles, metal roofing)
- Traditional fasteners and hardware appropriate to historic periods
- Custom-fabricated materials matching original specifications and appearance
- Preservation-approved sealants and adhesives compatible with historic materials
- Specialty tools for working with historic materials and techniques

### Documentation and Analysis Equipment:
- High-resolution photography equipment for historic documentation
- 3D scanning equipment for precise historic element replication
- Materials analysis equipment for composition and condition assessment
- Environmental monitoring equipment for preservation condition assessment
- Archive-quality storage systems for historic material samples and documentation

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Assess historic materials for potential hazardous substances (lead, asbestos)
- Implement specialized safety protocols for working with fragile historic materials
- Coordinate with environmental specialists for hazardous material abatement
- Ensure structural integrity assessment before work on older buildings
- Maintain proper ventilation and contamination control during restoration work
- Implement fall protection appropriate for historic roof configurations

## 6. Pre-Operation Checklist
- [ ] Research property's historic designation and applicable preservation standards
- [ ] Coordinate with State Historic Preservation Office (SHPO) and local preservation authorities
- [ ] Obtain necessary permits and regulatory approvals for historic work
- [ ] Conduct comprehensive historic material analysis and documentation
- [ ] Coordinate with preservation architects and historic consultants
- [ ] Source authentic materials and verify preservation compliance
- [ ] Prepare detailed restoration specifications and methodology
- [ ] Document existing conditions with professional historic photography

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Historic Research and Documentation (Week 1-2)
1. **Comprehensive Historic Property Research**:
   - Research property history, architectural significance, and previous modifications
   - Review National Register nominations and historic survey documentation
   - Analyze original architectural drawings and specifications when available
   - Document current conditions and identify historic vs. non-historic elements
   - Coordinate with local historical societies and archives for additional documentation

2. **Regulatory Compliance and Permitting**:
   - **SHPO Coordination**: Coordinate with Florida State Historic Preservation Office for review requirements
   - **Local Historic Commission**: Present project plans to local historic preservation commissions
   - **Federal Tax Credit Compliance**: Ensure compliance with Historic Tax Credit program requirements if applicable
   - **Building Code Integration**: Balance historic preservation with current building code requirements
   - **Environmental Review**: Complete environmental review for federally funded or licensed projects

### Phase 2: Materials Analysis and Specification (Week 2-3)
1. **Historic Materials Assessment**:
   - Conduct detailed analysis of existing roofing materials and systems
   - Document construction techniques and fastening methods used in original installation
   - Assess condition and deterioration patterns of historic materials
   - Identify appropriate repair vs. replacement strategies for different elements
   - Coordinate with materials conservation specialists for complex analysis

2. **Authentic Materials Sourcing and Specification**:
   - **Period-Appropriate Materials**: Source materials matching original specifications and appearance
   - **Traditional Techniques**: Research and implement traditional installation and fabrication methods
   - **Custom Fabrication**: Coordinate custom fabrication of unavailable historic materials
   - **Preservation Standards**: Ensure all materials meet Secretary of Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation
   - **Compatibility Testing**: Verify chemical and physical compatibility with existing historic materials

### Phase 3: Preservation Planning and Design (Week 3-4)
1. **Restoration Methodology Development**:
   - Develop detailed restoration plans preserving maximum historic fabric
   - Design interventions that are reversible and compatible with historic materials
   - Plan phased restoration approach minimizing impact on building occupancy
   - Coordinate with preservation architects for design review and approval
   - Prepare specifications meeting preservation standards and building codes

2. **Craftsperson Training and Preparation**:
   - **Traditional Techniques Training**: Train crew in historic roofing installation techniques
   - **Materials Handling**: Provide specialized training for working with fragile historic materials
   - **Quality Standards**: Establish quality control measures appropriate for historic work
   - **Documentation Requirements**: Train team in required documentation and photography procedures
   - **Preservation Ethics**: Educate crew on preservation principles and historic building respect

### Phase 4: Restoration Implementation (Week 4-12)
1. **Historic Material Preservation and Repair**:
   - Carefully remove and catalog reusable historic materials for restoration
   - Implement conservation treatments for deteriorated but salvageable materials
   - Use traditional repair techniques and compatible materials for restoration
   - Document all intervention techniques and materials used for future reference
   - Coordinate with conservation specialists for complex material treatments

2. **Authentic Installation Techniques**:
   - **Traditional Fastening Methods**: Use period-appropriate fasteners and installation techniques
   - **Historic Detailing**: Recreate historic details and profiles using traditional methods
   - **Weather Protection**: Implement weather protection using reversible and compatible methods
   - **Structural Integration**: Integrate new structural elements with minimal impact on historic fabric
   - **Quality Control**: Maintain strict quality standards for historic accuracy and preservation compliance

### Phase 5: Documentation and Compliance Verification (Week 12-13)
1. **Comprehensive Project Documentation**:
   - Document all restoration work with professional photography and detailed descriptions
   - Prepare as-built drawings showing all modifications and interventions
   - Create maintenance manuals specific to historic materials and techniques
   - Document all materials sources and specifications for future maintenance
   - Prepare final reports for regulatory agencies and property owners

2. **Preservation Standards Compliance Verification**:
   - Coordinate final inspections with preservation authorities and regulatory agencies
   - Verify compliance with all applicable preservation standards and guidelines
   - Obtain necessary certificates of appropriateness and regulatory approvals
   - Complete Historic Tax Credit compliance documentation if applicable
   - Establish ongoing maintenance recommendations and preservation planning

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Historic accuracy: 95% authenticity rating from preservation authorities
- Preservation compliance: 100% compliance with applicable preservation standards
- Material authenticity: 90% use of authentic or historically accurate materials
- Craftsmanship quality: Master craftsperson level execution for all visible work
- Documentation completeness: 100% documentation meeting preservation standards

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Material unavailability**: Develop relationships with specialty suppliers and custom fabricators
- **Regulatory approval delays**: Maintain early coordination and detailed documentation procedures
- **Hidden condition discoveries**: Implement flexible project planning and preservation consultation
- **Code compliance conflicts**: Coordinate with building officials and preservation authorities for variance procedures
- **Weather protection needs**: Develop temporary protection systems compatible with historic materials

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Engage preservation consultants and specialists immediately for complex issues
2. Coordinate with regulatory authorities for guidance on standards interpretation
3. Escalate material sourcing issues to Historic Materials Specialist for alternative solutions
4. Document all discoveries and changes for regulatory approval and future reference
5. Maintain preservation principles while finding practical solutions to implementation challenges

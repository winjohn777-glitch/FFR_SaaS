# SOP-3021: Progressive Installation Inspection Procedures

## 1. Purpose
Establish systematic procedures for progressive quality inspections during roofing installation to identify and correct issues early, ensure continuous quality compliance, and prevent costly rework through proactive quality management.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all progressive inspection activities, quality checkpoints, and continuous monitoring procedures during roofing installation for residential and commercial projects throughout Florida First Roofing operations.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Quality Inspector**: Conduct progressive inspections and document findings
- **Installation Supervisor**: Coordinate inspections with installation progress
- **Lead Installer**: Support inspection activities and implement corrections
- **Project Manager**: Schedule inspections and manage quality requirements
- **Site Supervisor**: Ensure inspection access and crew coordination

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Inspection Tools:
- Digital measuring devices and precision levels
- Moisture detection equipment and infrared thermometers
- Digital cameras with macro lens capability for detail documentation
- Testing equipment for fastener pull-out and material verification
- Inspection mirrors and flashlights for hard-to-reach areas

### Documentation Equipment:
- Mobile tablets with inspection software and forms
- Real-time reporting systems and communication devices
- Barcode scanners for material tracking and verification
- GPS devices for location tagging and area mapping
- Weather monitoring equipment for environmental documentation

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Coordinate inspection activities with ongoing installation work
- Use appropriate fall protection during roof access and inspections
- Implement electrical safety for testing and measurement equipment
- Establish communication protocols between inspectors and crews
- Plan for emergency access and response during inspection activities
- Verify safe working conditions before conducting inspections

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Inspection Planning and Checkpoint Establishment
1. **Progressive Inspection Schedule Development**:
   - **Pre-Installation**: Substrate preparation and material staging
   - **Initial Installation**: Starter courses and foundation work
   - **Mid-Installation**: Field work and pattern establishment
   - **Detail Installation**: Flashing, penetrations, and trim work
   - **Pre-Final**: System completion and preliminary quality verification

2. **Quality Checkpoint Definition**:
   - **25% Completion**: Substrate preparation and initial installation quality
   - **50% Completion**: Field installation pattern and technique verification
   - **75% Completion**: Detail work and system integration quality
   - **90% Completion**: Final installation and cleanup preparation
   - **100% Completion**: Final quality verification and documentation

3. **Inspection Coordination and Communication**:
   - Schedule inspections to minimize work interruption
   - Coordinate with crew schedules and weather conditions
   - Establish communication protocols for immediate issue reporting
   - Plan for inspector access and safety requirements
   - Coordinate with other trades and site operations

### Phase 2: Progressive Inspection Execution
1. **Pre-Installation Substrate Inspection**:
   - **Deck Condition**: Structural integrity, fastening adequacy, moisture content
   - **Surface Preparation**: Cleanliness, repairs, and readiness for installation
   - **Material Staging**: Proper storage, protection, and organization
   - **Safety Setup**: Fall protection systems and safety equipment verification
   - **Environmental Conditions**: Weather suitability for installation commencement

2. **Initial Installation Quality Verification (0-25% Complete)**:
   - **Underlayment Installation**: Proper overlap, fastening, and weather protection
   - **Starter Course Quality**: Alignment, overhang, and fastening compliance
   - **Pattern Establishment**: Reference lines, layout accuracy, and consistency
   - **Flashing Preparation**: Base flashing installation and integration
   - **Quality Documentation**: Photographic record and compliance verification

3. **Field Installation Monitoring (25-75% Complete)**:
   - **Installation Technique**: Proper fastening, spacing, and pattern adherence
   - **Material Quality**: Condition verification and damage assessment
   - **Workmanship Standards**: Alignment, exposure consistency, and appearance
   - **Weather Protection**: Temporary protection and work area management
   - **Progress Quality**: Continuous improvement and technique refinement

### Phase 3: Critical Detail Inspection and Verification
1. **Detail Work Quality Assessment (75-90% Complete)**:
   - **Ridge and Hip Installation**: Alignment, fastening, and weatherproofing
   - **Penetration Details**: Flashing integration, sealing, and weather tightness
   - **Valley Installation**: Proper drainage, fastening, and weather protection
   - **Trim and Edge Work**: Alignment, fastening, and aesthetic quality
   - **Ventilation Systems**: Proper installation and integration

2. **System Integration and Performance Verification**:
   - **Water Management**: Drainage patterns and weather barrier continuity
   - **Wind Resistance**: Fastening compliance and uplift resistance
   - **Thermal Performance**: Insulation continuity and ventilation effectiveness
   - **Accessibility**: Maintenance access and safety considerations
   - **Code Compliance**: Building code requirements and permit compliance

3. **Pre-Final Quality Assessment (90-100% Complete)**:
   - **Overall Appearance**: Uniformity, alignment, and aesthetic quality
   - **System Completeness**: All components installed and functioning
   - **Cleanup Progress**: Debris removal and site preparation
   - **Documentation Preparation**: Warranty materials and certification readiness
   - **Customer Preparation**: Final inspection scheduling and communication

### Phase 4: Real-Time Documentation and Corrective Action
1. **Immediate Issue Identification and Documentation**:
   - **Digital Photography**: High-resolution images of defects and concerns
   - **Detailed Descriptions**: Specific location and nature of quality issues
   - **Severity Classification**: Critical, major, and minor defect categorization
   - **Correction Requirements**: Specific actions needed for resolution
   - **Timeline Establishment**: Deadlines for corrective action completion

2. **Real-Time Communication and Coordination**:
   - **Immediate Notification**: Real-time communication of critical issues
   - **Crew Coordination**: Direct communication with installation personnel
   - **Supervisor Notification**: Management notification of significant issues
   - **Customer Communication**: Proactive communication of quality measures
   - **Resolution Tracking**: Progress monitoring for corrective actions

3. **Corrective Action Implementation and Verification**:
   - **Work Stoppage**: Halt installation in affected areas when necessary
   - **Correction Planning**: Develop specific corrective action procedures
   - **Resource Allocation**: Assign personnel and materials for corrections
   - **Re-inspection**: Verify correction completion and quality compliance
   - **Documentation Update**: Record corrective actions and final status

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Inspection frequency: Minimum four progressive inspections per project
- Response time: Immediate notification of critical quality issues
- Documentation completeness: 100% digital documentation of all inspections
- Correction timeline: Critical issues corrected within 24 hours
- Communication effectiveness: Real-time notification and coordination

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Progressive Inspection Issues:
- **Schedule conflicts**: Coordinate alternative inspection timing and access
- **Weather delays**: Implement weather protection and adjust schedule
- **Access problems**: Plan alternative inspection methods and safety measures
- **Equipment failures**: Use backup inspection equipment and manual procedures
- **Communication issues**: Establish backup communication protocols

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document inspection issues and impact on quality verification
2. Implement alternative inspection methods when standard procedures fail
3. Coordinate with project management for schedule and resource adjustments
4. Maintain quality standards despite inspection challenges
5. Update procedures to prevent similar issues in future projects

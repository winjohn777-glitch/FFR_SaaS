# SOP-5011: Accounts Receivable Management and Collection

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for managing accounts receivable and collection activities to optimize cash flow, minimize bad debt losses, maintain positive customer relationships, and ensure financial stability for Florida First Roofing LLC operations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in billing, accounts receivable management, and collection activities, including accounting staff, customer service representatives, project managers, and management personnel responsible for credit decisions and collection procedures.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Accounts Receivable Manager**: Oversee all receivables management and collection strategies
- **Billing Specialists**: Generate accurate invoices and manage billing cycle procedures
- **Collections Coordinator**: Execute collection procedures and maintain customer payment communications
- **Credit Manager**: Evaluate customer creditworthiness and establish payment terms
- **Customer Service Representatives**: Handle payment inquiries and resolve billing disputes
- **Project Managers**: Provide project completion documentation for final billing
- **Legal Counsel**: Handle complex collection issues and legal action procedures

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Accounting Systems:
- Accounts receivable management software with automated aging reports
- Customer credit evaluation and monitoring systems
- Electronic invoicing and payment processing platforms
- Collection management software with communication tracking
- Financial reporting and cash flow analysis tools

### Communication Tools:
- Customer contact management systems with payment history tracking
- Automated payment reminder and notification systems
- Document management for contracts, liens, and legal correspondence
- Credit bureau reporting and monitoring services
- Payment processing and merchant services platforms

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Ensure compliance with Florida debt collection laws and federal regulations
- Maintain customer privacy and confidentiality during collection activities
- Document all collection communications for legal protection
- Follow proper procedures for mechanic's lien filings and enforcement
- Protect sensitive financial information and customer payment data

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Credit Evaluation and Payment Terms Establishment
1. **Customer Credit Assessment**:
   - **Credit Application Review**: Evaluate financial statements, trade references, and payment history
   - **Credit Bureau Reports**: Obtain and analyze credit scores and payment patterns
   - **Financial Capacity Analysis**: Assess ability to pay based on project size and customer resources
   - **Reference Verification**: Contact trade references and previous contractors for payment history
   - **Risk Assessment**: Determine appropriate credit limits and payment terms based on evaluation

2. **Payment Terms and Contract Provisions**:
   - **Payment Schedule Development**: Establish progress payment milestones tied to project completion
   - **Contract Terms Specification**: Include clear payment terms, late fees, and collection procedures
   - **Lien Rights Preservation**: Ensure proper notices and documentation for mechanic's lien protection
   - **Interest and Late Fee Policies**: Establish rates and procedures compliant with Florida law
   - **Collection Cost Provisions**: Include attorney fees and collection costs in contract terms

3. **Credit Limit and Monitoring Setup**:
   - Establish appropriate credit limits based on customer evaluation and project requirements
   - Setup automated monitoring for credit limit compliance and payment performance
   - Create customer risk profiles with appropriate collection and communication strategies
   - Implement early warning systems for credit deterioration and payment issues
   - Establish approval procedures for credit limit increases and payment term modifications

### Phase 2: Invoicing and Billing Procedures
1. **Invoice Generation and Documentation**:
   - **Progress Billing**: Generate invoices based on completed work and approved progress reports
   - **Final Billing**: Complete final invoices upon project completion and customer acceptance
   - **Supporting Documentation**: Include detailed work descriptions, material lists, and change orders
   - **Lien Notice Compliance**: Include required Florida mechanic's lien notices on invoices
   - **Accuracy Verification**: Review invoices for mathematical accuracy and completeness before sending

2. **Invoice Delivery and Communication**:
   - **Electronic Delivery**: Email invoices with delivery confirmation and read receipts
   - **Physical Delivery**: Mail hard copies when required by contract or customer preference
   - **Customer Portal Access**: Provide online access to invoices and payment history
   - **Invoice Explanation**: Offer detailed explanations of charges and payment instructions
   - **Payment Options**: Clearly communicate available payment methods and processing procedures

3. **Billing Cycle Management**:
   - Establish consistent billing cycles and invoice generation schedules
   - Monitor invoice accuracy and completeness before delivery to customers
   - Track invoice delivery and customer receipt confirmation
   - Maintain detailed records of all billing activities and customer communications
   - Coordinate with project managers to ensure timely and accurate billing information

### Phase 3: Payment Monitoring and Collection Procedures
1. **Payment Tracking and Aging Analysis**:
   - **Daily Payment Processing**: Record and apply payments promptly and accurately
   - **Aging Report Generation**: Produce weekly aging reports showing payment status by customer
   - **Past Due Identification**: Identify accounts requiring collection attention and action
   - **Payment Pattern Analysis**: Monitor customer payment trends and potential issues
   - **Cash Flow Reporting**: Provide management with current receivables status and projections

2. **Collection Communication Strategy**:
   - **Initial Reminders**: Send friendly payment reminders 5-10 days after due date
   - **Follow-up Communications**: Escalate communication frequency and urgency for older accounts
   - **Phone Contact**: Make personal contact for accounts 30+ days past due
   - **Written Notices**: Send formal collection letters for accounts 60+ days past due
   - **Final Demands**: Issue final demand letters before legal action consideration

3. **Collection Escalation Procedures**:
   - **Management Involvement**: Escalate significant accounts to management for resolution
   - **Payment Plan Negotiation**: Offer structured payment plans for customers experiencing difficulties
   - **Legal Action Preparation**: Prepare documentation for potential legal collection procedures
   - **Mechanic's Lien Filing**: File liens when appropriate to secure payment rights
   - **Credit Reporting**: Report delinquent accounts to credit bureaus when appropriate

### Phase 4: Advanced Collection and Legal Procedures
1. **Mechanic's Lien Process**:
   - **Preliminary Notice**: Send required notices within Florida statutory timeframes
   - **Lien Filing**: File mechanic's liens for unpaid amounts within legal deadlines
   - **Lien Enforcement**: Pursue foreclosure proceedings when necessary and cost-effective
   - **Settlement Negotiation**: Negotiate settlements while maintaining lien rights
   - **Lien Release**: Properly release liens upon payment or settlement

2. **Legal Collection Actions**:
   - **Attorney Referral**: Refer accounts to collection attorneys for legal action
   - **Litigation Decision**: Evaluate cost-effectiveness of pursuing legal collection
   - **Judgment Collection**: Pursue asset identification and garnishment procedures
   - **Settlement Evaluation**: Consider settlement offers and cost-benefit analysis
   - **Bankruptcy Monitoring**: Monitor customer bankruptcy filings and file appropriate claims

3. **Alternative Collection Methods**:
   - **Collection Agency Placement**: Place accounts with professional collection agencies
   - **Factoring Consideration**: Evaluate factoring options for immediate cash flow needs
   - **Trade Credit Insurance**: Consider credit insurance for large customer exposures
   - **Customer Payment Plans**: Negotiate extended payment terms to facilitate collection
   - **Partial Payment Acceptance**: Consider partial payments and account resolution strategies

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Collection effectiveness: 95% of accounts collected within 90 days of due date
- Bad debt percentage: Less than 2% of total sales written off as uncollectible
- Invoice accuracy: 98% of invoices generated without errors requiring correction
- Days sales outstanding: Maintain average DSO of 45 days or less
- Customer satisfaction: 4.0/5 rating for billing and collection process fairness

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Billing disputes**: Establish clear dispute resolution procedures and documentation requirements
- **Payment delays**: Implement proactive communication and payment plan options
- **Documentation problems**: Enhance record keeping and project documentation procedures
- **Customer financial difficulties**: Develop payment assistance and workout procedures
- **Legal compliance issues**: Ensure adherence to Florida debt collection and construction lien laws

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Investigate billing disputes promptly and provide detailed documentation
2. Offer flexible payment arrangements for customers experiencing temporary difficulties
3. Enhance communication with project managers for accurate billing information
4. Seek legal counsel for complex collection issues and compliance questions
5. Implement technology solutions to improve billing accuracy and collection efficiency

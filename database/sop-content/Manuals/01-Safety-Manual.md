## 3. FALL PROTECTION SYSTEMS {#fall-protection}

### 3.1 Fall Protection Requirements

#### OSHA Compliance Standards
- **Fall Protection Required**: 6 feet and above for all personnel
- **Inspection Requirements**: Daily equipment inspection mandatory
- **Training Certification**: Annual fall protection training required
- **Documentation**: Complete inspection and training records

#### Fall Protection Hierarchy
1. **Elimination**: Remove fall hazards when possible
2. **Passive Systems**: Guardrails and safety nets
3. **Personal Fall Arrest**: Harnesses and lifelines
4. **Positioning Systems**: Work positioning equipment
5. **Administrative Controls**: Training and procedures

### 3.2 Personal Fall Arrest Systems

#### Equipment Requirements
- **Full Body Harness**: ANSI Z359 certified
- **Shock Absorbing Lanyards**: Energy absorption capability
- **Anchorage Points**: 5,000 lb minimum strength
- **Connectors**: Self-locking snap hooks required

#### Inspection Procedures
- **Daily Pre-use**: Visual and tactile inspection
- **Documented Inspection**: Weekly formal inspection
- **Annual Certification**: Professional equipment inspection
- **Immediate Removal**: Damaged equipment quarantine

### 3.3 Roof-Specific Fall Protection

#### Residential Roofing
- **Safety Harness**: Required on all pitched roofs
- **Roof Brackets**: Installed every 8 feet maximum
- **Safety Lines**: Properly anchored horizontal lifelines
- **Edge Protection**: Temporary guardrails where applicable

#### Commercial Roofing
- **Perimeter Protection**: Guardrail systems required
- **Roof Openings**: Covers or guardrail protection
- **Low-slope Requirements**: 15-foot rule compliance
- **Access Controls**: Controlled access zones where applicable

**Related SOPs**: SOP-0401, SOP-0411, SOP-0412

- **Side Shields**: Required for impact protection
- **Face Shields**: Required for grinding operations
- **Prescription Safety**: Available for prescription wearers

#### Hand Protection
- **Work Gloves**: Cut-resistant, grip-enhanced
- **Electrical Gloves**: Required for electrical work
- **Chemical Gloves**: Required for sealant/chemical handling
- **Specialized Gloves**: Task-specific protection

#### Foot Protection
- **Safety Boots**: ANSI Z41 steel toe minimum

## 5. LADDER AND SCAFFOLD SAFETY {#ladder-safety}

### 5.1 Ladder Safety Requirements

#### Ladder Selection and Inspection
- **Load Rating**: Minimum Type I (250 lb) industrial rating
- **Length Requirements**: Extend 3 feet above landing point
- **Daily Inspection**: Check for damage, wear, and function
- **Setup Requirements**: 4:1 ratio for lean angle

#### Ladder Safety Procedures
- **Three-Point Contact**: Maintain at all times
- **Weight Limits**: Never exceed manufacturer ratings
- **Weather Conditions**: No use during wind/rain
- **Electrical Safety**: Non-conductive ladders near electrical

### 5.2 Scaffold Safety Systems

#### Scaffold Requirements
- **Competent Person**: Required for assembly/disassembly
- **Inspection**: Daily before use by competent person
- **Fall Protection**: Required above 10 feet
- **Access**: Safe means of access/egress required

#### Scaffold Components
- **Planking**: Full planking, no gaps over 1 inch
- **Guardrails**: Top rail, mid rail, and toe boards
- **Base Plates**: Stable, level foundation required
- **Ties**: Secured to building every 26 feet vertically

### 5.3 Aerial Lift Safety

#### Operator Requirements
- **Training Certification**: Required before operation
- **Daily Inspection**: Pre-use safety inspection
- **Fall Protection**: Full body harness required
- **Communication**: Ground spotter required

#### Operational Safety
- **Setup Procedures**: Level, stable positioning
- **Electrical Hazards**: Maintain safe distances
- **Weather Limitations**: Wind and storm restrictions
- **Load Limits**: Never exceed manufacturer specifications

**Related SOPs**: SOP-0403, SOP-0411

## 7. ENVIRONMENTAL SAFETY {#environmental-safety}

### 7.1 Florida Weather Safety

#### Heat Stress Prevention
- **Temperature Monitoring**: Track heat index hourly
- **Work Schedules**: Adjust for extreme heat
- **Hydration**: Mandatory water breaks every 30 minutes
- **Recognition**: Early heat illness identification

#### Lightning Safety
- **Weather Monitoring**: Track approaching storms
- **30-30 Rule**: Seek shelter when thunder within 30 seconds
- **Safe Shelter**: Substantial buildings or vehicles
- **Work Resumption**: 30 minutes after last thunder

#### Hurricane and Storm Safety
- **Seasonal Preparedness**: Pre-season safety planning
- **Work Stoppage**: Immediate when wind exceeds 25 mph
- **Securing**: Secure all tools and materials
- **Evacuation**: Clear evacuation procedures

### 7.2 Electrical Safety

#### Electrical Hazard Recognition
- **Overhead Lines**: Maintain 10-foot minimum clearance
- **Electrical Equipment**: Lock out/tag out procedures
- **Wet Conditions**: No electrical work in wet conditions
- **Arc Flash**: Proper PPE for electrical work

#### Electrical Safety Procedures
- **Qualified Persons**: Only qualified personnel on electrical
- **Testing**: Verify circuits are de-energized
- **GFCI Protection**: Required for all portable tools
- **Emergency Response**: Electrical shock response procedures

### 7.3 Chemical and Material Safety

#### Hazardous Materials
- **Safety Data Sheets**: Available for all chemicals
- **Proper Storage**: Segregated, secure storage
- **Personal Protection**: Appropriate PPE required
- **Spill Response**: Emergency spill procedures

#### Asbestos and Lead Safety
- **Pre-1980 Buildings**: Assume presence of hazardous materials
- **Testing Requirements**: Professional testing before disturbance
- **Abatement Procedures**: Certified abatement contractors
- **Worker Protection**: Full respiratory protection

**Related SOPs**: SOP-0405, SOP-0406, SOP-0605

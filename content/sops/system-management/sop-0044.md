# SOP-0044: Competency Testing and Assessment Procedures

**FLORIDA FIRST ROOFING LLC**
**Standard Operating Procedure**

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

**Purpose:** This SOP establishes standardized procedures for developing, implementing, administering, and evaluating competency tests and assessments within the Learning Management System (LMS) to ensure Florida First Roofing LLC employees and subcontractors demonstrate required knowledge, skills, and abilities for safe and effective job performance.

**Scope:** This procedure applies to all competency assessments including safety knowledge tests, technical skill evaluations, certification examinations, performance assessments, and compliance verification testing across all job roles and organizational levels.

## 3. DEFINITIONS

- **Competency:** Demonstrated ability to perform job tasks safely and effectively
- **Assessment:** Systematic evaluation of knowledge, skills, and abilities
- **Formative Assessment:** Ongoing evaluation during learning process
- **Summative Assessment:** Final evaluation of learning outcomes and competency
- **Performance-Based Assessment:** Evaluation through demonstration of actual job tasks
- **Adaptive Testing:** Computer-based assessment that adjusts difficulty based on responses
- **Cut Score:** Minimum score required to demonstrate competency
- **Item Analysis:** Statistical evaluation of test question effectiveness

---

## 4. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**Training Manager:**
- Oversee assessment strategy and competency framework development
- Approve assessment instruments and cut score requirements
- Ensure regulatory compliance and industry standard alignment
- Coordinate with SMEs on assessment content and validation
- Monitor assessment effectiveness and continuous improvement

**Assessment Developer:**
- Design and develop competency-based assessment instruments
- Create test questions, scenarios, and performance evaluations
- Ensure assessment validity, reliability, and fairness
- Implement accessibility accommodations and universal design
- Maintain assessment security and integrity protocols

**Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):**
- Validate assessment content for technical accuracy and relevance
- Define competency standards and performance criteria
- Review and approve assessment instruments and scoring rubrics
- Provide input on industry best practices and standards
- Participate in cut score setting and validation studies

**Test Administrators:**
- Schedule and administer assessments according to protocols
- Ensure secure testing environment and procedures
- Monitor test sessions and maintain assessment integrity
- Provide technical support and accommodation assistance
- Document assessment sessions and incident reports

**Supervisors:**
- Identify employees requiring competency assessment
- Schedule assessment sessions with minimal operational disruption
- Review assessment results and performance reports
- Provide coaching and remediation support as needed
- Ensure compliance with assessment and certification requirements

## 6. PROCEDURES

### A. Competency Framework Development

1. **Job Analysis and Competency Identification:**
   - Conduct systematic job analysis for each role
   - Identify critical knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs)
   - Define performance standards and criteria
   - Validate competencies with SMEs and industry standards
   - Document competency framework and requirements

2. **Competency Mapping:**
   - Align competencies with training curricula and learning objectives
   - Map competencies to job tasks and performance requirements
   - Identify prerequisite competencies and progression pathways
   - Cross-reference with regulatory and certification requirements
   - Create competency matrices for different roles and levels

3. **Performance Standards Setting:**
   - Define acceptable performance levels and criteria
   - Establish cut scores and passing requirements
   - Specify assessment frequency and renewal requirements
   - Document competency maintenance and update procedures
   - Align standards with industry benchmarks and best practices

### B. Assessment Design and Development

1. **Assessment Planning:**
   - Select appropriate assessment methods and formats
   - Determine test length, timing, and administration parameters
   - Plan for accessibility accommodations and universal design
   - Design scoring rubrics and evaluation criteria
   - Establish security and integrity requirements

2. **Test Item Development:**
   - Create multiple-choice, scenario-based, and performance items
   - Develop realistic job simulations and case studies
   - Design hands-on practical assessments and demonstrations
   - Ensure item alignment with learning objectives and competencies
   - Implement item review and validation procedures

3. **Assessment Validation:**
   - Conduct content validity review with SMEs
   - Pilot test assessments with representative samples
   - Analyze item statistics and performance data
   - Validate assessment reliability and consistency
   - Document validation evidence and justification

### C. Assessment Administration

1. **Pre-Assessment Preparation:**
   - Schedule assessment sessions and notify participants
   - Prepare testing environment and equipment
   - Verify participant eligibility and prerequisites
   - Review accommodation requests and arrangements
   - Brief test administrators on procedures and protocols

2. **Assessment Session Management:**
   - Verify participant identity and registration
   - Explain assessment procedures and expectations
   - Monitor session for security and integrity violations
   - Provide technical support and assistance as needed
   - Document session events and any incidents

3. **Security and Integrity Measures:**
   - Implement secure testing environment protocols
   - Monitor for cheating, misconduct, and security breaches
   - Use randomized question pools and item banks
   - Control access to assessment materials and content
   - Maintain chain of custody for assessment records

### D. Scoring and Results Management

1. **Automated Scoring:**
   - Configure LMS scoring algorithms and parameters
   - Validate automated scoring accuracy and reliability
   - Implement immediate feedback and results delivery
   - Generate detailed performance reports and analytics
   - Ensure secure storage and transmission of results

2. **Performance-Based Scoring:**
   - Train assessors on scoring rubrics and criteria
   - Implement multiple-rater reliability procedures
   - Conduct calibration sessions and inter-rater agreement
   - Document scoring decisions and justifications
   - Review and quality-check scoring accuracy

3. **Results Reporting and Communication:**
   - Generate individual performance reports and feedback
   - Provide detailed competency gap analysis
   - Create aggregate reports for supervisors and managers
   - Communicate results to relevant stakeholders
   - Document results in training records and systems

### E. Remediation and Follow-Up

1. **Performance Gap Analysis:**
   - Identify specific competency deficiencies and weaknesses
   - Analyze patterns and trends in assessment performance
   - Determine root causes of performance issues
   - Prioritize remediation needs and interventions
   - Develop individualized improvement plans

2. **Remediation Planning:**
   - Design targeted training and development interventions
   - Select appropriate remediation methods and resources
   - Schedule remediation activities and timelines
   - Assign coaches, mentors, and support personnel
   - Monitor remediation progress and effectiveness

3. **Reassessment Procedures:**
   - Establish reassessment eligibility and timing requirements
   - Implement parallel assessment forms and alternate versions
   - Monitor reassessment performance and improvement
   - Document reassessment results and outcomes
   - Update competency records and certification status

# SOP-5000: Employee Recruitment and Hiring Procedures

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for recruiting, screening, and hiring qualified employees to ensure consistent hiring practices, legal compliance, and successful integration of new team members into Florida First Roofing LLC operations.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in recruitment and hiring activities, including human resources staff, department managers, supervisors, and executives participating in the employee selection process for all positions within the company.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Human Resources Manager**: Oversee recruitment process and ensure legal compliance
- **Department Managers**: Define position requirements and participate in candidate evaluation
- **Hiring Supervisors**: Conduct interviews and make hiring recommendations
- **Executive Team**: Approve hiring decisions and compensation packages
- **Administrative Staff**: Coordinate scheduling and documentation processes

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Recruitment Tools:
- Job posting platforms and applicant tracking systems
- Standard interview guides and evaluation forms
- Background check and reference verification services
- Drug testing and medical examination coordination
- Employment application and documentation systems

### Documentation Systems:
- Employee file management and confidential storage

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Verify all safety training requirements for specific positions
- Ensure new employees understand company safety policies and procedures
- Confirm physical capabilities match job requirements and safety demands
- Implement workplace safety orientation for all new hires
- Maintain confidentiality of medical and personal information

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Position Definition and Job Analysis
1. **Job Requirements Analysis**:
   - Work with department managers to define specific job duties and responsibilities
   - Identify required skills, experience, and educational qualifications
   - Determine physical requirements and working conditions
   - Establish performance expectations and success metrics
   - Define reporting relationships and team interaction requirements

2. **Compensation and Benefits Package Development**:
   - Research market rates for comparable positions in the roofing industry
   - Determine salary range, overtime eligibility, and commission structures
   - Define benefits package including health insurance, retirement, and time off
   - Consider performance incentives and career advancement opportunities
   - Obtain approval for compensation package from executive team

3. **Legal Compliance Review**:
   - Ensure job requirements comply with ADA and equal employment opportunity laws
   - Review for any discriminatory language or requirements
   - Verify compliance with Florida employment law requirements
   - Check for proper classification as exempt or non-exempt under FLSA
   - Confirm background check and drug testing requirements are job-related

### Phase 2: Recruitment Strategy and Candidate Sourcing
1. **Recruitment Channel Selection**:
   - **Industry-Specific Sources**: Roofing trade publications, associations, and specialty job boards
   - **General Job Boards**: Indeed, LinkedIn, and regional employment websites
   - **Educational Institutions**: Community colleges, trade schools, and apprenticeship programs
   - **Employee Referrals**: Internal referral programs with incentive structures
   - **Professional Networks**: Industry contacts and professional associations

2. **Job Posting Development and Distribution**:
   - Create compelling job descriptions highlighting company culture and opportunities
   - Include specific qualifications and requirements clearly stated
   - Post on selected recruitment channels with appropriate timing
   - Monitor application flow and adjust recruitment strategy as needed
   - Track source effectiveness for future recruitment planning

3. **Initial Candidate Screening**:
   - Review applications for minimum qualifications and requirements
   - Conduct phone screenings to assess communication skills and basic fit
   - Verify employment eligibility and availability for required work schedule
   - Screen for safety consciousness and commitment to quality work
   - Schedule qualified candidates for in-person interviews

### Phase 3: Interview Process and Candidate Evaluation
1. **Interview Structure and Coordination**:
   - **Initial Interview**: HR screening for basic qualifications and cultural fit
   - **Technical Interview**: Department manager assessment of skills and experience
   - **Panel Interview**: Multiple stakeholders for key positions
   - **Practical Assessment**: Hands-on evaluation of technical skills when applicable
   - **Final Interview**: Executive approval for management and specialized positions

2. **Interview Content and Evaluation Criteria**:
   - Behavioral interview questions to assess past performance and decision-making
   - Technical questions specific to roofing industry knowledge and skills
   - Safety awareness and commitment evaluation
   - Customer service orientation and communication skills assessment
   - Cultural fit and alignment with company values and work environment

3. **Reference and Background Verification**:
   - Contact previous employers to verify employment history and performance
   - Check professional references for character and work quality assessment
   - Conduct criminal background checks appropriate for position requirements
   - Verify educational credentials and professional certifications
   - Complete drug testing and physical examinations when required

### Phase 4: Hiring Decision and Onboarding Preparation
1. **Candidate Evaluation and Selection**:
   - Compile interview feedback and assessment results from all participants
   - Compare candidates against established criteria and job requirements
   - Consider diversity and inclusion goals in final selection process
   - Make hiring recommendation with supporting documentation
   - Obtain final approval from appropriate management level

2. **Offer Development and Negotiation**:
   - Prepare formal job offer including all compensation and benefits details
   - Include start date, reporting relationships, and initial training requirements
   - Present offer verbally and follow up with written confirmation
   - Negotiate terms within approved parameters when appropriate
   - Obtain signed offer acceptance and employment agreement

3. **Pre-Employment Requirements Completion**:
   - Complete I-9 employment eligibility verification with required documentation
   - Collect tax withholding forms and direct deposit information
   - Enroll new employee in benefits programs and insurance coverage
   - Order necessary equipment, uniforms, and safety gear
   - Prepare workspace and access credentials for first day

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Time to fill positions: Average 30 days from posting to start date
- Candidate quality: 90% of hires successfully complete probationary period
- Legal compliance: 100% compliance with employment law requirements
- New hire satisfaction: 4.5/5 rating for hiring process experience
- Retention rate: 80% of new hires remain employed after one year

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Low application volume**: Expand recruitment channels and improve job posting content
- **Unqualified candidates**: Refine job requirements and screening criteria
- **Lengthy hiring process**: Streamline interview coordination and decision-making
- **Competitive offers**: Review compensation packages and value proposition
- **Background check issues**: Develop clear policies for handling adverse findings

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Analyze recruitment data to identify process bottlenecks or issues
2. Adjust recruitment strategy and channels based on effectiveness data
3. Provide additional training for hiring managers on interview techniques
4. Review and update compensation packages to maintain competitiveness
5. Seek legal counsel for complex employment law or discrimination issues

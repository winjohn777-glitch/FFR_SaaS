# SOP-3001: Inspection Schedule Development and Management

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for developing, managing, and executing inspection schedules to ensure quality control, regulatory compliance, and customer satisfaction throughout all phases of roofing projects.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in quality control activities, including project managers, quality inspectors, site supervisors, and administrative staff coordinating inspection activities for residential and commercial roofing projects.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Quality Control Manager**: Oversee inspection program development and compliance
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate project-specific inspection schedules and requirements
- **Lead Inspector**: Execute inspections and maintain quality standards
- **Site Supervisor**: Prepare work areas and coordinate inspection readiness
- **Scheduler**: Manage inspection calendars and stakeholder coordination

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Inspection Tools:
- Digital inspection forms and checklists
- Measuring devices and testing equipment
- Photographic documentation equipment
- Weather monitoring and tracking systems
- Communication devices for real-time coordination

### Scheduling Systems:
- Project management software with inspection tracking
- Calendar systems with automated reminders and notifications
- Mobile applications for field schedule updates
- Integration platforms for stakeholder communication

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Ensure all inspections comply with site safety protocols
- Coordinate inspection timing with safe work conditions
- Verify inspector safety training and equipment requirements
- Maintain safe access for inspection activities
- Document any safety issues discovered during inspections

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Inspection Requirements Analysis
1. **Project-Specific Inspection Planning**:
   - Review contract specifications and quality requirements
   - Identify all required inspection points and milestones
   - Coordinate with building department inspection schedules
   - Determine customer inspection preferences and requirements
   - Plan for manufacturer or warranty inspection requirements

2. **Regulatory and Code Compliance Integration**:
   - **Building Department Inspections**: Coordinate required municipal inspections
   - **Insurance Inspections**: Schedule carrier-required inspections
   - **Manufacturer Inspections**: Arrange warranty compliance inspections
   - **Internal Quality Inspections**: Plan company quality control points
   - **Final Inspections**: Coordinate customer walkthrough and approval

3. **Resource and Timeline Coordination**:
   - Assess inspector availability and qualification requirements
   - Coordinate with project timeline and critical path activities
   - Plan for weather dependencies and seasonal considerations
   - Integrate with material delivery and installation schedules
   - Account for potential re-inspection and correction time

### Phase 2: Schedule Development and Communication
1. **Master Schedule Creation**:
   - Develop comprehensive inspection calendar with all required inspections
   - Assign specific inspectors based on qualifications and availability
   - Coordinate with project milestones and completion targets
   - Build in contingency time for weather delays and corrections
   - Integrate with overall project management and resource planning

2. **Stakeholder Notification and Coordination**:
   - Notify building departments of inspection timing requirements
   - Communicate with customers regarding inspection schedules
   - Coordinate with subcontractors and specialty installers
   - Schedule manufacturer representatives for warranty inspections
   - Arrange insurance adjuster or carrier inspection timing

3. **Documentation and Tracking Systems**:
   - Create inspection tracking spreadsheets and databases
   - Set up automated reminder systems for all participants
   - Establish communication protocols for schedule changes
   - Implement real-time status updates and progress tracking
   - Prepare backup scheduling for weather or emergency situations

### Phase 3: Inspection Execution and Management
1. **Pre-Inspection Preparation**:
   - Verify work area readiness and quality standards
   - Confirm inspector availability and equipment preparation
   - Check weather conditions and safety requirements
   - Coordinate site access and stakeholder participation
   - Review inspection criteria and documentation requirements

2. **Inspection Coordination and Communication**:
   - Provide 24-hour advance notification to all participants
   - Confirm inspector credentials and qualification requirements
   - Coordinate site access and safety briefing for inspectors
   - Manage inspection process and address questions or concerns
   - Document inspection results and any deficiencies identified

3. **Post-Inspection Follow-up**:
   - Review inspection results and approval status
   - Coordinate correction of any deficiencies or issues identified
   - Schedule re-inspections as required for compliance
   - Update project status and communicate results to stakeholders
   - Document lessons learned and process improvements

### Phase 4: Schedule Monitoring and Adjustment
1. **Progress Tracking and Status Updates**:
   - Monitor inspection completion against planned schedules
   - Track any delays or schedule modifications required
   - Communicate status updates to project teams and customers
   - Identify potential conflicts or resource constraints
   - Implement corrective actions for schedule deviations

2. **Schedule Optimization and Efficiency Improvement**:
   - Analyze inspection scheduling effectiveness and efficiency
   - Identify opportunities for schedule compression or optimization
   - Coordinate with other project activities for maximum efficiency
   - Implement best practices for future schedule development
   - Document successful scheduling strategies and lessons learned

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Schedule accuracy: 95% on-time inspection completion rate
- Communication effectiveness: 100% stakeholder notification compliance
- Inspection pass rate: 90% first-time inspection approval rate
- Documentation completeness: 100% inspection records maintained
- Customer satisfaction: 95% approval rating for inspection coordination

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Weather delays**: Implement flexible scheduling and backup dates
- **Inspector availability**: Maintain backup inspector qualification lists
- **Work readiness delays**: Coordinate with site supervision for preparation
- **Stakeholder conflicts**: Provide multiple scheduling options and coordination
- **Failed inspections**: Implement rapid correction and re-inspection procedures

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Identify scheduling conflict or issue immediately
2. Assess impact on project timeline and critical path
3. Coordinate alternative scheduling options with all stakeholders
4. Implement schedule adjustments and communicate changes
5. Document resolution and update procedures to prevent recurrence

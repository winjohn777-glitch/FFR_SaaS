# SOP-2001: Permit Application and Building Department Coordination

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for obtaining building permits, coordinating with local building departments, and ensuring regulatory compliance for all roofing projects throughout Florida counties and municipalities.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all project managers, office administrators, and field supervisors involved in permit application processes, building department interactions, and regulatory compliance activities for residential and commercial roofing projects.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Oversee permit process and building department coordination
- **Permit Coordinator**: Execute permit applications and track approval status
- **Office Administrator**: Maintain permit documentation and filing systems
- **Site Supervisor**: Coordinate inspections and ensure field compliance
- **Compliance Officer**: Monitor regulatory changes and update procedures

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Documentation Systems:
- Digital permit application software and portals
- Project drawings and specifications database
- Contractor license and insurance documentation
- Customer authorization and contract documents
- Building code reference materials and updates

### Communication Tools:
- Direct contact database for building department personnel
- Electronic submission systems and tracking portals
- Mobile devices for field coordination and updates

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Ensure all permit requirements include appropriate safety specifications
- Verify code compliance for fall protection and safety systems
- Coordinate permit conditions with site safety plans
- Address any special safety requirements in permit applications
- Maintain current knowledge of safety-related code changes

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Permit Requirement Assessment
1. **Project Analysis and Classification**:
   - **Repair Work**: Determine if permit required based on scope and value
   - **Re-roofing**: Identify structural work and system upgrades
   - **New Construction**: Coordinate with general contractor permits
   - **Commercial Projects**: Review complex permitting requirements
   - **Historic Properties**: Address special preservation requirements

2. **Jurisdiction Identification**:
   - Verify property location and applicable building department
   - Confirm incorporated vs. unincorporated area jurisdiction
   - Identify any overlay districts or special requirements
   - Check for homeowners association or covenant restrictions
   - Research any pending code changes or special conditions

3. **Code Requirements Research**:
   - Review current Florida Building Code applicable sections
   - Check local amendments and jurisdiction-specific requirements
   - Identify wind load and hurricane protection requirements
   - Verify energy code compliance for new installations
   - Research accessibility requirements for commercial projects

### Phase 2: Documentation Preparation
1. **Required Document Assembly**:
   - **Permit Application**: Complete all required fields accurately
   - **Site Plan**: Show building footprint and property boundaries
   - **Roof Plan**: Detail roof layout, materials, and drainage
   - **Construction Details**: Provide installation specifications
   - **Product Documentation**: Submit manufacturer specifications

2. **Professional Certifications**:
   - Engineer stamped drawings when required by code
   - Architect involvement for commercial or complex residential
   - Energy code compliance calculations and documentation
   - Structural analysis for significant roof modifications
   - Wind resistance calculations and uplift specifications

3. **Contractor Documentation**:
   - Current roofing contractor license verification
   - General liability and workers compensation certificates
   - Bonding documentation when required
   - Previous inspection history and compliance record
   - Specialized certifications for specific products or systems

### Phase 3: Application Submission and Tracking
1. **Submission Process**:
   - Submit applications through preferred electronic systems
   - Pay required fees and obtain receipt documentation
   - Upload all supporting documents in required formats
   - Verify submission completeness before final submission
   - Obtain permit application number and tracking information

2. **Review Process Management**:
   - Monitor application status through online portals
   - Respond promptly to plan review comments and corrections
   - Coordinate with engineers or architects for revision requirements
   - Schedule and attend plan review meetings when required
   - Track review timelines and follow up on delays

3. **Permit Issuance and Distribution**:
   - Verify permit accuracy and project scope upon issuance
   - Distribute copies to all relevant project personnel
   - Post permits at job site as required by local codes
   - Update project management systems with permit information
   - Schedule required inspections and coordinate timing

### Phase 4: Inspection Coordination
1. **Inspection Schedule Management**:
   - **Rough Inspection**: Structural work and substrate preparation
   - **Progress Inspection**: Installation compliance during work
   - **Final Inspection**: Completed work and code compliance
   - **Special Inspections**: Wind resistance testing or specialized systems
   - **Follow-up Inspections**: Address any deficiencies or corrections

2. **Inspection Preparation**:
   - Verify work readiness and quality before inspection request
   - Prepare required documentation and test reports
   - Coordinate site access and safety requirements
   - Brief site personnel on inspection procedures and requirements
   - Ensure compliance with permit conditions and approved plans

3. **Inspection Follow-up**:
   - Review inspection reports and address any deficiencies
   - Schedule re-inspections after corrective work completion
   - Obtain final approval and certificate of completion
   - Update project documentation with inspection results
   - Close permits and obtain final documentation

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Application accuracy: 100% complete and accurate initial submissions
- Approval timeline: Meet or exceed local department standard processing times
- Inspection pass rate: 95% first-time inspection approval rate
- Documentation completeness: 100% required documentation submitted
- Code compliance: Zero code violations or compliance issues

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Application delays**: Check completeness and coordinate with reviewers
- **Plan review comments**: Address technical issues with design professionals
- **Inspection failures**: Correct deficiencies and schedule re-inspection
- **Code interpretation disputes**: Research precedents and seek clarification
- **Jurisdiction confusion**: Verify correct department and requirements

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document issue details and impact on project timeline
2. Research applicable codes and precedent interpretations
3. Coordinate with building department staff for clarification
4. Engage professional consultants when technical expertise required
5. Update procedures to prevent similar issues on future projects

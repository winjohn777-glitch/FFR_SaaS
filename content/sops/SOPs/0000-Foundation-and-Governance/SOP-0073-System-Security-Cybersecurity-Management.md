## BLOCK 1: PURPOSE - Strategic Intent and Business Objectives

To establish comprehensive system security and cybersecurity management procedures that protect Florida First Roofing LLC's digital assets, customer data, and business operations from cyber threats while ensuring regulatory compliance and maintaining business continuity. This SOP provides framework for proactive security management, threat detection, incident response, and continuous security improvement.

**Strategic Business Objectives:**
- Protect customer data and business information from unauthorized access and cyber threats
- Ensure compliance with cybersecurity regulations and industry standards
- Maintain business continuity through robust security controls and incident response
- Minimize financial and reputational risks associated with cybersecurity incidents
- Enable secure digital transformation and technology adoption
- Build customer trust through demonstrated commitment to data protection and security

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Cybersecurity:** The practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, damage, or theft through comprehensive security controls and risk management.

**Information Security:** The protection of information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

**Threat Intelligence:** Information about current and emerging security threats that enables organizations to make informed decisions about security controls and risk mitigation strategies.

**Vulnerability Management:** The systematic identification, evaluation, and remediation of security vulnerabilities in systems, applications, and infrastructure components.

**Incident Response:** The organized approach to addressing and managing security incidents to minimize impact, contain threats, and restore normal operations.

**Security Awareness:** The knowledge and understanding of cybersecurity risks, threats, and best practices necessary for personnel to protect organizational assets and information.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Security Framework Implementation and Management

**Phase 1: Security Assessment and Strategy Development (Weeks 1-4)**
1. Conduct comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessment including asset inventory and threat modeling
2. Evaluate current security controls and identify gaps against industry frameworks
3. Develop cybersecurity strategy aligned with business objectives and risk tolerance
4. Create security policies and procedures covering all aspects of information security
5. Establish security governance structure including committees and reporting relationships

**Phase 2: Security Controls Implementation (Weeks 5-12)**
1. Deploy network security controls including firewalls, intrusion detection, and monitoring
2. Implement endpoint security solutions including antivirus, endpoint detection and response
3. Configure access controls including multi-factor authentication and privilege management
4. Deploy security monitoring and logging infrastructure for threat detection
5. Establish vulnerability management processes including scanning and patch management

**Phase 3: Security Operations and Monitoring (Ongoing)**
1. Monitor security events and alerts through Security Operations Center (SOC) activities
2. Conduct regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
3. Investigate and respond to security incidents according to incident response procedures
4. Maintain threat intelligence and update security controls based on emerging threats
5. Conduct security awareness training and education for all personnel

### 5.2 Daily Security Operations Procedures

**Security Monitoring and Alert Management:**
1. Review security monitoring dashboards and alert queues for potential threats
2. Investigate security alerts and determine appropriate response actions
3. Escalate security incidents according to severity levels and response procedures
4. Document security events and maintain incident tracking and reporting
5. Coordinate with external security services and threat intelligence providers

**Vulnerability and Patch Management:**
1. Conduct automated vulnerability scanning of all systems and applications
2. Prioritize vulnerabilities based on risk assessment and business impact
3. Test and deploy security patches according to change management procedures
4. Verify patch installation and validate security control effectiveness
5. Update vulnerability tracking and report on remediation progress

### 5.3 Incident Response and Recovery

**Security Incident Detection and Classification:**
1. Identify potential security incidents through monitoring, alerts, or user reports
2. Classify incidents based on severity, impact, and type of security threat
3. Assemble incident response team and initiate communication procedures
4. Contain threats and prevent further damage or unauthorized access
5. Collect and preserve evidence for forensic analysis and legal proceedings

**Incident Response and Recovery Activities:**
1. Investigate incident scope, impact, and root cause through forensic analysis
2. Implement remediation actions to eliminate threats and restore normal operations
3. Communicate with stakeholders including management, customers, and regulatory agencies
4. Conduct post-incident review and lessons learned to improve security controls
5. Update incident response procedures and security controls based on incident findings

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Cybersecurity Risk Assessment and Mitigation
**Critical Security Risks:**
- Data breaches compromising customer information and business data
- Ransomware attacks disrupting business operations and demanding payments
- Insider threats from malicious or negligent employees and contractors
- Supply chain attacks through compromised vendors or third-party services
- Social engineering attacks targeting personnel with access to sensitive systems

**Risk Mitigation Strategies:**
- Implementation of defense-in-depth security architecture with multiple layers of protection
- Regular security awareness training to reduce human error and social engineering risks
- Comprehensive backup and disaster recovery capabilities to maintain business continuity
- Vendor security assessments and contract requirements for third-party protection
- Continuous monitoring and threat intelligence to detect and respond to emerging threats

### 8.2 Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery
**Security Incident Impact Management:**
- Business continuity plans addressing various cybersecurity incident scenarios
- Disaster recovery procedures for critical systems and data restoration
- Communication plans for customers, vendors, and regulatory agencies during incidents
- Alternative operating procedures during system outages or security incidents
- Insurance coverage and financial protection for cybersecurity incidents

**Emergency Response and Crisis Management:**
- Executive incident response team with clear roles and decision-making authority
- Communication protocols for media relations and public disclosure
- Legal and regulatory reporting requirements for security incidents
- Customer notification and support procedures during security incidents
- Coordination with law enforcement and cybersecurity authorities

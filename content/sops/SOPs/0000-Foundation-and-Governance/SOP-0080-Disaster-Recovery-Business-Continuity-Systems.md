## BLOCK 1: PURPOSE - Strategic Intent and Business Objectives

To establish comprehensive disaster recovery and business continuity systems that ensure Florida First Roofing LLC can maintain critical operations during natural disasters, system failures, cyberattacks, and other disruptions common to Florida's business environment. This SOP provides framework for rapid recovery, data protection, and continuous service delivery to customers during emergency situations.

**Strategic Business Objectives:**
- Ensure business operations continuity during hurricanes, storms, and natural disasters
- Protect critical business data and systems from loss or corruption
- Minimize revenue loss and customer service disruption during incidents
- Maintain regulatory compliance and safety standards during emergency operations
- Enable rapid recovery and restoration of full business capabilities
- Support emergency response coordination and communication with stakeholders

## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Disaster Recovery:** The process of restoring IT systems, data, and infrastructure to normal operations following a disruptive event, focusing on technology recovery and data restoration.

**Business Continuity:** The overall capability to maintain essential business functions during and after a disaster or disruption, encompassing people, processes, and technology.

**Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** The maximum acceptable length of time that business functions can be down after a disaster before the organization suffers unacceptable consequences.

**Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time, representing how much data the organization can afford to lose.

**Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** The process of identifying critical business functions and assessing the financial and operational impact of their disruption.

**Emergency Operations Center (EOC):** The designated facility and team responsible for coordinating disaster response and business continuity activities during emergencies.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Pre-Disaster Preparedness and Planning

**Business Continuity Plan Development (Annual Review):**
1. Conduct comprehensive business impact analysis identifying critical functions and dependencies
2. Develop detailed recovery procedures for each critical business function and system
3. Establish recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) for all systems
4. Create communication plans for employees, customers, vendors, and emergency services
5. Develop alternative operating procedures for extended disruptions

**Infrastructure and Data Protection:**
1. Implement automated backup systems with geographically diverse storage locations
2. Configure disaster recovery systems and test failover capabilities quarterly
3. Maintain emergency equipment inventory including generators, communications, and supplies
4. Establish alternative facility arrangements for extended displacement scenarios
5. Document all system configurations and recovery procedures with regular updates

**Team Training and Exercises:**
1. Conduct annual disaster recovery tabletop exercises with all key personnel
2. Perform quarterly technical recovery tests for critical systems and data
3. Train employees on emergency procedures and communication protocols
4. Coordinate with local emergency services and participate in community preparedness activities
5. Maintain current contact information and emergency notification systems

### 5.2 Disaster Response and Immediate Recovery

**Emergency Activation and Assessment (Hours 0-4):**
1. Activate emergency operations center and assemble business continuity team
2. Conduct immediate safety assessment and ensure all personnel are accounted for
3. Assess facility damage and infrastructure availability for continued operations
4. Evaluate IT systems status and initiate emergency data protection procedures
5. Communicate initial status to employees, customers, and key stakeholders

**Critical Systems Recovery (Hours 4-24):**
1. Prioritize recovery efforts based on predefined critical function priorities
2. Activate disaster recovery systems and restore essential business applications
3. Establish temporary communication systems and customer service capabilities
4. Coordinate with vendors and service providers for emergency support and supplies
5. Implement alternative operating procedures for disrupted business functions

**Extended Recovery Operations (Days 1-30):**
1. Continue systematic restoration of all business systems and functions
2. Monitor recovery progress and adjust priorities based on changing conditions
3. Coordinate insurance claims and damage assessment with professional adjusters
4. Manage customer communications and project timeline adjustments
5. Plan for transition back to normal operations and lessons learned integration

### 5.3 Post-Disaster Recovery and Improvement

**Full Recovery and Normalization:**
1. Complete restoration of all business systems and validate data integrity
2. Conduct comprehensive post-disaster assessment and document lessons learned
3. Update business continuity plans based on actual disaster experience
4. Debrief with all team members and stakeholders to identify improvement opportunities
5. Report final recovery status to insurance companies and regulatory agencies

## BLOCK 7: QUALITY CONTROL - Standards and Verification

### 7.1 Recovery Performance Standards
**Recovery Time Objectives (RTO):**
- Critical systems (CRM, Communication): 4 hours maximum downtime
- Financial systems: 8 hours maximum downtime
- Project management systems: 12 hours maximum downtime
- Supporting systems: 24 hours maximum downtime
- Full operations restoration: 72 hours maximum for normal disasters

**Recovery Point Objectives (RPO):**
- Critical customer data: Maximum 1 hour of data loss
- Financial data: Maximum 4 hours of data loss
- Project and operational data: Maximum 8 hours of data loss
- Supporting system data: Maximum 24 hours of data loss

### 7.2 Testing and Validation Standards
**Regular Testing Requirements:**
- Monthly backup verification and restoration testing
- Quarterly disaster recovery system testing and failover validation
- Semi-annual business continuity tabletop exercises
- Annual full-scale disaster recovery testing with all systems
- Continuous monitoring of backup systems and data integrity

**Quality Verification Procedures:**
- Independent verification of backup completeness and restoration capability
- Regular testing of emergency communication systems and contact information
- Vendor service level agreement compliance monitoring and validation
- Employee training completion tracking and competency verification
- Insurance coverage adequacy review and gap analysis

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Personnel Safety During Disasters
**Employee Safety Priorities:**
- Life safety takes precedence over business continuity and recovery activities
- No personnel should return to damaged facilities without professional safety assessment
- Emergency communication systems must include family notification and welfare checks
- Alternative transportation and shelter arrangements for employees unable to return home
- Mental health and counseling support for employees affected by disasters

**Emergency Response Safety:**
- Coordination with local emergency services and compliance with evacuation orders
- Personal protective equipment requirements for damage assessment activities
- Safety protocols for operating emergency generators and equipment
- Hazardous material assessment and proper handling during recovery
- Documentation of safety incidents and compliance with OSHA emergency procedures

### 8.2 Data Security During Recovery
**Data Protection During Emergencies:**
- Secure handling of backup media and recovery systems during transportation
- Authentication and access control maintenance during emergency operations
- Protection of sensitive customer and business data during recovery activities
- Secure disposal of damaged equipment containing sensitive information
- Incident response coordination between disaster recovery and cybersecurity teams

**System Security During Recovery:**
- Verification of system integrity before resuming normal operations
- Enhanced monitoring for cyber threats during vulnerable recovery periods
- Secure configuration of temporary systems and alternative operating procedures
- Regular security assessments during extended recovery operations
- Documentation of security incidents and compliance with breach notification procedures

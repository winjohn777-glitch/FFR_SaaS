## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

### 3.1 Retention and Archive Terms
**Document Retention Period:** Minimum time duration documents must be preserved before disposal
**Active Records:** Documents frequently accessed for current business operations
**Inactive Records:** Documents rarely accessed but retained for legal or compliance purposes
**Archive Storage:** Secure, organized storage system for inactive records
**Record Destruction:** Secure disposal process ensuring complete information elimination

### 3.2 Classification and Management Terms
**Legal Hold:** Suspension of normal retention schedules due to litigation or investigation
**Vital Records:** Documents essential for business continuity and disaster recovery
**Confidential Records:** Documents requiring special security and access restrictions
**Public Records:** Documents subject to public disclosure requirements
**Proprietary Information:** Trade secrets and confidential business information

### 3.3 Storage and Access Terms
**Digital Archive:** Electronic storage system for scanned and born-digital documents
**Physical Archive:** Secure facility for storing original paper documents
**Metadata Management:** Systematic organization of document descriptive information
**Indexing System:** Organizational structure enabling efficient document retrieval
**Access Controls:** Security measures restricting document access to authorized personnel

## BLOCK 6: STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Document Classification and Retention Assignment
**Step 1: Document Category Identification**
- Review new documents for content type and business function
- Assign appropriate document category based on established classification system
- Determine retention period based on legal, regulatory, and business requirements
- Apply security classification and access restriction requirements
- Update document metadata with retention and classification information

**Step 2: Retention Schedule Implementation**
- Configure automated retention rules in document management systems
- Set calendar reminders for retention milestone dates and reviews
- Establish review processes for retention period extensions or modifications
- Document retention decisions and approval authorities
- Integrate retention schedules with CRM and LMS platform configurations

### 6.2 Archive Transfer and Storage Procedures
**Step 1: Active to Inactive Record Transition**
- Identify documents reaching inactive status based on business usage patterns
- Prepare documents for archive transfer with complete indexing and organization
- Verify document completeness and integrity before transfer
- Update document management system with archive location information
- Coordinate physical or digital transfer to appropriate archive storage

**Step 2: Archive Organization and Maintenance**
- Organize archived documents using standardized filing and indexing systems
- Maintain climate control and security measures for physical archives
- Perform regular inventory verification and location accuracy checks
- Monitor archive storage capacity and plan for expansion needs
- Conduct periodic archive access testing and retrieval verification

### 6.3 Document Destruction and Disposal
**Step 1: Retention Period Expiration Processing**
- Generate monthly reports of documents reaching retention period expiration
- Review documents for legal hold status or business necessity for extended retention
- Obtain required approvals for document destruction from appropriate authorities
- Prepare documents for secure destruction using certified disposal methods
- Document destruction activities with certificates and audit trails

**Step 2: Secure Destruction Implementation**
- Use certified document destruction services for confidential materials
- Verify complete destruction of digital records and backup copies
- Obtain certificates of destruction for audit and compliance purposes
- Update document management systems to reflect destroyed record status
- Maintain destruction logs and certificates for regulatory compliance

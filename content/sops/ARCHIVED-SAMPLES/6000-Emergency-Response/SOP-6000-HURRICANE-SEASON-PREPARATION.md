# SOP-6000: Hurricane Season Preparation and Planning

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes comprehensive protocols for preparing Florida First Roofing LLC for hurricane season operations, ensuring business continuity, employee safety, and effective emergency response capabilities throughout Florida's annual hurricane season (June 1 - November 30).

### Scope
- Pre-season preparation activities and planning
- Equipment and material securing procedures
- Employee safety and communication protocols
- Customer notification and service continuity planning
- Insurance and documentation requirements
- Post-storm preparation for rapid response operations

## 2. Regulatory Framework

### Florida Building Code Requirements
- Chapter 16: Structural Design (Wind Load Requirements)
- Section 1609: Wind Loads for hurricane-force wind resistance
- Chapter 7: Fire and Smoke Protection Features during emergency conditions

### OSHA Compliance Standards
- 29 CFR 1926.95: Personal Protective Equipment during storm preparation
- 29 CFR 1926.501: Fall Protection Systems in high-wind conditions
- Emergency Action Plan requirements per 29 CFR 1910.38

### Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation
- Emergency contracting license activation procedures
- Post-disaster roofing contractor requirements
- Consumer protection compliance during emergency operations

### Insurance and Bonding Requirements
- Proof of active general liability insurance (minimum $1M)
- Workers' compensation coverage verification
- Surety bond status confirmation for emergency operations

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Pre-Season Equipment Preparation
```
Equipment Securing Checklist:
□ Vehicle fuel tanks filled to capacity
□ Generator maintenance and fuel verification
□ Hand tools inventoried and secured
□ Safety equipment inspection and replacement
□ Communication devices tested and charged
□ Material inventory secured and protected
□ Heavy equipment secured and positioned
□ Office equipment backed up and protected
```

### 5.2 72-Hour Storm Preparation Protocol
```
Activation Timeline:
Hour 0-24: Initial threat assessment and planning
Hour 24-48: Customer notification and work stoppage
Hour 48-72: Complete equipment securing and evacuation
```

### 5.3 Employee Safety and Communication
```
Communication Protocol:
1. Initial notification via phone tree and text alerts
2. Safety briefing and instruction distribution
3. Evacuation procedure implementation if required
4. Check-in requirements during storm passage
5. Return-to-work clearance and safety verification
```

### 5.4 Customer Service Continuity
```
Customer Notification Process:
1. Active project status communication
2. Material and equipment protection notification
3. Post-storm service priority scheduling
4. Emergency contact information distribution
5. Insurance coordination assistance offering
```

# SOP-6200: Advanced Hurricane Preparation Protocol

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive advanced hurricane preparation procedures for Florida First Roofing LLC to ensure maximum operational readiness, equipment protection, personnel safety, and rapid response capability during Category 3-5 hurricane events and extended storm seasons.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC operations during hurricane season (June 1 - November 30), including advanced preparation for major hurricanes, multi-location coordination, supply chain management, insurance coordination, and post-storm rapid deployment protocols.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Emergency Response Coordinator**: Oversee all hurricane preparation activities and coordinate multi-location response
- **Operations Manager**: Manage equipment securing, inventory protection, and facility preparation
- **Safety Director**: Ensure personnel safety protocols and emergency evacuation procedures
- **Fleet Manager**: Coordinate vehicle and equipment protection and deployment strategies
- **Communications Manager**: Manage customer communications and media relations
- **Regional Supervisors**: Coordinate local area preparations and personnel management

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Advanced Monitoring Systems:
- Professional weather monitoring stations with satellite uplinks
- Hurricane tracking software with 72-hour precision modeling
- Emergency communication systems (satellite phones, HAM radios)
- Mobile command centers with independent power systems
- Real-time damage assessment drones with hurricane-resistant capabilities

### Protection Equipment:
- Industrial-grade building protection materials (hurricane panels, reinforcement systems)
- Heavy-duty equipment securing systems and hurricane-rated storage
- Emergency generator systems with 14-day fuel capacity
- Water damage prevention systems and flood barriers
- Advanced inventory protection and climate control systems

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Maintain 72-hour advance notice for all personnel evacuation decisions
- Ensure emergency shelter capabilities for essential personnel
- Verify backup communication systems and emergency contact protocols
- Establish medical emergency procedures for storm period isolation
- Implement advanced personal safety protocols for post-storm operations
- Coordinate with local emergency management and first responders

## 6. Pre-Operation Checklist
- [ ] Activate hurricane tracking systems 96 hours before projected landfall
- [ ] Verify all emergency communication systems and backup power
- [ ] Secure all equipment, vehicles, and inventory at designated facilities
- [ ] Coordinate with insurance carriers and document pre-storm conditions

- [ ] Activate customer communication protocols and project suspension procedures
- [ ] Verify fuel reserves and emergency supply inventories
- [ ] Coordinate with emergency services and obtain necessary permits

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Advanced Warning Period (96-72 Hours Before Landfall)
1. **Hurricane Tracking Activation**:
   - Activate professional weather monitoring stations and satellite tracking
   - Begin continuous monitoring of National Hurricane Center forecasts
   - Initiate coordination with emergency management agencies
   - Establish communication with insurance carriers and adjusters
   - Begin customer notification and project status updates

2. **Operational Assessment and Planning**:
   - Assess all active projects and determine suspension timelines
   - Review equipment inventory and identify critical protection priorities
   - Evaluate facility vulnerabilities and implement reinforcement measures
   - Coordinate with suppliers for emergency material delivery
   - Establish post-storm response team assignments and deployment plans

### Phase 2: Preparation Period (72-48 Hours Before Landfall)
1. **Equipment and Facility Securing**:
   - Relocate all mobile equipment to designated hurricane-safe facilities
   - Install hurricane protection systems on all company buildings
   - Secure outdoor inventory and materials using advanced protection systems
   - Verify generator systems and fuel reserves for extended operation
   - Document all facility conditions with comprehensive photo/video records

2. **Personnel and Safety Preparation**:
   - Conduct final safety briefings and emergency procedure reviews
   - Distribute emergency communication devices and contact information
   - Verify evacuation routes and emergency shelter assignments
   - Establish medical emergency protocols and first aid station preparation
   - Coordinate with families for extended absence contingencies

### Phase 3: Final Preparation (48-24 Hours Before Landfall)
1. **Critical System Verification**:
   - Test all emergency communication systems and backup protocols
   - Verify generator operation and fuel delivery systems
   - Conduct final equipment securing and facility protection verification
   - Complete insurance documentation and claims preparation procedures
   - Establish post-storm assessment team deployment schedules

2. **Customer and Stakeholder Communication**:
   - Send final customer updates and project suspension confirmations
   - Coordinate with insurance carriers for post-storm adjuster deployment
   - Establish media communication protocols and public relations management
   - Verify emergency contact information for all critical stakeholders
   - Activate automated communication systems for status updates

### Phase 4: Storm Period Operations (During Hurricane)
1. **Emergency Command Center Operations**:
   - Maintain continuous operation of emergency command center
   - Monitor weather conditions and adjust response plans accordingly
   - Coordinate with emergency services and maintain situational awareness
   - Document storm progression and potential damage assessment
   - Maintain communication with essential personnel and emergency contacts

2. **Safety and Security Monitoring**:
   - Continuously monitor personnel safety and emergency shelter conditions
   - Maintain security systems and facility monitoring capabilities
   - Document storm damage in real-time using available technology
   - Coordinate with emergency services for any required assistance
   - Prepare for immediate post-storm assessment and response activation

### Phase 5: Immediate Post-Storm Response (0-24 Hours After)
1. **Initial Damage Assessment**:
   - Deploy drone systems for aerial facility and equipment assessment
   - Conduct preliminary safety inspections of all company facilities
   - Document storm damage using advanced assessment technologies
   - Coordinate with emergency services for facility access and safety clearance
   - Begin initial customer property damage assessment planning

2. **Operational Recovery Initiation**:
   - Activate emergency response teams and begin deployment preparation
   - Restore communication systems and establish customer contact protocols
   - Coordinate with suppliers for emergency material and equipment needs
   - Begin insurance claim documentation and adjuster coordination
   - Initiate media relations and public communication strategies

## 8. Quality Control Standards

- Post-storm response time: Deployment within 4 hours of all-clear status

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Equipment securing delays**: Implement staged protection schedules and additional crews
- **Communication system failures**: Activate redundant systems and alternative protocols
- **Personnel evacuation conflicts**: Establish clear priority procedures and family support
- **Supplier delivery disruptions**: Maintain strategic inventory reserves and alternative sources
- **Insurance coordination delays**: Pre-establish relationships and documentation procedures

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Activate backup systems and alternative procedures immediately
2. Escalate critical issues to Emergency Response Coordinator
3. Coordinate with external agencies and emergency services as needed
4. Document all deviations and implement process improvements
5. Conduct post-storm debriefing and procedure refinement

# SOP-3040: Drone Inspection Technology Procedures

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for using unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) drone technology for roof inspections, documentation, and quality control assessments. This procedure ensures safe, legal, and effective use of drone technology while maintaining high standards for inspection accuracy and documentation.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC drone operations including:
- Pre-project roof condition assessments
- Work progress documentation and monitoring
- Post-installation quality control inspections
- Insurance claim documentation and support
- Marketing and promotional photography
- Emergency response and damage assessment

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle)**: Aircraft operated without a human pilot aboard, commonly called a drone
- **Remote Pilot**: FAA-certified operator responsible for drone flight operations
- **Part 107**: FAA regulations governing commercial drone operations
- **Visual Line of Sight (VLOS)**: Requirement to maintain visual contact with drone during flight
- **Controlled Airspace**: Airspace requiring specific authorization for drone operations
- **Thermal Imaging**: Technology using infrared sensors to detect temperature variations

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Pre-Flight Planning and Preparation

#### 5.1.1 Mission Planning
1. **Inspection Objectives Definition**
   - Define specific inspection goals and requirements
   - Identify areas of interest and concern
   - Plan optimal flight patterns for complete coverage
   - Determine required resolution and data quality standards

2. **Site Assessment**
   - Review property boundaries and access restrictions
   - Identify potential hazards and obstacles
   - Assess weather conditions and flight windows
   - Coordinate with property owners and occupants

3. **Regulatory Compliance Check**
   - Verify airspace classifications and restrictions
   - Obtain required authorizations for controlled airspace
   - Check for temporary flight restrictions (TFRs)
   - Review local ordinances and permit requirements

#### 5.1.2 Equipment Preparation
1. **Aircraft Inspection**
   - Conduct pre-flight inspection checklist
   - Check battery levels and charging status
   - Verify camera and sensor functionality
   - Test communication and control systems

2. **Data Collection Setup**
   - Configure camera settings for optimal image quality
   - Set up thermal imaging parameters if applicable
   - Prepare data storage devices and backup systems
   - Test real-time data transmission capabilities

### 5.2 Flight Operations

#### 5.2.1 Safety Procedures
1. **Pre-Flight Safety Check**
   - Establish visual observer if required
   - Set up no-fly zones and safety boundaries
   - Check weather conditions and wind speeds
   - Verify emergency procedures and contacts

2. **Launch and Flight Operations**
   - Maintain visual line of sight at all times
   - Monitor aircraft systems and battery levels
   - Follow planned flight path with safety margins
   - Adjust for changing conditions and obstacles

3. **Emergency Procedures**
   - Execute return-to-home function if needed
   - Implement emergency landing procedures
   - Report incidents according to FAA requirements
   - Document emergency actions and outcomes

#### 5.2.2 Data Collection Standards
1. **Photography Standards**
   - Capture images with minimum 80% overlap for 3D modeling
   - Maintain consistent altitude for uniform resolution
   - Use appropriate exposure settings for lighting conditions
   - Document specific areas of interest with detail shots

2. **Video Documentation**
   - Record smooth, stable video footage
   - Narrate observations during flight when appropriate
   - Focus on specific defects and conditions
   - Maintain consistent speed and altitude

### 5.3 Thermal Imaging Inspections

#### 5.3.1 Thermal Camera Operations
1. **Equipment Setup**
   - Calibrate thermal camera for ambient conditions
   - Set appropriate temperature ranges and scales
   - Configure image capture and recording settings
   - Verify thermal and visual image registration

2. **Data Collection Techniques**
   - Conduct flights during optimal thermal conditions
   - Capture both thermal and visual spectrum images
   - Document temperature variations and anomalies
   - Record environmental conditions affecting thermal readings

#### 5.3.2 Thermal Analysis Procedures
1. **Data Processing**
   - Analyze thermal images for temperature anomalies
   - Identify moisture intrusion and insulation defects
   - Correlate thermal data with visual observations
   - Document findings with temperature measurements

2. **Report Generation**
   - Create thermal analysis reports with images
   - Provide temperature data and interpretation
   - Recommend further investigation where needed
   - Include methodology and limitations of thermal analysis

### 5.4 Data Processing and Analysis

#### 5.4.1 Image Processing
1. **Quality Control**
   - Review all collected imagery for quality and completeness
   - Identify and flag any data gaps or quality issues
   - Process images for optimal viewing and analysis
   - Create backup copies of all original data

2. **Enhancement and Analysis**
   - Adjust image exposure and contrast for clarity
   - Create panoramic views and 3D models where appropriate
   - Identify and mark defects and areas of concern
   - Measure dimensions and areas using image analysis tools

#### 5.4.2 Documentation and Reporting
1. **Inspection Reports**
   - Create comprehensive inspection reports with images
   - Document all identified conditions and defects
   - Provide recommendations for repairs or further investigation
   - Include metadata for all images and data collected

2. **Client Deliverables**
   - Prepare client-specific reports and presentations
   - Provide high-resolution images and analysis
   - Create executive summaries for key findings
   - Deliver data in requested formats and platforms

### 5.5 Quality Control and Validation

#### 5.5.1 Data Verification
1. **Ground Truth Validation**
   - Compare drone observations with ground-level inspections
   - Verify identified defects through traditional inspection methods
   - Validate thermal anomalies with direct measurement
   - Document correlation between drone and ground observations

2. **Technical Review**
   - Have qualified inspectors review drone findings
   - Verify compliance with inspection standards
   - Check analysis methodology and conclusions
   - Ensure consistency with industry best practices

#### 5.5.2 Continuous Improvement
1. **Performance Assessment**
   - Monitor accuracy of drone-based inspections
   - Track time savings and efficiency improvements
   - Assess client satisfaction with drone services
   - Identify areas for procedural improvements

2. **Technology Updates**
   - Evaluate new drone technologies and capabilities
   - Update procedures based on equipment improvements
   - Training on new features and analysis techniques
   - Integration of artificial intelligence and automation

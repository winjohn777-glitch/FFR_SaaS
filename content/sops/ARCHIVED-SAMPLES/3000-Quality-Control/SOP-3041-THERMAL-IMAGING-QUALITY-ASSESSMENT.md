# SOP-3041: Thermal Imaging Quality Assessment

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for using thermal imaging technology to assess roof system quality, detect defects, and identify performance issues. This procedure ensures accurate thermal data collection, analysis, and interpretation for quality control and diagnostic purposes.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC thermal imaging applications including:
- Moisture intrusion detection and mapping
- Insulation defect identification
- Energy efficiency assessments
- Quality control inspections
- Warranty investigation support
- Preventive maintenance planning

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Thermal Imaging**: Technology using infrared radiation to create images showing temperature variations
- **Thermography**: Process of using thermal imaging for analysis and documentation
- **Infrared (IR)**: Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light
- **Emissivity**: Measure of a material's ability to emit infrared energy
- **Thermal Bridge**: Area where heat transfer occurs more readily than surrounding areas
- **Delta T**: Temperature difference between areas being compared

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Pre-Inspection Planning and Preparation

#### 5.1.1 Inspection Objectives and Scope
1. **Problem Definition**
   - Identify specific issues to be investigated
   - Define areas of concern based on visual inspection
   - Establish success criteria for thermal investigation
   - Plan follow-up actions based on potential findings

2. **Site Assessment**
   - Review building plans and construction details
   - Identify access routes and safety requirements
   - Assess weather conditions and optimal timing
   - Coordinate with building occupants and operations

3. **Equipment Selection**
   - Choose appropriate thermal camera for application
   - Select proper lenses and measurement accessories
   - Verify equipment calibration and certification
   - Prepare backup equipment and power supplies

#### 5.1.2 Environmental Conditions Assessment
1. **Weather Conditions**
   - Monitor ambient temperature and humidity
   - Assess wind speed and direction effects
   - Check for precipitation and solar loading
   - Plan optimal timing for thermal contrast

2. **Building Conditions**
   - Verify HVAC system operation status
   - Document interior and exterior temperatures
   - Assess artificial heating or cooling sources
   - Record occupancy and internal heat loads

### 5.2 Thermal Imaging Data Collection

#### 5.2.1 Equipment Setup and Calibration
1. **Camera Configuration**
   - Set appropriate temperature range for application
   - Configure emissivity settings for roof materials
   - Adjust focus and measurement parameters
   - Verify date, time, and location settings

2. **Reference Measurements**
   - Take ambient temperature readings with contact thermometer
   - Establish temperature reference points
   - Document solar loading and environmental conditions
   - Record equipment settings and parameters

#### 5.2.2 Image Acquisition Techniques
1. **Survey Patterns**
   - Use systematic grid patterns for complete coverage
   - Overlap images for comprehensive documentation
   - Maintain consistent distance and angle when possible
   - Document viewing angle and measurement distance

2. **Image Quality Standards**
   - Ensure sharp focus throughout thermal range
   - Minimize reflected radiation from external sources
   - Capture images during optimal thermal conditions
   - Document both thermal and visual reference images

#### 5.2.3 Data Documentation
1. **Image Annotation**
   - Record GPS coordinates for each image location
   - Document measurement parameters and settings
   - Note environmental conditions at time of capture
   - Include visual reference photographs

2. **Measurement Recording**
   - Document temperature measurements and differentials
   - Record emissivity settings used for different materials
   - Note any calibration adjustments made during survey
   - Create detailed site sketches and reference maps

### 5.3 Thermal Pattern Analysis

#### 5.3.1 Moisture Detection and Analysis
1. **Moisture Pattern Identification**
   - Recognize typical thermal signatures of wet insulation
   - Differentiate between moisture and thermal bridging
   - Assess extent and severity of moisture intrusion
   - Correlate thermal patterns with roof drainage

2. **Quantitative Analysis**
   - Measure temperature differentials across wet areas
   - Calculate moisture-affected areas using image analysis
   - Compare readings to established moisture criteria
   - Document findings with measurement data

#### 5.3.2 Insulation Defect Assessment
1. **Thermal Bridge Identification**
   - Locate areas of thermal bridging through structure
   - Identify missing or compressed insulation
   - Assess insulation continuity and effectiveness
   - Evaluate energy loss patterns

2. **Performance Evaluation**
   - Compare thermal performance to design requirements
   - Calculate energy efficiency impacts
   - Identify priority areas for improvement
   - Assess cost-benefit of corrective actions

### 5.4 Quality Control Applications

#### 5.4.1 Installation Quality Verification
1. **Membrane Installation Assessment**
   - Verify proper insulation installation below membrane
   - Identify air gaps or voids in installation
   - Assess adhesive coverage and bonding quality
   - Detect delamination or separation issues

2. **System Integration Evaluation**
   - Check thermal performance at roof penetrations
   - Assess flashing and detail installation quality
   - Verify proper insulation around mechanical equipment
   - Evaluate edge and perimeter installation quality

#### 5.4.2 Performance Monitoring
1. **Baseline Establishment**
   - Create thermal baseline immediately after installation
   - Document normal thermal patterns for roof system
   - Establish temperature differential criteria
   - Create reference library for future comparisons

2. **Periodic Assessment**
   - Conduct regular thermal surveys per maintenance schedule
   - Compare current conditions to baseline measurements
   - Track changes in thermal performance over time
   - Identify developing issues before failure occurs

### 5.5 Data Analysis and Reporting

#### 5.5.1 Image Processing and Enhancement
1. **Thermal Image Analysis**
   - Process images for optimal contrast and clarity
   - Apply appropriate color palettes for visualization
   - Enhance images to highlight thermal anomalies
   - Create composite images for comprehensive view

2. **Quantitative Analysis**
   - Calculate temperature statistics for different areas
   - Measure areas of thermal anomalies
   - Generate histograms and temperature profiles
   - Compare measurements to specification limits

#### 5.5.2 Report Generation
1. **Technical Analysis Report**
   - Document methodology and equipment used
   - Present findings with thermal and visual images
   - Provide quantitative data and measurements
   - Include recommendations for corrective actions

2. **Executive Summary**
   - Summarize key findings and implications
   - Prioritize recommended actions by severity
   - Estimate costs and benefits of repairs
   - Provide timeline for recommended actions

## 7. PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

### 7.1 Data Quality Standards
- Thermal measurement accuracy within ±2°C or ±2% of reading
- Complete coverage of specified inspection areas
- Clear, focused thermal images with proper contrast
- Consistent measurement methodology throughout survey

### 7.2 Analysis Standards
- Accurate identification of thermal anomalies
- Quantitative analysis with statistical validation
- Clear correlation between thermal and visual observations
- Recommendations based on established criteria and standards

### 7.3 Reporting Standards
- Comprehensive reports delivered within 48 hours
- Clear presentation of findings with supporting data
- Professional quality thermal images and documentation
- Actionable recommendations with priority ranking

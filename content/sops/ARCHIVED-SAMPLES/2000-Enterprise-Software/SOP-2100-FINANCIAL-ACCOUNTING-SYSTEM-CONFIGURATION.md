## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS

**Financial Management System:** The integrated software platform managing all financial transactions, accounting processes, reporting functions, and compliance requirements for comprehensive business financial management.

**Chart of Accounts:** The organized structure of account codes and categories used to classify and track all financial transactions according to standard accounting principles and business requirements.

**Financial Controls:** The systematic procedures and safeguards implemented to ensure accuracy, prevent fraud, maintain compliance, and provide reliable financial information for business decision-making.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE

### 5.1 System Configuration and Setup

**Chart of Accounts Configuration:**
1. Establish account structure for assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expenses
2. Configure job costing accounts for project tracking
3. Set up department and location codes for detailed reporting
4. Create customer and vendor account structures
5. Configure tax codes and reporting requirements

**General Ledger Setup:**
1. Configure accounting periods and fiscal year settings
2. Set up automated journal entry rules and procedures
3. Configure approval workflows for transactions
4. Establish closing procedures and month-end processes
5. Set up financial reporting templates and schedules

### 5.2 Accounts Receivable Management

**Customer Account Setup:**
1. Create customer profiles with credit terms and limits
2. Configure invoicing templates and automated billing
3. Set up payment terms and collection procedures
4. Establish aging reports and collection workflows
5. Configure integration with CRM and project management systems

**Invoicing and Collections:**
1. Generate invoices based on project milestones and deliverables
2. Process customer payments and apply to accounts
3. Monitor accounts receivable aging and follow up procedures
4. Manage collection activities and write-off procedures
5. Generate customer statements and account reports

### 5.3 Accounts Payable Processing

**Vendor Management:**
1. Set up vendor profiles with payment terms and tax information
2. Configure purchase order and approval workflows
3. Establish three-way matching procedures (PO, receipt, invoice)
4. Set up electronic payment processing and check printing
5. Configure expense reporting and reimbursement procedures

**Payment Processing:**
1. Review and approve vendor invoices for payment
2. Schedule payments according to terms and cash flow
3. Process electronic payments and print checks
4. Reconcile bank accounts and payment records
5. Generate vendor reports and 1099 forms

### 5.4 Payroll Administration

**Employee Setup:**
1. Configure employee profiles with tax information and deductions
2. Set up pay rates, overtime rules, and benefit calculations
3. Configure time and attendance integration
4. Establish payroll schedules and processing procedures
5. Set up tax withholding and reporting requirements

**Payroll Processing:**
1. Import time and attendance data for pay period
2. Calculate gross pay, deductions, and net pay
3. Generate paychecks and direct deposit transactions
4. Process payroll taxes and government remittances
5. Generate payroll reports and employee statements

## BLOCK 7: QUALITY CONTROL

### 7.1 Financial Accuracy Standards
- Transaction accuracy: 99.9% minimum for all financial entries
- Bank reconciliation: 100% completion within 5 business days
- Month-end closing: Complete within 10 business days
- Financial reporting: 100% accuracy for management and regulatory reports
- Audit compliance: Zero material findings for internal and external audits

### 7.2 Internal Controls and Verification
- Segregation of duties for financial transactions
- Approval requirements for expenditures and adjustments
- Regular account reconciliation and verification
- Monthly financial review and analysis procedures
- Annual external audit and compliance verification

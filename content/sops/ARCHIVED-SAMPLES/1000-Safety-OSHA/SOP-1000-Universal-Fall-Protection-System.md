## BLOCK 3: DEFINITIONS - Key Terms and Concepts

**Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS):** A complete system consisting of full-body harness, connecting device (lanyard or self-retracting lifeline), and anchorage point, designed to arrest a worker's fall and limit maximum arresting force to 1,800 pounds.

**Anchorage Point:** A secure point of attachment capable of supporting at least 5,000 pounds per employee attached, or designed with a safety factor of at least two under the supervision of a qualified person.

**Guardrail System:** A barrier erected to prevent employees from falling to lower levels, consisting of top rail, midrail, and posts, meeting specific height and strength requirements.

**Safety Net System:** A horizontal or inclined catching system positioned to arrest falling workers, meeting specific impact and installation requirements.

**Controlled Access Zone:** An area designated on a low-slope roof where work may take place without conventional fall protection systems, with specific boundary and supervision requirements.

**Warning Line System:** A barrier erected on a roof to warn employees that they are approaching an unprotected roof side or edge, used in conjunction with other fall protection measures.

**Competent Person:** An individual capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards and authorized to take prompt corrective measures to eliminate them.

**Qualified Person:** An individual with recognized degree, certificate, or professional standing, or extensive knowledge, training, and experience in fall protection systems.

## BLOCK 5: PROCEDURE - Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

### 5.1 Fall Protection System Selection
**Step 1: Hazard Assessment and Site Evaluation (30 minutes)**
1. Identify all potential fall hazards and exposure areas
2. Measure distances to lower levels and determine protection requirements
3. Evaluate roof slope, surface conditions, and structural capabilities
4. Assess weather conditions and environmental factors
5. Document findings and develop protection plan

**Step 2: System Selection and Engineering (15 minutes)**
1. Select appropriate fall protection system based on:
   - Work activity and duration requirements
   - Roof type, slope, and structural conditions
   - Employee skill level and training requirements
   - Equipment availability and cost considerations
2. Verify system compatibility and integration requirements
3. Ensure compliance with manufacturer specifications and OSHA standards

**Step 3: Installation Planning and Preparation (20 minutes)**
1. Develop detailed installation plan with anchor point locations
2. Verify structural adequacy and load-bearing capacity
3. Coordinate with other trades and project activities
4. Gather required tools, equipment, and materials
5. Brief installation team and review safety procedures

### 5.2 Personal Fall Arrest System Implementation
**Equipment Inspection and Setup (10 minutes per person)**
1. **Harness Inspection:**
   - Check all webbing for cuts, tears, abrasion, or chemical damage
   - Inspect all hardware for cracks, sharp edges, or deformation
   - Verify proper fit and adjustment for individual user
   - Ensure all buckles and connections function properly

2. **Lanyard/SRL Inspection:**
   - Examine entire length for damage or wear
   - Test locking mechanisms and energy absorber function
   - Verify proper connections and hardware integrity
   - Check manufacturer labels and inspection dates

3. **Anchorage Point Verification:**
   - Confirm 5,000-pound capacity or engineered design
   - Inspect installation and structural connection
   - Verify proper positioning and accessibility
   - Document inspection and approval

**Connection and Use Procedures (5 minutes)**
1. Don harness properly with all straps secured and adjusted
2. Connect to anchorage point before accessing hazard area
3. Maintain proper positioning to minimize fall distance
4. Move systematically to avoid swing hazards
5. Disconnect only after returning to safe area

### 5.3 Guardrail System Installation
**System Design and Layout (45 minutes)**
1. **Post Installation:**
   - Space posts maximum 8 feet on center
   - Install posts with minimum 200-pound resistance in any direction
   - Ensure posts extend minimum 42 inches above working surface
   - Secure posts to structural members or use weighted bases

2. **Rail Installation:**
   - Install top rail 42 inches Â± 3 inches above working surface
   - Install midrail approximately halfway between top rail and surface
   - Ensure rails withstand 200-pound force in any direction
   - Eliminate gaps greater than 19 inches

3. **Toe Board Installation (if required):**
   - Install minimum 3.5-inch height toe boards
   - Secure to prevent dislodgment
   - Eliminate gaps greater than 1 inch at surface

## BLOCK 8: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS - Risk Management and Mitigation

### 8.1 Fall Protection System Risks
**System Failure Risks:**
- Inadequate anchor point capacity or installation defects
- Equipment wear, damage, or improper maintenance
- Incorrect system selection for work conditions
- Employee error or non-compliance with procedures

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Engineering review and load testing of all anchor points
- Comprehensive inspection and maintenance programs
- Proper system selection based on hazard assessment
- Continuous training and competency verification

### 8.2 Emergency Response and Rescue Procedures
**Fall Incident Response (5-minute protocol):**
1. **Immediate Response:**
   - Call 911 and company emergency number
   - Provide first aid if qualified and safe to do so
   - Do not move injured person unless immediate danger exists
   - Preserve incident scene for investigation

2. **Rescue Procedures:**
   - Deploy trained rescue team if available
   - Use proper rescue equipment and techniques
   - Consider suspension trauma and rapid rescue needs
   - Coordinate with emergency services and medical personnel

3. **Post-Incident Actions:**
   - Secure area and prevent additional exposure
   - Document incident and preserve evidence
   - Conduct immediate investigation and root cause analysis
   - Implement corrective actions and communicate lessons learned

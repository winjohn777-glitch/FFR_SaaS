# SOP-1060: Incident Reporting and Investigation Procedures

- Investigation methodology and root cause analysis

- Performance monitoring and trend analysis for prevention

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Incident Classification and Reporting Criteria
```
Reportable Incidents (OSHA Recordable):
□ Work-related fatality or serious injury
□ Work-related injury requiring medical treatment beyond first aid
□ Work-related illness or disease from workplace exposure
□ Work-related injury resulting in days away from work
□ Work-related injury requiring restricted work activity

Immediate Notification Required (Within 8-24 hours):
□ Fatality or multiple hospitalizations
□ Amputation or loss of eye injury
□ Serious injury requiring emergency medical treatment
□ Incident with potential for fatality or serious injury
□ Incident involving multiple employees or third parties
```

### 5.2 Immediate Response Procedures
```
Emergency Response Checklist:
1. Ensure scene safety and eliminate ongoing threats
2. Provide immediate medical assistance and emergency care
3. Contact emergency services (911) if required
4. Notify immediate supervisor and safety officer
5. Secure incident scene and preserve evidence

Initial Documentation:
□ Date, time, and location of incident
□ Names of injured persons and witnesses
□ Brief description of incident circumstances
□ Immediate actions taken and medical response
□ Preliminary assessment of contributing factors
```

### 5.3 Investigation Methodology
```
Investigation Team Composition:
- Investigation Coordinator (lead investigator)
- Safety Officer or qualified safety professional
- Immediate supervisor or department manager
- Employee representative or union delegate (if applicable)
- Subject matter expert (as needed for technical issues)

Investigation Process:
1. Scene examination and physical evidence collection
2. Equipment inspection and malfunction analysis
3. Witness interviews and statement documentation
4. Procedure review and compliance verification
5. Root cause analysis and contributing factor identification
```

### 5.4 Root Cause Analysis Framework
```
Primary Investigation Areas:
- Human factors and employee actions
- Equipment and tool condition and suitability
- Environmental conditions and workplace hazards
- Procedures and training adequacy and compliance
- Management systems and safety culture factors

Analysis Methods:
- 5 Whys technique for systematic questioning
- Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram for cause categorization
- Fault tree analysis for complex incident sequences
- Timeline reconstruction for sequence understanding
- Barrier analysis for prevention system evaluation
```

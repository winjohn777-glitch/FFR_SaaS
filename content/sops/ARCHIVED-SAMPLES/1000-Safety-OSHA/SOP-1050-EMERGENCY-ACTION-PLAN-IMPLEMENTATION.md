# SOP-1050: Emergency Action Plan Implementation

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Emergency Classification and Response
```
Level 1 - Minor Emergency (Local Response):
□ Minor injury requiring first aid treatment
□ Small fire controlled by portable extinguisher
□ Minor chemical spill contained and manageable
□ Equipment malfunction creating localized hazard
□ Weather watch conditions requiring monitoring

Level 2 - Major Emergency (Full Response):
□ Serious injury requiring emergency medical services
□ Fire requiring fire department response and evacuation
□ Severe weather warning and immediate threat
□ Hazardous material exposure requiring specialized response
□ Workplace violence or security threat requiring police response

Level 3 - Catastrophic Emergency (Regional Response):
□ Multiple serious injuries or fatalities
□ Major fire with structural damage and regional impact
□ Hurricane or tornado with widespread damage
□ Major hazardous material release with community impact
□ Active shooter or terrorist threat requiring SWAT response
```

### 5.2 Emergency Communication Procedures
```
Internal Communication:
1. Immediate supervisor notification and assessment
2. Safety Officer and Emergency Coordinator alert
3. All-employee notification via emergency communication system
4. Management team activation and coordination
5. Family notification and employee support coordination

External Communication:
1. Emergency services (911) notification and coordination
2. Regulatory agency notification (OSHA, EPA as required)
3. Insurance carrier emergency reporting and claim initiation
4. Customer notification and service impact communication
5. Media and public relations coordination (if required)
```

### 5.3 Evacuation Procedures
```
Evacuation Decision Criteria:
- Fire or explosion hazard with structural threat
- Hazardous material release requiring area clearance
- Severe weather with immediate life safety threat
- Workplace violence requiring immediate area evacuation
- Structural collapse or major building damage

Evacuation Process:
1. Emergency signal activation (alarm, horn, announcement)
2. Immediate work stoppage and equipment securing
3. Evacuation route utilization and coordination
4. Assembly point gathering and headcount verification
5. Emergency services coordination and site security
```

### 5.4 Medical Emergency Response
```
Medical Emergency Protocol:
1. Immediate first aid and basic life support provision
2. Emergency medical services (EMS) activation and coordination
3. Victim stabilization and care until professional help arrives
4. Accident scene preservation and evidence protection
5. Family notification and support coordination

First Aid and Medical Support:
- Trained first aid responders on each work crew
- First aid kit availability and regular inspection
- AED (Automated External Defibrillator) accessibility and training
- Emergency contact information and medical history access
- Medical emergency transportation and hospital coordination
```

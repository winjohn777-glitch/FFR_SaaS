# SOP-1080: Mobile Equipment and Vehicle Safety

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive safety protocols for the operation, maintenance, and management of mobile equipment and vehicles used by Florida First Roofing LLC. This procedure ensures compliance with OSHA standards, DOT regulations, and Florida state requirements while promoting safe transportation and equipment operation.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC:
- Company-owned vehicles and equipment
- Leased and rental vehicles
- Personal vehicles used for business purposes
- Mobile equipment including trailers, lifts, and machinery
- Fleet management and maintenance operations
- Driver qualification and training programs

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Commercial Motor Vehicle (CMV)**: Vehicle with GVWR of 26,001 pounds or more, designed to transport 16+ passengers, or transporting hazardous materials
- **Fleet Safety Program**: Comprehensive program managing vehicle operations, maintenance, and driver safety
- **Vehicle Inspection**: Systematic examination of vehicle condition and safety systems
- **Driver Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR)**: Daily pre-trip and post-trip inspection documentation
- **Mobile Equipment**: Self-propelled or towed equipment used for construction operations

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Driver Qualification and Training

#### 5.1.1 Driver Qualification Requirements
1. **License and Certification**
   - Valid Florida driver's license appropriate for vehicle class
   - Commercial Driver's License (CDL) for vehicles requiring CDL
   - Current DOT medical certification for CDL drivers
   - Clean driving record with acceptable violation history

2. **Background Verification**
   - Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) check within past 30 days
   - Drug and alcohol testing per DOT requirements
   - Criminal background check completion
   - Previous employment verification

3. **Training Requirements**
   - Defensive driving course completion
   - Vehicle-specific operation training
   - Company policy and procedure training
   - Emergency response and first aid training

#### 5.1.2 Ongoing Driver Management
1. **Regular Monitoring**
   - Annual MVR review for all drivers
   - Quarterly performance evaluations
   - Monthly safety meeting participation
   - Incident and violation tracking

2. **Continuing Education**
   - Annual defensive driving refresher
   - New equipment training as needed
   - Safety regulation updates
   - Professional development opportunities

### 5.2 Vehicle Inspection and Maintenance

#### 5.2.1 Daily Inspection Procedures
1. **Pre-Trip Inspection (Every Operation)**
   - Engine compartment: Fluids, belts, hoses, battery
   - Lights: Headlights, taillights, turn signals, hazard lights
   - Tires: Tread depth, pressure, damage, lug nuts
   - Brakes: Air pressure, brake fluid, warning lights
   - Steering: Play, alignment, power steering fluid
   - Mirrors and windshield: Cleanliness, cracks, adjustment

2. **Post-Trip Inspection**
   - Review any problems encountered during operation
   - Check for new damage or mechanical issues
   - Document fuel consumption and mileage
   - Report any safety concerns or needed repairs

3. **Documentation Requirements**
   - Complete DVIR for each vehicle daily
   - Report all defects immediately
   - Obtain supervisor approval before operating defective vehicles
   - Maintain inspection records for DOT compliance

#### 5.2.2 Scheduled Maintenance Program
1. **Preventive Maintenance Schedule**
   - Oil changes every 3,000-5,000 miles
   - Tire rotation every 6,000-8,000 miles
   - Brake inspection every 15,000 miles
   - Comprehensive inspection every 30,000 miles

2. **Annual Inspections**
   - DOT annual inspection for commercial vehicles
   - State safety inspection and emissions testing
   - Insurance carrier safety inspections
   - Third-party mechanical inspections

3. **Maintenance Records**
   - Complete service history for each vehicle
   - Parts and labor cost tracking
   - Warranty information and claim documentation
   - Inspection reports and certifications

### 5.3 Mobile Equipment Operations

#### 5.3.1 Equipment-Specific Procedures
1. **Aerial Lifts and Boom Trucks**
   - Daily inspection of hydraulic systems and controls
   - Verification of ground conditions and overhead clearances
   - Proper use of fall protection and safety harnesses
   - Load capacity verification and weight distribution

2. **Trailers and Towed Equipment**
   - Proper coupling and safety chain attachment
   - Brake system function verification
   - Load securement and weight distribution
   - Turn signal and brake light operation

3. **Material Handling Equipment**
   - Fork lift operation and load handling procedures
   - Conveyor system safety and emergency stops
   - Crane operation and rigging safety
   - Compressed air and hydraulic system safety

#### 5.3.2 Load Securement and Transportation
1. **Cargo Loading Procedures**
   - Verify vehicle load capacity and weight limits
   - Distribute load weight evenly across vehicle
   - Use appropriate tie-downs and securing devices
   - Check load securement every 50 miles or 3 hours

2. **Material-Specific Requirements**
   - Roofing materials: Prevent sliding and shifting
   - Tools and equipment: Secure in tool boxes or compartments
   - Hazardous materials: Follow DOT hazmat regulations
   - Long materials: Use proper flagging and lighting

### 5.4 Emergency Procedures

#### 5.4.1 Accident Response
1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Ensure safety of all persons involved
   - Call emergency services (911) if injuries occur
   - Move vehicles to safe location if possible
   - Set up emergency warning devices

2. **Documentation Requirements**
   - Complete accident report forms
   - Photograph accident scene and vehicle damage
   - Obtain police report number
   - Collect witness information and statements

3. **Notification Procedures**
   - Contact supervisor and safety manager immediately
   - Notify insurance carrier within 24 hours
   - Report to DOT if commercial vehicle involved
   - Coordinate with legal counsel if necessary

#### 5.4.2 Vehicle Breakdown Response
1. **Safety Procedures**
   - Move vehicle to safe location off roadway
   - Activate hazard lights and set warning devices
   - Contact company dispatcher for assistance
   - Remain with vehicle until help arrives

2. **Emergency Equipment**
   - Emergency warning triangles or flares
   - First aid kit and emergency supplies
   - Fire extinguisher appropriate for vehicle type
   - Emergency communication devices

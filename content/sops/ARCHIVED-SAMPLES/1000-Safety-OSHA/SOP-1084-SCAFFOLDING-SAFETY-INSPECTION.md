# SOP-1084: Scaffolding Safety and Inspection

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive safety procedures for scaffolding erection, use, and inspection during roofing operations, ensuring compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart L and protecting workers from falls, structural failure, and other scaffolding-related hazards in Florida's challenging weather conditions.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC scaffolding operations including:
- Frame scaffolds (tube and coupler)
- System scaffolds (manufactured)
- Mobile scaffolds and rolling towers
- Suspended scaffolds and swing stages
- Supported scaffolds and platforms
- Specialty scaffolds for unique applications
- Scaffolding modifications and alterations
- Scaffolding dismantling and removal

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Scaffold**: Any temporary elevated platform and its supporting structure used to support workers, materials, or both.

**Competent Person**: An individual capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards and having authorization to take prompt corrective measures.

**Qualified Person**: A person who possesses a recognized degree, certificate, or professional standing, or has extensive knowledge and experience.

**Platform**: The working surface of a scaffold.

**Guardrail System**: A barrier system designed to prevent falls from the working level of a scaffold.

**Personal Fall Arrest System**: A system used to arrest an employee in a fall from a working level.

## 5. SCAFFOLD PLANNING AND DESIGN

### 5.1 Planning Requirements
```
Pre-Construction Planning:
1. Site assessment and survey
2. Load calculations and requirements
3. Foundation design and preparation
4. Access and egress planning
5. Weather consideration planning
6. Emergency access planning
```

### 5.2 Load Requirements
**Working Loads:**
- Light duty: 25 psf uniformly distributed
- Medium duty: 50 psf uniformly distributed
- Heavy duty: 75 psf uniformly distributed
- Special duty: As designed by qualified person

**Concentrated Loads:**
- Consider point loads and equipment
- Account for dynamic loading factors
- Evaluate load distribution
- Design for maximum anticipated loads

### 5.3 Foundation Requirements
- Stable and rigid support
- Adequate bearing capacity
- Level within 1% of level
- Proper drainage considerations

### 5.4 Florida-Specific Considerations
- Hurricane wind load requirements
- Soil conditions and drainage
- Lightning protection systems
- High humidity effects on materials

## 6. SCAFFOLD ERECTION PROCEDURES

### 6.1 General Erection Requirements
1. **Foundation Preparation**
   - Level and compact base
   - Install base plates and mud sills
   - Verify adequate drainage
   - Check bearing capacity

2. **Frame Assembly**
   - Follow manufacturer instructions
   - Use proper connections and fittings
   - Maintain plumb and level
   - Install bracing as required

3. **Platform Installation**
   - Fully decked or planked platforms
   - Secure platform components
   - Maximum gap: 1 inch
   - Extend over supports 6-18 inches

### 6.2 Fall Protection Requirements
**Guardrail Systems:**
- Top rail height: 38-45 inches
- Midrail height: 19-23 inches
- Toeboards: 3.5 inches minimum
- 200 lb load capacity in any direction

**Personal Fall Arrest:**
- Required when guardrails not feasible
- Proper anchor point selection
- 6-foot maximum free fall
- Competent person selection of equipment

### 6.3 Access Requirements
- Proper stairs or ladders
- Platforms every 35 feet vertically
- Rest platforms for long climbs
- Clear access pathways

## 7. SCAFFOLD TYPES AND REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 Frame Scaffolds
**Assembly Requirements:**
- Properly cross-braced
- Level base plates
- Plumb and square frames
- Secure connections

**Platform Requirements:**
- Full platform coverage
- Proper support spacing
- Secured against movement
- Load rating compliance

### 7.2 System Scaffolds
**Manufacturer Requirements:**
- Follow specific assembly instructions
- Use only compatible components
- Maintain structural integrity
- Regular inspection protocols

### 7.3 Mobile Scaffolds
**Stability Requirements:**
- Height-to-base ratio maximum 4:1
- Wheel locks when occupied
- Level caster requirements
- Outriggers when required

**Movement Procedures:**
- No personnel during movement
- Check for overhead hazards
- Verify surface conditions
- Secure materials before moving

### 7.4 Suspended Scaffolds
**Rigging Requirements:**
- Qualified rigger installation
- Proper suspension points
- Load calculations verified
- Emergency descent procedures

**Safety Systems:**
- Independent lifelines required
- Fall arrest systems
- Emergency communication
- Backup suspension systems

## 9. USE AND MAINTENANCE

### 9.1 Safe Use Procedures
**Weight Limitations:**
- Do not exceed design loads
- Distribute loads evenly
- Avoid concentrated loads
- Consider dynamic factors

**Access Procedures:**
- Use designated access points
- Maintain three points of contact
- Do not climb on guardrails
- Keep access clear

**Material Handling:**
- Use material hoists when possible
- Secure loose materials
- Avoid overloading platforms
- Use proper lifting techniques

### 9.2 Prohibited Uses
- Scaffold movement with personnel
- Use of damaged components
- Alteration without authorization
- Use during severe weather

### 9.3 Maintenance Requirements
- Regular component inspection
- Prompt repair or replacement
- Proper storage procedures
- Inventory management

## 11. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### 11.1 Structural Failure
1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Evacuate scaffold immediately
   - Establish exclusion zone
   - Account for all personnel
   - Call emergency services

2. **Investigation Procedures**
   - Secure scene for investigation
   - Document conditions
   - Photograph evidence
   - Coordinate with authorities

### 11.2 Weather Emergencies
**High Wind Procedures:**
- Monitor wind speeds continuously
- Cease operations at 25 mph
- Secure loose materials
- Consider scaffold bracing

**Lightning Procedures:**
- Evacuate scaffold when lightning within 6 miles
- Stay away from metal components
- Wait 30 minutes after last lightning
- Inspect for damage before re-use

### 11.3 Fall Incidents
- Provide immediate medical attention
- Do not move injured person
- Secure rescue area
- Document incident thoroughly

# SOP-5020: Insurance Program Management and Compliance

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for managing insurance programs, ensuring adequate coverage, maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements, and protecting Florida First Roofing LLC from financial losses through effective risk management and insurance coordination.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in insurance management, including executives, risk managers, project managers, safety coordinators, and administrative staff responsible for insurance compliance, claims management, and vendor coordination activities.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Risk Manager**: Oversee insurance program management and ensure adequate coverage levels
- **Insurance Coordinator**: Manage policy renewals, claims processing, and vendor relationships
- **Safety Manager**: Coordinate safety programs with insurance requirements and loss prevention
- **Project Managers**: Ensure project-specific insurance compliance and certificate management
- **Accounting Manager**: Track insurance costs and coordinate premium payments
- **Legal Counsel**: Review insurance contracts and handle complex coverage disputes

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Insurance Management Systems:
- Insurance policy management and tracking software
- Certificate of insurance generation and verification systems
- Claims management and documentation platforms
- Risk assessment and loss control monitoring tools
- Vendor insurance verification and compliance tracking systems

### Documentation Systems:
- Policy documentation and contract storage systems
- Claims documentation and correspondence tracking
- Safety training and incident reporting integration
- Financial tracking for premiums and deductibles
- Regulatory compliance and audit preparation tools

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Ensure all insurance requirements support comprehensive safety programs
- Coordinate safety training with insurance loss control requirements
- Maintain documentation linking safety performance to insurance costs
- Implement incident reporting procedures that support insurance claims
- Regular review of insurance requirements for high-risk activities and locations

- [ ] Review current insurance coverage and policy expiration dates

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Insurance Program Design and Coverage Analysis
1. **Risk Assessment and Coverage Evaluation**:
   - **Business Risk Analysis**: Evaluate operational risks specific to roofing industry in Florida
   - **Coverage Gap Identification**: Assess current policies for adequacy and identify coverage gaps
   - **Regulatory Requirement Review**: Ensure compliance with Florida construction and workers' compensation requirements
   - **Project-Specific Coverage**: Analyze insurance needs for specialized projects and high-value contracts
   - **Vendor and Subcontractor Requirements**: Establish minimum insurance standards for all business partners

2. **Insurance Portfolio Development**:
   - **General Liability**: Comprehensive coverage for bodily injury, property damage, and professional liability
   - **Workers' Compensation**: Florida-compliant coverage for all employees and subcontractors
   - **Commercial Auto**: Fleet coverage for vehicles and equipment transportation
   - **Property Insurance**: Coverage for buildings, equipment, and business personal property
   - **Umbrella/Excess Liability**: Additional coverage layers for catastrophic loss protection

3. **Carrier Selection and Policy Negotiation**:
   - **Carrier Financial Stability**: Evaluate insurance company financial ratings and claim-paying ability
   - **Industry Expertise**: Select carriers with construction and roofing industry experience
   - **Premium Competitiveness**: Obtain competitive quotes while maintaining coverage quality
   - **Loss Control Services**: Evaluate carrier loss control and risk management support services
   - **Claims Handling Reputation**: Assess carrier claims handling efficiency and customer service

### Phase 2: Policy Administration and Compliance Management
1. **Policy Implementation and Documentation**:
   - **Policy Review and Acceptance**: Thoroughly review policy terms, conditions, and exclusions
   - **Certificate Production**: Generate certificates of insurance for customers and vendors
   - **Additional Insured Management**: Process additional insured requests and endorsements
   - **Policy Storage and Access**: Maintain secure, accessible storage for all insurance documentation
   - **Renewal Calendar Management**: Track expiration dates and coordinate renewal processes

2. **Compliance Monitoring and Verification**:
   - **Regulatory Compliance**: Ensure policies meet Florida Department of Financial Services requirements
   - **Contract Compliance**: Verify insurance requirements are met for all customer contracts
   - **Vendor Insurance Verification**: Monitor subcontractor and vendor insurance compliance
   - **Licensing Coordination**: Coordinate insurance requirements with professional licensing
   - **Audit Preparation**: Maintain documentation for insurance and regulatory audits

3. **Premium Management and Cost Control**:
   - **Payment Schedule Coordination**: Manage premium payment schedules and cash flow planning
   - **Experience Rating Monitoring**: Track workers' compensation experience modification factors
   - **Loss Control Implementation**: Implement safety programs to reduce claims and control costs
   - **Deductible Management**: Manage self-insured retentions and deductible obligations
   - **Cost Allocation**: Allocate insurance costs to projects and departments appropriately

### Phase 3: Claims Management and Loss Control
1. **Incident Response and Claim Reporting**:
   - **Immediate Response**: Establish 24-hour incident reporting and initial response procedures
   - **Claim Notification**: Report claims to carriers within required timeframes
   - **Documentation Collection**: Gather comprehensive incident documentation and evidence
   - **Witness Coordination**: Identify and coordinate with witnesses and involved parties
   - **Medical Coordination**: Coordinate medical treatment for workers' compensation claims

2. **Claims Investigation and Management**:
   - **Carrier Cooperation**: Work with insurance company adjusters and investigators
   - **Legal Coordination**: Coordinate with legal counsel for complex or disputed claims
   - **Settlement Evaluation**: Evaluate settlement offers and negotiate appropriate resolutions
   - **Return-to-Work Programs**: Implement modified duty and return-to-work procedures
   - **Subrogation Coordination**: Pursue subrogation opportunities and coordinate with carriers

3. **Loss Control and Prevention Programs**:
   - **Safety Program Integration**: Align safety training with insurance loss control requirements
   - **Incident Analysis**: Analyze claims patterns and implement preventive measures
   - **Risk Mitigation**: Implement specific risk mitigation strategies based on claims experience
   - **Training Coordination**: Coordinate safety training with insurance carrier resources
   - **Performance Monitoring**: Track safety performance metrics and insurance cost correlation

### Phase 4: Vendor and Contract Insurance Management
1. **Subcontractor Insurance Requirements**:
   - **Minimum Coverage Standards**: Establish minimum insurance requirements for all subcontractors
   - **Certificate Verification**: Verify subcontractor insurance certificates and policy validity
   - **Additional Insured Status**: Ensure Florida First Roofing is named as additional insured
   - **Waiver of Subrogation**: Obtain appropriate waivers of subrogation from subcontractors
   - **Coverage Monitoring**: Monitor ongoing compliance throughout subcontractor relationships

2. **Customer Contract Insurance Coordination**:
   - **Contract Review**: Review customer contracts for insurance and indemnification requirements
   - **Certificate Production**: Provide certificates of insurance as required by contracts
   - **Coverage Verification**: Ensure adequate coverage for contract-specific requirements
   - **Indemnification Analysis**: Evaluate indemnification clauses and insurance adequacy
   - **Risk Transfer**: Implement appropriate risk transfer mechanisms through contracts

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Policy compliance: 100% compliance with regulatory and contractual insurance requirements
- Claims response time: 24-hour claim reporting to carriers for all incidents
- Certificate accuracy: 99% accuracy in certificate of insurance production and delivery
- Vendor compliance: 95% of vendors maintain required insurance coverage throughout contracts
- Cost effectiveness: Annual insurance cost increases limited to 5% above inflation

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Coverage disputes**: Maintain detailed policy documentation and coordinate with legal counsel
- **Claim delays**: Provide complete documentation and maintain regular carrier communication
- **Vendor non-compliance**: Implement systematic monitoring and enforcement procedures
- **Premium increases**: Implement loss control measures and evaluate alternative coverage options
- **Certificate delays**: Streamline certificate production and maintain current vendor information

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Review policy language and coordinate with carriers for coverage interpretation
2. Escalate claim issues to carrier management and consider legal assistance when needed
3. Implement vendor monitoring systems and enforcement procedures for insurance compliance
4. Analyze loss experience and implement targeted risk reduction strategies
5. Evaluate market alternatives and negotiate with carriers for competitive pricing

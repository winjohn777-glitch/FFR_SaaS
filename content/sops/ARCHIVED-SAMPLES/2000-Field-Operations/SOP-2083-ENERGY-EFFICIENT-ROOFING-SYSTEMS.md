# SOP-2083: Energy-Efficient Roofing Systems

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for installing energy-efficient roofing systems on all Florida First Roofing LLC projects, ensuring optimal thermal performance, energy savings, and code compliance while addressing Florida's challenging climate conditions including intense solar radiation, high temperatures, and humidity.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all energy-efficient roofing system installations including:
- Cool roof systems and materials
- Reflective roofing technologies
- Advanced insulation systems
- Radiant barrier installations
- Solar reflective surfaces
- Thermal bridging reduction
- Energy recovery ventilation
- Green roof energy systems

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Cool Roof**: A roofing system designed to reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat than standard roofs.

**Solar Reflectance**: The fraction of solar energy reflected by a surface.

**Thermal Emittance**: The ability of a surface to release absorbed heat.

**Solar Reflectance Index (SRI)**: A measure combining solar reflectance and thermal emittance.

**R-Value**: Thermal resistance to heat flow through a material.

**U-Factor**: Overall coefficient of heat transmission through a building component.

**Thermal Bridge**: A path for heat transfer through building assemblies.

**Radiant Barrier**: A reflective material that reduces radiant heat transfer.

- Cause: Discontinuous insulation
- Solution: Provide continuous insulation
- Prevention: Careful design and installation

- Cause: Poor air barrier installation
- Solution: Seal all penetrations
- Prevention: Continuous air barrier

- Cause: Vapor barrier placement

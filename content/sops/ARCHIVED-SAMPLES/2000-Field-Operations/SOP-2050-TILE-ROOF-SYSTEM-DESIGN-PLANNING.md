# SOP-2050: Tile Roof System Design and Planning

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for tile roof system design, material selection, structural evaluation, and installation planning to ensure proper performance, hurricane resistance, and compliance with Florida Building Code requirements.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in tile roof system design, engineering evaluation, material selection, and installation planning for residential and commercial projects throughout Florida's hurricane-prone regions.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Design Engineer**: Structural analysis and system design specifications
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate design process and client requirements
- **Tile Specialist**: Material selection and installation method determination
- **Structural Engineer**: Load analysis and framing adequacy assessment
- **Building Code Specialist**: Compliance verification and permit coordination

- Consider wind uplift resistance and hurricane performance requirements

- Plan for safe material handling and distribution procedures
- Consider weather exposure and installation timing requirements

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Initial Assessment and Requirements Analysis
1. **Structural Evaluation and Load Analysis**:
   - Calculate dead load capacity for tile weight (8-12 lbs/sq ft)
   - Analyze existing framing for adequacy and reinforcement needs
   - Evaluate roof slope requirements (minimum 2.5:12 for most tiles)
   - Assess wind uplift resistance requirements per hurricane zone
   - Document structural deficiencies requiring reinforcement

2. **Client Requirements and Aesthetic Planning**:
   - Determine tile style preferences (barrel, flat, slate simulation)
   - Select color options and architectural compatibility
   - Review energy efficiency requirements and cool roof standards
   - Assess budget constraints and value engineering opportunities
   - Coordinate with architectural design and neighborhood restrictions

3. **Site Analysis and Constraint Assessment**:
   - Evaluate roof geometry and complexity factors
   - Assess access limitations for material delivery and installation
   - Identify utility conflicts and clearance requirements
   - Review drainage patterns and gutter system integration
   - Document environmental factors affecting installation

### Phase 2: System Design and Material Selection
1. **Tile Type Selection and Specification**:
   - **Concrete Tiles**: Evaluate weight, color retention, and durability
   - **Clay Tiles**: Assess natural material properties and longevity
   - **Composite Tiles**: Review synthetic material performance and cost
   - Verify Miami-Dade hurricane approval and wind resistance ratings
   - Confirm tile availability and delivery lead times

2. **Underlayment and Attachment System Design**:
   - Specify appropriate underlayment type (synthetic vs. felt)
   - Design tile attachment method (mechanical vs. mortar set)
   - Plan for enhanced attachment in high-wind zones
   - Select appropriate fasteners and attachment hardware
   - Design vapor barrier and ventilation systems

3. **Drainage and Water Management Planning**:
   - Design valley systems for tile roof compatibility
   - Plan ridge and hip details for proper water shedding
   - Specify gutter and downspout sizing for tile runoff characteristics
   - Design penetration flashing and detail work
   - Plan for ice dam prevention in applicable areas

### Phase 3: Technical Specifications and Documentation
1. **Engineering Calculations and Analysis**:
   - Perform wind load calculations per ASCE 7 standards
   - Calculate fastener requirements and spacing specifications
   - Analyze thermal movement and expansion joint needs
   - Verify seismic considerations for applicable regions
   - Document structural reinforcement requirements

2. **Installation Method Specification**:
   - Specify tile installation pattern and layout requirements
   - Detail attachment methods and fastener specifications
   - Plan for field cutting and custom fitting procedures
   - Specify mortar requirements for applicable applications
   - Design quality control checkpoints and inspection procedures

3. **Detail Design and Custom Solutions**:
   - Design ridge and hip cap systems
   - Plan penetration details and flashing integration
   - Specify starter course and eave protection methods
   - Design snow retention systems where applicable
   - Plan for maintenance access and safety considerations

### Phase 4: Compliance and Approval Documentation
1. **Building Code Compliance Verification**:
   - Verify Florida Building Code Chapter 15 compliance
   - Confirm wind resistance requirements for project location
   - Document energy code compliance for applicable projects
   - Verify accessibility requirements for maintenance
   - Confirm fire resistance ratings where required

2. **Permit Application Preparation**:
   - Prepare detailed drawings and specifications for permit submittal
   - Include structural calculations and engineering stamps
   - Provide manufacturer specifications and approval documentation
   - Submit wind load calculations and hurricane compliance verification
   - Coordinate with building department review process

3. **Quality Assurance and Installation Planning**:
   - Develop installation sequence and timeline
   - Plan material staging and delivery coordination
   - Specify quality control inspection requirements
   - Design weather protection and work stoppage protocols
   - Plan for warranty registration and documentation

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Structural adequacy: 100% compliance with dead and live load requirements
- Wind resistance: Compliance with Miami-Dade hurricane approval standards
- Installation specifications: Detailed drawings for all roof components
- Code compliance: 100% compliance with Florida Building Code requirements
- Documentation quality: Complete and accurate technical specifications

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Structural inadequacy**: Design reinforcement solutions or alternative systems
- **Slope limitations**: Evaluate alternative tile types or roof modifications
- **Access constraints**: Plan alternative installation methods or equipment
- **Code compliance issues**: Coordinate with building department for solutions
- **Material availability**: Identify approved alternatives and lead time adjustments

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Identify design constraints and technical limitations
2. Evaluate alternative solutions and system modifications
3. Coordinate with structural engineer for reinforcement options
4. Consult with building department for compliance alternatives
5. Update design documentation and obtain required approvals

# SOP-2095: Coastal Construction Corrosion Protection

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for protecting roofing systems and components from corrosion in coastal environments. This procedure addresses the unique challenges of salt air exposure, high humidity, and extreme weather conditions typical of Florida's coastal regions.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC projects within:
- Coastal zones (within 3 miles of saltwater)
- High Velocity Hurricane Zones (HVHZ)
- Areas with elevated salt spray exposure
- Marine environments and waterfront properties
- Industrial coastal areas with additional corrosive agents
- Historic coastal structures requiring preservation

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Corrosion**: Deterioration of materials due to chemical or electrochemical reaction with environment
- **Salt Spray**: Airborne salt particles carried by wind from ocean/gulf waters
- **Galvanic Corrosion**: Electrochemical corrosion between dissimilar metals
- **Atmospheric Corrosion**: Corrosion caused by moisture and atmospheric contaminants
- **Marine Grade Materials**: Materials specifically designed for saltwater environment exposure
- **Sacrificial Anode**: Metal component designed to corrode preferentially to protect other metals

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Environmental Assessment and Planning

#### 5.1.1 Coastal Environment Analysis
1. **Salt Exposure Assessment**
   - Measure distance from saltwater sources
   - Evaluate prevailing wind patterns and salt spray intensity
   - Assess microclimate conditions and local environmental factors
   - Document seasonal variations in salt exposure

2. **Atmospheric Conditions Evaluation**
   - Monitor humidity levels and temperature variations
   - Assess air quality and additional corrosive agents
   - Evaluate building height and exposure to salt-laden air
   - Consider urban and industrial pollution effects

3. **Historical Performance Review**
   - Research corrosion patterns in local area
   - Review performance of existing buildings and materials
   - Identify common failure modes and problem areas
   - Document lessons learned from previous projects

#### 5.1.2 Material Selection Criteria
1. **Corrosion Resistance Requirements**
   - Select materials based on expected service life
   - Evaluate cost-benefit of various protection levels
   - Consider maintenance requirements and accessibility
   - Plan for future replacement and upgrade needs

2. **Compatibility Assessment**
   - Ensure compatibility between different materials
   - Plan galvanic isolation where dissimilar metals contact
   - Evaluate thermal expansion compatibility
   - Assess chemical compatibility of all components

### 5.2 Material Selection and Specification

#### 5.2.1 Fasteners and Hardware
1. **Stainless Steel Components**
   - Use 316 stainless steel for highest corrosion resistance
   - Select appropriate grade based on chloride exposure level
   - Verify composition and certification for marine environments
   - Ensure proper passivation and surface finish

2. **Galvanized Steel Options**
   - Use hot-dip galvanized steel with heavy coating (G-185 minimum)
   - Apply additional protective coatings where specified
   - Inspect galvanizing quality and coverage before installation
   - Plan for galvanic compatibility with other materials

3. **Aluminum Components**
   - Select marine-grade aluminum alloys (5000 or 6000 series)
   - Use anodized or powder-coated finishes for additional protection
   - Install galvanic isolation between aluminum and steel
   - Verify alloy composition and surface treatment specifications

#### 5.2.2 Roofing Materials
1. **Metal Roofing Systems**
   - Use standing seam systems to minimize fastener exposure
   - Select appropriate metal type and coating for environment
   - Install galvanic isolation between dissimilar metals
   - Apply protective coatings to cut edges and field modifications

2. **Membrane Roofing Systems**
   - Choose UV-resistant and salt-tolerant membrane materials
   - Use corrosion-resistant fasteners and plates
   - Apply protective coatings to exposed metal components
   - Install enhanced wind resistance features

#### 5.2.3 Flashing and Trim Materials
1. **Base Metal Selection**
   - Use lead-coated copper, stainless steel, or marine-grade aluminum
   - Avoid zinc and galvanized steel in high-exposure areas
   - Select appropriate thickness for durability
   - Plan proper expansion joint details

2. **Protective Coatings**
   - Apply marine-grade paints and primers
   - Use multi-coat systems for enhanced protection
   - Select UV-resistant and salt-tolerant coating systems
   - Plan maintenance coating schedules

### 5.3 Surface Preparation and Protection

#### 5.3.1 Metal Surface Preparation
1. **Cleaning Procedures**
   - Remove all mill scale, rust, and surface contaminants
   - Use appropriate abrasive blasting or chemical cleaning
   - Achieve specified surface profile for coating adhesion
   - Protect cleaned surfaces from recontamination

2. **Primer Application**
   - Apply zinc-rich or epoxy primers as specified
   - Maintain proper film thickness throughout application
   - Allow proper curing time before topcoat application
   - Protect primer coat from damage during construction

#### 5.3.2 Coating Systems
1. **Multi-Coat Systems**
   - Apply primer, intermediate, and topcoat as specified
   - Maintain proper film thickness for each coat
   - Allow proper curing time between coats
   - Inspect for holidays and defects between coats

2. **Specialized Coatings**
   - Use polyurethane or fluoropolymer topcoats for maximum durability
   - Apply ceramic or zinc-filled coatings for enhanced protection
   - Use sacrificial coatings where appropriate
   - Apply anti-corrosion wax or grease to threaded connections

### 5.4 Installation Procedures

#### 5.4.1 Galvanic Isolation Installation
1. **Isolation Material Selection**
   - Use plastic or rubber isolators between dissimilar metals
   - Select materials compatible with both metals
   - Ensure adequate thickness for electrical isolation
   - Verify long-term stability in marine environment

2. **Installation Techniques**
   - Install isolation materials during component assembly
   - Ensure complete coverage of contact areas
   - Maintain isolation integrity during fastener installation
   - Verify isolation effectiveness with electrical testing

#### 5.4.2 Protective Coating Application
1. **Environmental Control**
   - Monitor temperature and humidity during application
   - Avoid application during high wind or salt spray conditions
   - Provide temporary shelters for coating application
   - Allow extended curing time in high humidity conditions

2. **Quality Control**
   - Use wet film thickness gauges during application
   - Test dry film thickness with electronic gauges
   - Perform adhesion tests before proceeding
   - Inspect for holidays using electronic detectors

### 5.5 Specialized Protection Systems

#### 5.5.1 Cathodic Protection
1. **Sacrificial Anode Systems**
   - Install zinc or magnesium anodes where appropriate
   - Connect anodes to protected metal components
   - Monitor anode consumption and replacement needs
   - Document electrical connections and anode locations

2. **Impressed Current Systems**
   - Install where large structures require protection
   - Monitor system operation and current output
   - Maintain control systems and power supplies
   - Document system performance and maintenance

#### 5.5.2 Barrier Protection
1. **Physical Barriers**
   - Install shields to reduce direct salt spray exposure
   - Use windscreens and deflectors where appropriate
   - Apply temporary protection during construction
   - Design permanent barriers for high-exposure areas

2. **Chemical Barriers**
   - Apply corrosion inhibitors to enclosed spaces
   - Use desiccants in sealed component assemblies
   - Install vapor barriers to reduce moisture penetration
   - Monitor and replace chemical protection systems

## 7. PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

### 7.1 Material Performance
- No visible corrosion for minimum 10 years in normal coastal exposure
- Coating adhesion and integrity maintained per ASTM standards
- Galvanic isolation effectiveness verified by electrical testing
- Fastener and hardware performance meets marine-grade standards

### 7.2 Installation Quality
- 100% coverage of specified protective coatings
- Proper galvanic isolation at all dissimilar metal connections
- Surface preparation meets specified standards before coating
- Complete documentation of all protection systems

### 7.3 Long-Term Durability
- Scheduled maintenance extends protection system life
- Performance meets or exceeds manufacturer warranties
- Cost-effective protection over building lifecycle
- Compatibility with building maintenance and operation

# SOP-2082: Cool Roof Technology Installation

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for installing cool roof technology systems that reduce building energy consumption, improve occupant comfort, and contribute to urban heat island reduction. This procedure ensures compliance with Florida energy codes and green building standards.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC cool roof installations including:
- High-reflectance roofing materials
- Thermal emittance enhancement systems
- Reflective coating applications
- Cool roof membrane installations
- Energy-efficient roofing upgrades
- Green building certification projects

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Cool Roof**: Roofing system designed to reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat than standard roofs
- **Solar Reflectance**: Fraction of solar energy reflected by a surface (measured 0-1 scale)
- **Thermal Emittance**: Ability of a surface to radiate absorbed heat (measured 0-1 scale)
- **Solar Reflectance Index (SRI)**: Measure combining reflectance and emittance properties
- **Urban Heat Island**: Metropolitan areas significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas
- **ENERGY STAR**: EPA program identifying energy-efficient products

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Cool Roof System Selection

#### 5.1.1 Performance Requirements Assessment
1. **Climate Analysis**
   - Evaluate local climate conditions and cooling degree days
   - Assess solar radiation levels and intensity patterns
   - Consider humidity and precipitation effects
   - Analyze seasonal temperature variations

2. **Building Energy Analysis**
   - Review existing building energy performance
   - Calculate potential cooling load reductions
   - Assess HVAC system compatibility and benefits
   - Determine return on investment projections

3. **Performance Targets**
   - Initial Solar Reflectance: Minimum 0.70 for low-slope roofs
   - Thermal Emittance: Minimum 0.75 for optimal performance
   - Solar Reflectance Index: Minimum 82 for ENERGY STAR qualification
   - Aged Solar Reflectance: Minimum 0.55 after 3 years

#### 5.1.2 Material Selection Criteria
1. **Reflective Materials**
   - White thermoplastic olefin (TPO) membranes
   - White EPDM rubber membranes with reflective coatings
   - Modified bitumen with reflective granules
   - Metal roofing with high-reflectance coatings

2. **Cool Roof Coatings**
   - Acrylic elastomeric coatings with ceramic microspheres
   - Silicone-based reflective coatings
   - Polyurethane coatings with infrared-reflective pigments
   - Specialty cool roof paint systems

### 5.2 Surface Preparation for Cool Roof Systems

#### 5.2.1 Substrate Evaluation and Preparation
1. **Surface Condition Assessment**
   - Inspect existing roof for structural adequacy
   - Evaluate surface cleanliness and contamination
   - Check for proper drainage and ponding issues
   - Assess compatibility with cool roof materials

2. **Cleaning Procedures**
   - Power wash surface to remove dirt, debris, and biological growth
   - Use appropriate cleaning solutions for stubborn stains
   - Allow complete drying before application
   - Verify surface pH compatibility with cool roof materials

3. **Surface Repairs**
   - Repair all cracks, holes, and membrane defects
   - Apply primer where required for adhesion
   - Install proper flashing and detail work
   - Ensure smooth, even surface for optimal performance

#### 5.2.2 Environmental Conditions Monitoring
1. **Weather Requirements**
   - Ambient temperature: 50°F minimum, 95°F maximum
   - Relative humidity: Less than 85% during application
   - Wind speed: Less than 25 mph sustained
   - No precipitation for 4 hours before and after application

2. **Surface Temperature Monitoring**
   - Surface temperature: Within manufacturer specifications
   - Monitor thermal expansion and contraction
   - Avoid application during thermal shock conditions
   - Allow proper cool-down time on hot surfaces

### 5.3 Cool Roof Installation Procedures

#### 5.3.1 Reflective Membrane Installation
1. **TPO/EPDM Cool Membrane Installation**
   - Follow manufacturer's installation specifications
   - Use white or light-colored membrane materials
   - Ensure proper seam welding or adhesive bonding
   - Install with adequate thermal expansion accommodation

2. **Quality Control for Membrane Systems**
   - Test all seams for proper bonding
   - Verify membrane reflectance meets specifications
   - Check for proper adhesion to substrate
   - Ensure complete coverage without gaps or overlaps

#### 5.3.2 Cool Roof Coating Application
1. **Primer Application (When Required)**
   - Apply primer using appropriate method (spray, roller, brush)
   - Achieve specified wet film thickness
   - Allow proper curing time before topcoat application
   - Verify primer coverage and adhesion

2. **Base Coat Application**
   - Apply base coat at manufacturer-specified rate
   - Maintain consistent wet film thickness
   - Use appropriate application equipment for material type
   - Monitor application rate and coverage patterns

3. **Topcoat Application**
   - Apply topcoat to achieve specified dry film thickness
   - Ensure uniform coverage without streaks or holidays
   - Back-roll spray applications for consistent texture
   - Monitor reflectance properties during application

#### 5.3.3 Specialized Installation Techniques
1. **Spray Application**
   - Use airless spray equipment for large areas
   - Maintain proper spray pressure and tip size
   - Control overspray and wind drift
   - Achieve consistent mil thickness throughout

2. **Roller Application**
   - Use high-quality synthetic rollers
   - Maintain consistent application pressure
   - Work in manageable sections for uniform coverage
   - Back-brush edges and detail areas

### 5.4 Performance Testing and Verification

#### 5.4.1 Field Testing Procedures
1. **Solar Reflectance Testing**
   - Use calibrated solar reflectometer
   - Test multiple locations across roof surface
   - Document readings and average values
   - Compare results to specifications

2. **Thermal Emittance Testing**
   - Use portable emissometer when available
   - Conduct tests under appropriate conditions
   - Record ambient and surface temperatures
   - Document compliance with requirements

#### 5.4.2 Quality Assurance Testing
1. **Adhesion Testing**
   - Perform tape pull tests on coating systems
   - Check membrane attachment and seaming
   - Verify primer adhesion where applicable
   - Document test results and compliance

2. **Thickness Measurements**
   - Use wet film thickness gauges during application
   - Measure dry film thickness after curing
   - Verify compliance with specifications
   - Document thickness variation across roof

### 5.5 Energy Performance Monitoring

#### 5.5.1 Baseline Energy Measurement
1. **Pre-Installation Energy Audit**
   - Record existing building energy consumption
   - Document HVAC operating costs and patterns
   - Measure roof surface temperatures
   - Establish baseline for comparison

2. **Post-Installation Monitoring**
   - Monitor building energy consumption changes
   - Track HVAC runtime and efficiency improvements
   - Measure roof surface temperature reductions
   - Calculate actual energy savings achieved

#### 5.5.2 Long-Term Performance Tracking
1. **Annual Performance Assessment**
   - Measure solar reflectance degradation over time
   - Monitor coating condition and performance
   - Track energy savings maintenance
   - Document any maintenance requirements

2. **Performance Reporting**
   - Provide annual performance reports to customers
   - Document energy savings and cost benefits
   - Report environmental benefits achieved
   - Recommend maintenance schedules

## 7. PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

### 7.1 Installation Quality
- Achieve specified solar reflectance values (minimum 0.70 initial)
- Maintain proper film thickness throughout application
- Complete coverage without holidays or thin spots
- Professional appearance with uniform color and texture

### 7.2 Energy Performance
- Demonstrate measurable reduction in cooling energy use
- Achieve surface temperature reductions of 50-80°F
- Maintain performance standards for minimum 10 years
- Meet or exceed projected energy savings calculations

### 7.3 Environmental Performance
- Contribute to urban heat island reduction
- Support green building certification requirements
- Reduce building carbon footprint
- Comply with municipal climate action goals

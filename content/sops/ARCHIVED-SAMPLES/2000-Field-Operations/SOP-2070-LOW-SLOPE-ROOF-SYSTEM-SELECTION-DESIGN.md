# SOP-2070: Low-Slope Roof System Selection and Design

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for low-slope roof system selection, design specification, performance evaluation, and installation planning to ensure proper drainage, weather protection, and compliance with Florida Building Code requirements for commercial and residential applications.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in low-slope roof system design, material selection, drainage design, and installation planning for roofs with slopes less than 2:12 throughout Florida's hurricane-prone regions.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Design Engineer**: System design and drainage calculations
- **Roofing Specialist**: Membrane system selection and specification
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate design requirements and client specifications
- **Structural Engineer**: Load analysis and deck adequacy assessment
- **Code Compliance Officer**: Building code verification and energy compliance

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Evaluate fall protection requirements for low-slope access
- Plan for safe drainage system installation and maintenance access
- Consider hot surface conditions and thermal protection requirements
- Assess electrical hazards around HVAC and electrical equipment
- Plan for proper ventilation during adhesive and solvent applications
- Implement fire safety protocols for torch-applied systems

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: System Type Selection and Analysis
1. **Low-Slope Roofing System Types Evaluation**:
   - **Built-Up Roofing (BUR)**: Multi-ply systems with gravel or smooth surface
   - **Modified Bitumen**: APP and SBS polymer modified systems
   - **Single-Ply Membranes**: EPDM, TPO, PVC, and KEE systems
   - **Spray Polyurethane Foam (SPF)**: Seamless foam and coating systems
   - **Fluid-Applied Systems**: Liquid membranes and restoration coatings

2. **Performance Requirements Assessment**:
   - **Wind Uplift Resistance**: FM and UL uplift classifications for hurricane zones
   - **Fire Resistance**: Class A fire ratings and assembly requirements
   - **Energy Performance**: Cool roof requirements and thermal resistance
   - **Durability**: Expected service life and maintenance requirements
   - **Environmental Resistance**: UV, ozone, and chemical exposure

3. **Application Suitability Analysis**:
   - Building type and occupancy considerations
   - Traffic and maintenance access requirements
   - Equipment mounting and penetration density
   - Aesthetic requirements and architectural compatibility
   - Budget constraints and lifecycle cost analysis

### Phase 2: Drainage Design and Water Management
1. **Drainage System Design**:
   - Calculate roof area and rainfall intensity for Florida regions
   - Design primary and secondary drainage systems per code requirements
   - Size roof drains, scuppers, and overflow systems
   - Plan for proper slope and water flow patterns
   - Design gutter and downspout systems for low-slope applications

2. **Slope and Deck Preparation**:
   - Specify minimum slopes (Â¼" per foot) for positive drainage
   - Design tapered insulation systems for slope creation
   - Plan for cricket and saddle installation at equipment
   - Specify deck preparation and substrate requirements
   - Design for thermal movement and expansion joints

3. **Penetration and Equipment Planning**:
   - Plan for HVAC equipment and electrical penetrations
   - Design curbed penetrations and equipment supports
   - Specify flashing systems for various penetration types
   - Plan for future equipment access and maintenance
   - Coordinate with mechanical and electrical systems

### Phase 3: Membrane System Design and Specification
1. **Single-Ply Membrane Systems**:
   - **EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer)**: Ballasted, adhered, or mechanically attached
   - **TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin)**: Heat-welded seams and energy efficiency
   - **PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)**: Chemical resistance and weld strength
   - Specify membrane thickness and reinforcement requirements
   - Design attachment methods and wind uplift resistance

2. **Modified Bitumen Systems**:
   - **APP (Atactic Polypropylene)**: Torch-applied or hot asphalt installation
   - **SBS (Styrene-Butadiene-Styrene)**: Cold-applied or hot asphalt systems
   - Design multi-ply configurations and base sheet requirements
   - Specify surface treatments and granule options
   - Plan for thermal expansion and movement accommodation

3. **Built-Up Roofing Systems**:
   - Design ply configuration and bitumen specifications
   - Specify reinforcement materials and application methods
   - Plan for aggregate surfacing or smooth surface options
   - Design vapor retarder and insulation systems
   - Specify quality control and installation procedures

### Phase 4: Insulation and Energy Compliance
1. **Thermal Insulation Design**:
   - Calculate R-value requirements per Florida Energy Code
   - Select appropriate insulation types for application
   - Design for thermal bridging and continuous insulation
   - Plan for moisture control and vapor retarder placement
   - Specify attachment methods and wind uplift resistance

2. **Cool Roof Compliance**:
   - Specify solar reflectance and thermal emittance requirements
   - Select ENERGY STAR qualified roofing materials
   - Calculate energy savings and utility rebate eligibility
   - Plan for long-term reflectance maintenance
   - Document compliance with green building standards

3. **Ventilation and Moisture Control**:
   - Design roof ventilation systems for moisture control
   - Plan for vapor retarder placement and continuity
   - Specify air sealing requirements and details
   - Design for condensation prevention and control
   - Plan for humidity and temperature monitoring systems

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Drainage design: Zero standing water after 48 hours of rainfall cessation
- Wind uplift resistance: Compliance with FM or UL uplift classifications
- Energy compliance: 100% compliance with Florida Energy Code requirements
- Membrane integrity: Seamless waterproof membrane installation
- Installation quality: Compliance with manufacturer and industry standards

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Ponding water**: Re-evaluate drainage design and slope requirements
- **Wind uplift problems**: Review attachment specifications and upgrade systems
- **Thermal movement issues**: Assess expansion joint design and accommodation
- **Energy code compliance**: Review insulation and cool roof specifications
- **Penetration failures**: Redesign flashing and sealing systems

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Identify design deficiency and assess impact on performance
2. Consult with structural engineer for load-related modifications
3. Review manufacturer specifications and warranty requirements
4. Coordinate with building department for code compliance issues
5. Update design documentation and obtain required approvals

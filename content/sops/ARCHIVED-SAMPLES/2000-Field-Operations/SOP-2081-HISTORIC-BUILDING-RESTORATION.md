# SOP-2081: Historic Building Roofing Restoration

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes comprehensive protocols for roofing restoration work on historic buildings to preserve architectural integrity while meeting modern performance standards. This procedure ensures compliance with historic preservation guidelines, building codes, and tax credit requirements.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all Florida First Roofing LLC projects involving:
- Buildings listed on National Register of Historic Places
- Local historic district properties
- Buildings seeking historic tax credits
- Architecturally significant structures
- Preservation and rehabilitation projects
- Cultural resource protection requirements

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **Historic Preservation**: Protection and restoration of buildings, objects, landscapes, or other artifacts of historical significance
- **Secretary of Interior's Standards**: Federal guidelines for historic preservation projects
- **Contributing Structure**: Building that adds to historical significance of a district
- **Character-Defining Features**: Physical elements that convey a property's historic identity
- **Rehabilitation**: Process of returning a property to a state of utility through repair or alteration
- **Historic Tax Credits**: Federal and state incentives for qualifying historic preservation projects

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Pre-Project Research and Planning

#### 5.1.1 Historical Documentation Research
1. **Archival Research**
   - Review original building plans and specifications
   - Research construction history and modifications
   - Study period photographs and documentation
   - Investigate original material sources and techniques

2. **Architectural Analysis**
   - Document existing conditions with measured drawings
   - Identify character-defining features
   - Assess structural and material conditions
   - Determine appropriate level of intervention

3. **Regulatory Compliance Assessment**
   - Verify historic designation status
   - Review applicable preservation standards
   - Identify required approvals and permits
   - Assess tax credit eligibility and requirements

#### 5.1.2 Condition Assessment and Documentation
1. **Comprehensive Survey**
   - Photograph all existing conditions before work
   - Document deterioration patterns and causes
   - Test and analyze existing materials
   - Create detailed condition assessment reports

2. **Material Analysis**
   - Conduct laboratory analysis of historic materials
   - Identify original composition and properties
   - Test compatibility of repair materials
   - Document findings for future reference

### 5.2 Material Selection and Sourcing

#### 5.2.1 Historic Material Matching
1. **Traditional Materials**
   - Source materials matching original composition
   - Use reclaimed materials when appropriate
   - Verify authenticity and period appropriateness
   - Test material compatibility and performance

2. **Modern Compatible Materials**
   - Select materials with similar properties to originals
   - Ensure physical and chemical compatibility
   - Verify long-term performance characteristics
   - Obtain preservation professional approval

#### 5.2.2 Specialized Procurement
1. **Traditional Suppliers**
   - Identify suppliers of historic building materials
   - Verify quality and authenticity certifications
   - Coordinate custom manufacturing when needed
   - Maintain supplier relationships for future projects

2. **Quality Control**
   - Inspect all materials upon delivery
   - Verify specifications and authenticity
   - Test samples before installation
   - Document material sources and properties

### 5.3 Traditional Installation Techniques

#### 5.3.1 Clay Tile Restoration
1. **Tile Assessment and Salvage**
   - Carefully remove tiles to assess condition
   - Clean and sort tiles by condition and usability
   - Repair minor damage using traditional techniques
   - Document tile patterns and installation methods

2. **Traditional Installation Methods**
   - Use period-appropriate fastening systems
   - Follow historic installation patterns
   - Apply traditional pointing and bedding techniques
   - Maintain original roof profiles and details

3. **New Tile Integration**
   - Source matching replacement tiles
   - Age new tiles to blend with existing
   - Install using compatible techniques
   - Maintain visual continuity and authenticity

#### 5.3.2 Slate Roof Restoration
1. **Slate Evaluation and Repair**
   - Test slate condition using standard methods
   - Assess structural adequacy of existing system
   - Identify and source matching replacement slates
   - Repair minor damage using traditional techniques

2. **Installation Procedures**
   - Use snow guards and installation methods original to building
   - Maintain historic fastening patterns
   - Install flashing using period-appropriate materials
   - Preserve original roof equipment and features

#### 5.3.3 Wood Shingle Restoration
1. **Material Selection**
   - Source cedar shingles matching original species
   - Use traditional cutting and preparation methods
   - Age new shingles for visual compatibility
   - Follow historic grading and selection criteria

2. **Installation Techniques**
   - Use traditional fastening methods and materials
   - Maintain historic exposure and coursing patterns
   - Apply traditional ridge and hip details
   - Install gutters and trim using period methods

### 5.4 Metal Roof Restoration

#### 5.4.1 Historic Metal Assessment
1. **Material Identification**
   - Determine original metal type and composition
   - Assess coating systems and corrosion patterns
   - Test for hazardous materials (lead paint, asbestos)
   - Document original forming and joining techniques

2. **Condition Evaluation**
   - Assess structural adequacy of metal components
   - Identify areas requiring repair vs. replacement
   - Evaluate coating condition and compatibility
   - Plan repair sequencing and methodology

#### 5.4.2 Traditional Metal Work
1. **Repair Techniques**
   - Use traditional soldering and welding methods
   - Apply period-appropriate coating systems
   - Maintain original profiles and details
   - Use compatible expansion joint systems

2. **Replacement Components**
   - Fabricate replacements using traditional methods
   - Match original metal specifications
   - Use period-appropriate forming techniques
   - Install with traditional fastening systems

### 5.5 Documentation and Compliance

#### 5.5.1 Work Documentation
1. **Progress Documentation**
   - Photograph work at all stages
   - Document discoveries and unexpected conditions
   - Record material sources and specifications
   - Maintain daily work logs and reports

2. **Change Documentation**
   - Document any deviations from original scope
   - Obtain preservation professional approval for changes
   - Record rationale for modification decisions
   - Update project drawings and specifications

#### 5.5.2 Compliance Verification
1. **Inspection Coordination**
   - Schedule inspections with preservation professionals
   - Coordinate with SHPO representatives
   - Arrange building official inspections
   - Document inspection results and approvals

2. **Standards Compliance**
   - Verify work meets Secretary of Interior's Standards
   - Document preservation methodology used
   - Ensure tax credit compliance documentation
   - Complete required reporting forms

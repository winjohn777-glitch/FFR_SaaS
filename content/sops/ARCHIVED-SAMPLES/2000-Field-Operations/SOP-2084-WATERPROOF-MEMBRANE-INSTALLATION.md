# SOP-2084: Waterproof Membrane Installation

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### Purpose
To establish comprehensive procedures for waterproof membrane installation on all Florida First Roofing LLC projects, ensuring complete water protection, structural integrity, and long-term performance while addressing Florida's challenging weather conditions including heavy rainfall, hurricanes, and extreme moisture exposure.

### Scope
This SOP applies to all waterproof membrane installations including:
- Single-ply membrane systems (TPO, PVC, EPDM)
- Modified bitumen membrane systems
- Built-up roofing (BUR) membranes
- Liquid-applied membrane systems
- Self-adhering membrane systems
- Vapor barrier installations
- Air barrier membrane systems
- Below-grade waterproofing membranes

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**Waterproof Membrane**: A continuous barrier designed to prevent water penetration.

**Single-Ply Membrane**: A sheet of waterproof material applied in one layer.

**Multi-Ply Membrane**: A membrane system consisting of multiple layers.

**Fully Adhered**: A membrane completely bonded to the substrate.

**Mechanically Attached**: A membrane secured with mechanical fasteners.

**Ballasted**: A membrane held in place with ballast material.

**Seam**: The joint where two membrane sheets meet.

**Lap Seam**: An overlapping joint between membrane sheets.

- Cause: Contaminated surfaces
- Solution: Clean and re-install
- Prevention: Proper surface preparation

- Cause: Improper handling
- Solution: Re-position membrane
- Prevention: Careful installation

**Leaking Seams:**
- Cause: Poor seam construction

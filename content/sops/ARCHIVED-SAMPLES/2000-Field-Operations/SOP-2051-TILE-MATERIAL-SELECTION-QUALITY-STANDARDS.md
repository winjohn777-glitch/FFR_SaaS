# SOP-2051: Tile Material Selection and Quality Standards

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for selecting appropriate tile materials, verifying quality standards, ensuring hurricane compliance, and maintaining consistent material performance across all Florida First Roofing tile installation projects.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all personnel involved in tile material specification, procurement, quality inspection, and acceptance for residential and commercial roofing projects throughout Florida's hurricane-prone regions.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Materials Engineer**: Technical evaluation and performance specification
- **Procurement Manager**: Vendor qualification and material sourcing
- **Quality Inspector**: Material inspection and acceptance procedures
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate material requirements and delivery schedules
- **Installation Supervisor**: Field quality verification and installation suitability

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Testing Equipment:
- Digital calipers and precision measuring instruments
- Impact testing equipment for hurricane compliance verification
- Color matching tools and spectrophotometers
- Moisture content meters and absorption testing apparatus
- Weight scales and density measurement equipment

### Documentation Tools:
- Material specification databases and manufacturer catalogs
- Quality control checklists and inspection forms
- Photographic equipment for documentation
- Barcode scanners for inventory tracking
- Mobile devices for field inspection and reporting

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Verify proper lifting techniques for heavy tile materials
- Ensure adequate storage support for material weight loads
- Implement dust control measures during tile cutting and handling
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment for sharp edges
- Plan for safe material distribution and installation procedures
- Consider weather protection requirements for material storage

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Material Specification and Selection Criteria
1. **Performance Requirements Analysis**:
   - Determine wind resistance requirements per project location
   - Establish minimum breaking strength and impact resistance
   - Specify freeze-thaw resistance for applicable climates
   - Define thermal performance and energy efficiency requirements
   - Establish color retention and UV resistance standards

2. **Florida-Specific Requirements**:
   - Verify Miami-Dade hurricane approval and test protocol compliance
   - Confirm Florida Building Code Chapter 15 tile standards
   - Check wind uplift resistance per HVHZ requirements
   - Verify impact resistance for hurricane debris simulation
   - Confirm salt air resistance for coastal installations

3. **Aesthetic and Functional Criteria**:
   - Define color consistency and matching standards
   - Establish texture and surface finish requirements
   - Specify dimensional tolerances and uniformity standards
   - Determine weight limitations and structural compatibility
   - Plan for maintenance accessibility and replacement availability

### Phase 2: Tile Type Evaluation and Comparison
1. **Concrete Tile Assessment**:
   - **Advantages**: Cost-effectiveness, color variety, local availability
   - **Performance**: Impact resistance, weight considerations, longevity
   - **Quality Standards**: Minimum 28-day cure strength, density requirements
   - **Hurricane Compliance**: Verify impact and wind uplift testing
   - **Warranty Coverage**: Standard 30-50 year material warranties

2. **Clay Tile Evaluation**:
   - **Performance Characteristics**: Natural durability, thermal properties
   - **Quality Indicators**: Firing temperature, absorption rates, color consistency
   - **Hurricane Resistance**: Impact testing and wind uplift performance
   - **Sourcing Considerations**: Import quality, delivery reliability
   - **Cost Analysis**: Premium pricing versus longevity benefits

3. **Composite and Synthetic Options**:
   - **Material Composition**: Recycled content, environmental impact
   - **Performance Testing**: UV resistance, thermal cycling, impact resistance
   - **Installation Advantages**: Weight reduction, cutting characteristics
   - **Long-term Performance**: Warranty coverage and proven track record
   - **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Initial cost versus lifecycle performance

### Phase 3: Vendor Qualification and Material Sourcing
1. **Manufacturer Evaluation Criteria**:
   - **Quality Management**: ISO certification and quality control systems
   - **Testing Compliance**: ASTM standards and Florida approval testing
   - **Production Capacity**: Ability to meet project volume and timeline
   - **Technical Support**: Installation guidance and problem resolution
   - **Warranty Support**: Claims processing and replacement availability

2. **Vendor Performance Assessment**:
   - Delivery reliability and on-time performance history
   - Quality consistency and defect rate tracking
   - Customer service responsiveness and technical support
   - Pricing competitiveness and payment terms
   - Geographic coverage and distribution network

3. **Material Certification Requirements**:
   - Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA) documentation
   - ASTM C1167 testing compliance for concrete tiles
   - ASTM C1024 testing compliance for clay tiles
   - ICC-ES evaluation reports for new products
   - Fire resistance ratings and code compliance documentation

### Phase 4: Quality Control and Acceptance Procedures
1. **Incoming Material Inspection**:
   - Visual inspection for chips, cracks, and surface defects
   - Dimensional verification using statistical sampling methods
   - Color consistency checking across production lots
   - Weight verification and density measurements
   - Documentation of delivery condition and packaging quality

2. **Performance Testing Verification**:
   - Random sampling for impact resistance testing
   - Water absorption testing per ASTM standards
   - Dimensional tolerance verification and documentation
   - Color fastness and UV resistance spot checking
   - Breakage assessment and handling damage evaluation

3. **Storage and Handling Quality Control**:
   - Proper stacking and support to prevent damage
   - Weather protection and moisture control measures
   - First-in, first-out rotation for inventory management
   - Damage prevention during handling and distribution
   - Regular inspection of stored materials for deterioration

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Dimensional tolerance: Maximum 2% variation from specified dimensions
- Color consistency: Delta E color difference maximum 1.5 units within lots
- Breakage rate: Maximum 2% breakage during delivery and handling
- Impact resistance: 100% compliance with Miami-Dade testing requirements
- Water absorption: Compliance with ASTM standards for tile type

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Issues:
- **Color variations**: Coordinate with manufacturer for lot matching
- **Dimensional inconsistencies**: Implement enhanced inspection procedures
- **Delivery damage**: Document damage and coordinate replacement
- **Performance deficiencies**: Verify testing compliance and specifications
- **Availability issues**: Coordinate alternative sources and scheduling

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document quality issue details and impact assessment
2. Contact manufacturer or vendor for immediate resolution
3. Implement temporary storage or protection measures
4. Coordinate replacement materials and delivery scheduling
5. Update quality control procedures to prevent recurrence

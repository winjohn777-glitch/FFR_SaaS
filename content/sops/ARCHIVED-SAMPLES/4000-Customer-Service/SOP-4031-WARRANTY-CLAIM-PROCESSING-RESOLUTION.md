# SOP-4031: Warranty Claim Processing and Resolution

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for warranty claim intake, processing, investigation, and resolution to ensure timely, fair, and professional handling of all warranty issues while maintaining customer satisfaction and manufacturer relationships.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all warranty claim processing, investigation, and resolution activities for material warranties, workmanship warranties, and system performance warranties throughout Florida First Roofing operations.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Warranty Claims Manager**: Oversee claim processing and resolution coordination
- **Claims Processor**: Handle claim intake, documentation, and initial assessment
- **Service Technician**: Conduct claim investigations and perform warranty repairs
- **Customer Service Representative**: Manage customer communication and satisfaction
- **Quality Inspector**: Assess claim validity and quality compliance

## 4. Required Materials and Equipment
### Claim Processing Systems:
- Warranty claim management software and tracking systems
- Digital documentation and photographic equipment
- Mobile devices for field claim investigation and reporting
- Communication systems for customer and manufacturer coordination
- Claim analysis and decision support tools

### Investigation Equipment:
- Diagnostic tools for roof and system assessment
- Moisture detection and thermal imaging equipment
- Testing equipment for material and installation verification
- Measurement tools for damage assessment and documentation
- Safety equipment for claim investigation work

## 5. Safety Considerations
- Implement safety protocols for claim investigation and service work
- Use appropriate fall protection during warranty claim inspections
- Assess structural safety before conducting detailed claim investigations
- Coordinate safety requirements with emergency response if needed
- Ensure claim investigation personnel training and safety certification
- Plan for weather-related safety considerations during claim assessment

## 6. Pre-Operation Checklist
- [ ] Verify claim receipt and initial customer contact completion
- [ ] Review customer information and warranty coverage details
- [ ] Prepare claim investigation equipment and documentation
- [ ] Confirm service technician availability and training certification
- [ ] Review applicable warranty terms and coverage limitations
- [ ] Establish customer communication and coordination schedule
- [ ] Verify insurance and liability coverage for claim activities

## 7. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Warranty Claim Intake and Initial Assessment
1. **Claim Receipt and Customer Contact**:
   - **Initial Contact**: Acknowledge claim receipt within 4 hours
   - **Customer Information**: Verify customer and property information
   - **Issue Description**: Obtain detailed description of reported problem
   - **Urgency Assessment**: Determine urgency and safety considerations
   - **Initial Documentation**: Create claim file and assign tracking number

2. **Warranty Coverage Verification**:
   - **Warranty Status**: Verify active warranty coverage and terms
   - **Coverage Analysis**: Assess reported issue against warranty coverage
   - **Exclusion Review**: Check for warranty exclusions or limitations
   - **Documentation Review**: Review installation and warranty documentation
   - **Preliminary Assessment**: Initial determination of coverage likelihood

3. **Claim Prioritization and Scheduling**:
   - **Emergency Issues**: Immediate response for safety or severe damage
   - **Urgent Claims**: 24-48 hour response for significant issues
   - **Standard Claims**: 3-5 business day response for routine issues
   - **Investigation Scheduling**: Coordinate investigation with customer availability
   - **Resource Allocation**: Assign appropriate personnel and equipment

### Phase 2: Claim Investigation and Assessment
1. **On-Site Investigation and Documentation**:
   - **Visual Inspection**: Comprehensive inspection of reported issue area
   - **Photographic Documentation**: Detailed photographs of conditions and damage
   - **Measurement and Testing**: Precise measurement and testing of affected areas
   - **Related Area Assessment**: Inspection of surrounding areas for related issues
   - **Environmental Factors**: Assessment of weather and environmental contributions

2. **Root Cause Analysis and Determination**:
   - **Installation Review**: Assess installation compliance with specifications
   - **Material Analysis**: Evaluate material condition and performance
   - **Environmental Impact**: Consider weather, aging, and external factors
   - **Maintenance History**: Review customer maintenance and care history
   - **Causation Determination**: Identify primary cause of reported issue

3. **Coverage Analysis and Decision Making**:
   - **Warranty Applicability**: Determine coverage under applicable warranties
   - **Manufacturer Consultation**: Coordinate with manufacturers when appropriate
   - **Engineering Review**: Obtain technical review for complex issues
   - **Cost Assessment**: Estimate repair costs and coverage implications
   - **Decision Documentation**: Document coverage decision and rationale

### Phase 3: Claim Resolution and Service Delivery
1. **Customer Communication and Approval**:
   - **Investigation Results**: Communicate findings and coverage determination
   - **Resolution Options**: Present available resolution alternatives
   - **Service Planning**: Coordinate service scheduling and logistics
   - **Customer Approval**: Obtain customer approval for proposed resolution
   - **Timeline Communication**: Provide realistic timeline for claim resolution

2. **Warranty Service Execution**:
   - **Service Preparation**: Coordinate materials, personnel, and equipment
   - **Quality Standards**: Execute service using company quality standards
   - **Safety Compliance**: Maintain safety protocols during service work
   - **Progress Communication**: Keep customer informed of service progress
   - **Completion Verification**: Verify service completion and quality standards

3. **Service Documentation and Follow-Up**:
   - **Service Documentation**: Complete documentation of work performed
   - **Before/After Photos**: Document conditions before and after service
   - **Customer Acceptance**: Obtain customer approval and satisfaction confirmation
   - **Warranty Updates**: Update warranty records and service history
   - **Preventive Recommendations**: Provide maintenance recommendations when appropriate

### Phase 4: Claim Closure and Quality Assurance
1. **Claim Resolution Documentation**:
   - **Complete Records**: Compile all claim documentation and records
   - **Service Summary**: Summarize investigation findings and resolution
   - **Cost Documentation**: Document all costs and expense allocation
   - **Customer Satisfaction**: Verify customer satisfaction with resolution
   - **File Closure**: Complete claim file and archive documentation

2. **Quality Assurance and Follow-Up**:
   - **Service Quality Review**: Assess quality of claim investigation and resolution
   - **Customer Satisfaction Survey**: Conduct customer satisfaction assessment
   - **Process Improvement**: Identify opportunities for process enhancement
   - **Training Needs**: Assess training needs based on claim experience
   - **Follow-Up Schedule**: Establish follow-up schedule for complex resolutions

3. **Performance Analysis and Reporting**:
   - **Claim Metrics**: Analyze claim resolution time and customer satisfaction
   - **Cost Analysis**: Review claim costs and impact on profitability
   - **Trend Identification**: Identify patterns and recurring issues
   - **Quality Improvement**: Develop quality improvement recommendations
   - **Reporting**: Provide management and manufacturer reporting as required

## 8. Quality Control Standards
- Response time: Initial customer contact within 4 hours of claim receipt
- Investigation timeliness: On-site investigation within 5 business days
- Resolution time: 95% of claims resolved within 10 business days
- Customer satisfaction: 95% customer satisfaction with claim resolution process
- Documentation completeness: 100% complete claim documentation and records

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide
### Common Claim Processing Issues:
- **Coverage disputes**: Review warranty terms and seek manufacturer consultation
- **Access problems**: Coordinate alternative investigation and service methods
- **Complex technical issues**: Obtain engineering consultation and technical support
- **Customer dissatisfaction**: Enhance communication and service delivery
- **Resource constraints**: Prioritize claims and coordinate additional resources

### Resolution Procedures:
1. Document claim processing issues and challenges encountered
2. Escalate complex issues to management and technical experts
3. Maintain customer communication throughout issue resolution
4. Implement alternative solutions while maintaining quality standards
5. Update procedures to prevent similar issues in future claims

# SOP-7010: OSHA 10-Hour Construction Training Program

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes comprehensive protocols for implementing and managing the OSHA 10-Hour Construction Industry Training Program at Florida First Roofing LLC, ensuring all employees receive proper safety training and certification in compliance with federal and Florida workplace safety requirements.

### Scope
- OSHA 10-Hour training program implementation and delivery
- Employee enrollment and completion tracking
- Certification verification and record maintenance
- Training content customization for roofing industry specifics
- Renewal and continuing education requirements
- Integration with company safety management systems

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Employee Enrollment Process
```
Enrollment Requirements:
□ New employee enrollment within 30 days of hire
□ Existing employee assessment and enrollment as needed
□ Supervisory personnel advanced training enrollment
□ Specialized role-specific additional training identification
□ Training schedule coordination and conflict resolution

Pre-Training Assessment:
- Previous safety training verification and credit evaluation
- Job role safety requirement identification and customization
- Learning style assessment and accommodation planning
- Language preference and accessibility requirement identification
- Training schedule preference and availability coordination
```

### 5.2 OSHA 10-Hour Training Curriculum
```
Required Training Topics (10 hours minimum):
Hour 1-2: Introduction to OSHA and Construction Safety
- OSHA history and mission
- Worker rights and employer responsibilities
- How to file a complaint and request inspections
- Construction industry accident statistics and trends

Hour 3-4: Fall Protection and Prevention
- Fall protection planning and systems
- Personal fall arrest systems and equipment
- Guardrail systems and safety nets
- Ladder safety and scaffold requirements

Hour 5-6: Electrical Safety in Construction
- Electrical hazard identification and control
- Lockout/tagout procedures and verification
- Grounding and electrical safety devices
- Power tool safety and maintenance

Hour 7-8: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- PPE selection and use requirements
- Head, eye, and hearing protection
- Respiratory protection and fit testing
- Hand and foot protection selection

Hour 9-10: Tool and Equipment Safety
- Hand and power tool safety procedures
- Equipment inspection and maintenance
- Crane and material handling safety
- Motor vehicle and heavy equipment operation
```

### 5.3 Roofing Industry Customization
```
Additional Focus Areas:
- Roof work fall protection systems and requirements
- Ladder and scaffold safety for roofing applications
- Material handling and storage on rooftops
- Weather-related safety considerations and protocols
- Hot work permits and fire prevention on roofs

Florida-Specific Considerations:
- Hurricane and severe weather safety protocols
- Heat stress prevention and recognition
- Lightning safety and electrical storm procedures
- Local code compliance and permit requirements
- Emergency response and evacuation procedures
```

### 5.4 Assessment and Certification
```
Assessment Methods:
- Written examination (minimum 70% passing score)
- Practical demonstration of safety procedures
- Participation and engagement during training sessions
- Understanding demonstration through Q&A sessions

Certification Requirements:
- Complete 10-hour minimum training attendance
- Pass written examination with 70% or higher score
- Demonstrate understanding of key safety concepts
- Receive official OSHA 10-Hour Construction card
- Maintain certification through continuing education
```

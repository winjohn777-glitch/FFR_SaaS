# SOP-2087: Skylight Installation and Integration

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for skylight installation and integration with roofing systems, ensuring proper weatherproofing, structural integrity, and energy efficiency while meeting Florida building codes and hurricane resistance requirements.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all field crews, project managers, and glazing specialists involved in skylight installation for residential and commercial roofing projects, including new construction and retrofit applications.

## 3. Definitions
- **Skylight Curb**: Raised frame structure that supports the skylight unit
- **Flashing System**: Integrated waterproofing materials around skylight perimeter
- **Glazing Unit**: Glass or plastic panel assembly within skylight frame
- **Impact Rating**: Resistance to projectile impact during severe weather
- **U-Factor**: Measure of heat transfer through skylight assembly
- **SHGC**: Solar Heat Gain Coefficient measuring solar energy transmission

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Coordination, permit compliance, quality oversight
- **Glazing Specialist**: Skylight selection, installation, and sealing
- **Structural Technician**: Framing modifications, load path verification
- **Waterproofing Technician**: Flashing installation, leak prevention
- **Safety Coordinator**: Fall protection, material handling safety

## 5. Prerequisites
- Building permits and structural approvals
- Skylight units delivered and inspected
- Roof structure prepared and verified
- Weather conditions suitable for installation
- Safety systems and equipment in place

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Materials:
- Skylight units (hurricane-rated for Florida)
- Curb materials (aluminum or treated wood)
- Flashing materials (step, head, and sill flashings)
- Sealants and adhesives
- Insulation materials
- Interior finishing materials

### Equipment:
- Reciprocating saw or roof cutting tools
- Framing square and measuring tools
- Caulk guns and sealant applicators
- Safety equipment and fall protection
- Material hoists or crane equipment

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Pre-Installation Planning
1. **Design Verification**
   - Review architectural plans and specifications
   - Verify skylight size and positioning
   - Check structural requirements and modifications
   - Confirm hurricane rating compliance
   - Plan integration with existing roofing system

2. **Site Preparation**
   - Mark skylight locations accurately
   - Verify utility locations and clearances
   - Set up safety perimeter and fall protection
   - Stage materials and equipment
   - Check weather forecast for installation window

### 7.2 Structural Modifications
1. **Opening Creation**
   - Cut roof decking to exact dimensions
   - Frame opening with properly sized headers
   - Install additional structural support as required
   - Verify square and level measurements
   - Clean up debris and prepare surface

2. **Curb Installation**
   - Build or install prefabricated curb
   - Ensure proper height (minimum 4 inches)
   - Secure to roof structure with appropriate fasteners
   - Verify level and square installation
   - Apply base flashing to curb

### 7.3 Waterproofing and Flashing
1. **Base Flashing Installation**
   - Install step flashing along sides
   - Install head flashing at top
   - Install sill flashing at bottom
   - Ensure proper overlap and water shedding
   - Apply continuous seal at all joints

2. **Integration with Roofing System**
   - Tie flashings into existing roof membrane
   - Apply compatible sealants at all interfaces
   - Install ice and water shield in critical areas
   - Verify drainage paths and slope
   - Test for potential leak points

### 7.4 Skylight Unit Installation
1. **Unit Placement**
   - Position skylight unit on prepared curb
   - Verify proper alignment and square placement
   - Check for level installation
   - Ensure adequate clearance for operation
   - Apply structural sealant as specified

2. **Securing and Sealing**
   - Install fasteners per manufacturer specifications
   - Apply weather sealant around perimeter
   - Install compression gaskets if required
   - Verify proper operation of operable units
   - Check for air and water infiltration

### 7.5 Florida Hurricane Compliance
1. **Impact Resistance**
   - Verify hurricane impact rating compliance
   - Install impact-resistant glazing
   - Use hurricane-rated fasteners and hardware
   - Document impact test certifications
   - Plan for storm shutter integration if required

2. **Wind Uplift Resistance**
   - Calculate and verify wind load resistance
   - Use appropriate fastener spacing and pattern
   - Install continuous attachment systems
   - Test attachment strength and integrity
   - Document compliance with Florida Building Code

3. **Water Penetration Prevention**
   - Design for extreme rainfall events
   - Install enhanced drainage systems
   - Use high-performance sealants
   - Plan for pressure differential effects
   - Include overflow drainage provisions

### 7.6 Energy Efficiency Optimization
1. **Thermal Performance**
   - Install thermal breaks where required
   - Use low-E glazing for energy efficiency
   - Add insulation around frame perimeter
   - Minimize thermal bridging
   - Verify U-factor compliance

2. **Solar Heat Gain Control**
   - Select appropriate SHGC ratings
   - Install interior or exterior shading
   - Consider automated controls
   - Plan for seasonal adjustments
   - Monitor performance over time

### 7.7 Quality Verification
1. **Water Testing**
   - Perform spray testing for water penetration
   - Check all flashing and sealant joints
   - Verify proper drainage and runoff
   - Document test results and photos
   - Address any identified deficiencies

2. **Operation Testing**
   - Test all operable components
   - Verify smooth operation and alignment
   - Check locking mechanisms and hardware
   - Test automated controls if installed
   - Document operational performance

## 8. Quality Standards
- Water penetration: Zero leaks under test conditions
- Structural attachment: Meet wind uplift requirements
- Energy performance: Achieve specified U-factor and SHGC
- Operation: Smooth operation throughout range of motion
- Appearance: Professional finish with uniform sealant lines

## 9. Safety Requirements
- Fall protection systems for all roof work
- Proper lifting techniques for heavy units
- Cut protection during opening creation
- Eye protection during cutting operations
- Respiratory protection when using sealants

## 10. Environmental Considerations

- Use of low-VOC sealants and adhesives
- Integration with natural lighting strategies
- Consideration of bird strike prevention

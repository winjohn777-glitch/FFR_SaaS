# SOP-0010: Emergency Response Protocol

## Purpose
Provide immediate response procedures for roofing emergencies to minimize property damage and ensure customer safety.

## Scope
Applies to all emergency response team members, dispatchers, and on-call personnel.

## Emergency Classification

### Level 1 - Critical Emergency (0-30 minute response)
- Active interior water intrusion
- Structural collapse or imminent failure
- Live electrical hazards from roof damage
- Fire damage with ongoing exposure risk

### Level 2 - Urgent Emergency (1-2 hour response)
- Contained leaks with limited interior damage
- Missing roof sections without structural compromise
- Significant storm damage requiring immediate temporary protection
- Insurance adjuster emergency requests

### Level 3 - Standard Emergency (2-6 hour response)
- Minor leaks identified after storms
- Loose materials creating safety concerns
- Preventive emergency services before predicted severe weather

## Response Procedures

### Immediate Response (0-15 minutes)
1. **Emergency Call Assessment**
   - Gather critical information using Emergency Intake Form
   - Determine emergency level classification
   - Verify property address and access information
   - Assess immediate safety concerns

2. **Team Dispatch**
   - Contact nearest certified emergency response team
   - Provide GPS coordinates and emergency details
   - Confirm ETA with customer
   - Alert backup team if primary unavailable

3. **Customer Safety Instructions**
   - Provide immediate safety guidance over phone
   - Instruct on emergency shut-offs if necessary
   - Advise on protective measures for belongings
   - Schedule safety check-in calls every 30 minutes

### On-Site Response (Upon Arrival)

#### Safety Assessment (First 10 minutes)
- Establish safety perimeter
- Check for electrical hazards
- Assess structural integrity
- Document safety concerns with photos

#### Immediate Stabilization (10-60 minutes)
- Apply emergency tarping using approved materials
- Secure loose materials
- Establish water diversion systems
- Install temporary supports if needed

#### Documentation and Communication (Throughout)
- Photo documentation every 15 minutes
- Real-time updates to customer and office
- Insurance notification within 30 minutes
- Temporary repair completion report

### Equipment and Materials

#### Standard Emergency Kit Contents:
- Heavy-duty tarps (multiple sizes)
- Emergency fasteners and hardware
- Water pumps and collection containers
- Safety equipment (harnesses, hard hats, gloves)
- Temporary lighting equipment
- Emergency contact cards

#### Vehicle Requirements:
- GPS tracking enabled
- Emergency beacon/strobe lights
- First aid kit and safety equipment
- Communication equipment (radio + cell backup)
- Full fuel tank (checked daily)

### Communication Protocol

#### Customer Updates:
- Initial contact confirmation within 5 minutes
- Arrival notification upon reaching property
- Hourly progress updates during emergency work
- Completion notification with next steps

#### Internal Communications:
- Emergency dispatch log entry
- Real-time status updates to emergency coordinator
- Completion report within 2 hours
- Follow-up inspection scheduling within 24 hours

#### Insurance and Third-Party Notifications:
- Insurance carrier notification within 30 minutes
- Local building department if structural concerns
- Utility companies if electrical/gas hazards present
- Emergency services coordination if required

### Quality and Safety Standards

#### Response Time Requirements:
- Level 1: 30 minutes maximum
- Level 2: 2 hours maximum
- Level 3: 6 hours maximum

#### Safety Compliance:
- OSHA fall protection requirements
- Local safety regulations compliance
- Company safety protocols adherence
- Incident reporting within 15 minutes

#### Work Quality:
- Temporary repairs must withstand 48 hours minimum
- All work photographically documented
- Customer approval for all emergency measures
- Code compliance for temporary installations

### Documentation Requirements

#### Emergency Response Log:
- Call received timestamp
- Team dispatched timestamp
- On-site arrival timestamp
- Work completion timestamp
- Customer satisfaction confirmation

#### Photographic Documentation:
- Before conditions (all angles)
- Safety hazard identification
- Work in progress (every 15 minutes)
- Completed temporary repairs
- Material and equipment used

#### Written Reports:
- Emergency Assessment Report (within 2 hours)
- Temporary Repair Completion Report
- Follow-up Inspection Scheduling
- Insurance Claim Support Documentation

### Post-Emergency Procedures

#### Immediate (0-4 hours post-completion):
- Customer satisfaction check-in call
- Emergency equipment inventory and restocking
- Team debrief and lessons learned documentation
- Insurance claim initiation if applicable

#### Short-term (24-48 hours):
- Follow-up inspection scheduling
- Permanent repair estimate preparation
- Material ordering for permanent repairs
- Insurance adjuster coordination

#### Long-term (1 week):
- Permanent repair work commencement
- Emergency response performance review
- Customer feedback collection and analysis
- Process improvement identification

## Training Requirements

### Initial Certification:
- Emergency response procedures training (8 hours)
- Safety protocol certification
- Equipment operation training
- Customer communication skills training

### Ongoing Requirements:
- Monthly emergency drill participation
- Quarterly safety recertification
- Annual emergency response protocol update training
- Customer service skills refresher (semi-annual)

### Performance Metrics:
- Response time compliance: 98% minimum
- Customer satisfaction: 4.8/5 minimum
- Safety incident rate: Zero tolerance
- Equipment readiness: 100% compliance

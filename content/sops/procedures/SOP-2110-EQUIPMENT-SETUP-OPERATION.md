# SOP-2110: Equipment Setup and Operation

## 1. Purpose
Establish standardized procedures for setup, operation, and maintenance of roofing equipment to ensure safe operation, optimal performance, and extended equipment life while maintaining compliance with manufacturer specifications and OSHA requirements.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all equipment operators, field supervisors, and maintenance personnel involved in setup, operation, and maintenance of roofing equipment including cranes, hoists, compressors, welding equipment, and specialized roofing tools.

## 3. Definitions
- **Pre-Operation Inspection**: Safety and functionality check before equipment use
- **Operational Parameters**: Manufacturer-specified operating limits and conditions
- **Preventive Maintenance**: Scheduled maintenance to prevent equipment failure
- **Equipment Certification**: Documentation of equipment safety and operational compliance
- **Load Calculations**: Engineering analysis of equipment lifting and operational capacities

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Equipment Operator**: Proper operation, daily inspections, basic maintenance
- **Equipment Supervisor**: Setup oversight, safety compliance, performance monitoring
- **Maintenance Technician**: Preventive maintenance, repairs, certification management
- **Safety Coordinator**: Safety compliance verification, incident investigation
- **Project Manager**: Equipment coordination, scheduling, cost management

## 5. Prerequisites
- Equipment operator certification and training current
- Equipment inspection and maintenance records reviewed
- Site conditions assessed for equipment operation
- Safety equipment and procedures verified
- Weather conditions suitable for operation

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Setup Materials:
- Rigging hardware and slings
- Outrigger pads and blocking materials
- Safety barriers and warning devices
- Fuel, lubricants, and operational fluids
- Certification and inspection documentation

### Safety Equipment:
- Fall protection systems
- Personal protective equipment
- Emergency shut-off systems
- Communication devices
- First aid and emergency equipment

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Pre-Operation Planning
1. **Site Assessment**
   - Evaluate ground conditions for stability
   - Check overhead clearances and obstacles
   - Identify utilities and underground hazards
   - Assess access routes and positioning
   - Plan for weather contingencies

2. **Equipment Selection**
   - Select appropriate equipment for job requirements
   - Verify equipment capacity for planned loads
   - Check equipment condition and maintenance status
   - Ensure operator certification for specific equipment
   - Plan for backup equipment if needed

### 7.2 Equipment Setup Procedures
1. **Initial Positioning**
   - Position equipment on stable, level ground
   - Set up proper blocking and outrigger pads
   - Establish safe working distances
   - Install barriers and warning devices
   - Verify proper orientation for operation

2. **Safety System Activation**
   - Test all safety systems and devices
   - Set up fall protection systems
   - Establish communication systems
   - Post safety signs and warnings
   - Brief all personnel on safety procedures

### 7.3 Pre-Operation Inspections
1. **Visual Inspection**
   - Check for obvious damage or wear
   - Verify all guards and safety devices in place
   - Inspect rigging and lifting equipment
   - Check fluid levels and leaks
   - Verify proper tire inflation and condition

2. **Functional Testing**
   - Test all controls and operating systems
   - Verify proper operation of safety devices
   - Check lifting capacity and load charts
   - Test communication systems
   - Document inspection results

### 7.4 Equipment Operation Procedures
1. **Startup Procedures**
   - Follow manufacturer startup sequence
   - Allow proper warm-up time for equipment
   - Test all systems at low load/speed
   - Verify proper operation before full load
   - Monitor gauges and warning systems

2. **Operational Guidelines**
   - Operate within manufacturer specifications
   - Monitor load limits and operating parameters
   - Maintain safe distances from personnel
   - Follow proper lifting and rigging procedures
   - Document operational hours and performance

### 7.5 Florida Climate Equipment Considerations
1. **Heat Management**
   - Monitor equipment operating temperatures
   - Provide adequate cooling and ventilation
   - Adjust operating schedules for extreme heat
   - Use heat-resistant materials and lubricants
   - Plan for more frequent maintenance intervals

2. **Humidity and Corrosion Protection**
   - Apply corrosion protection to exposed metals
   - Monitor for accelerated wear from salt air
   - Use moisture-resistant electrical components
   - Implement enhanced cleaning and maintenance
   - Store equipment in controlled environments when possible

3. **Storm Preparation**
   - Secure equipment against high winds
   - Plan for rapid equipment shutdown
   - Protect electrical systems from water
   - Implement enhanced tie-down procedures
   - Document storm preparation activities

### 7.6 Lifting and Rigging Operations
1. **Load Calculation and Planning**
   - Calculate total load including rigging weight
   - Verify equipment capacity for planned lifts
   - Plan lift path and clearances
   - Identify potential hazards and obstacles
   - Brief all personnel on lift plan

2. **Rigging Procedures**
   - Inspect all rigging equipment before use
   - Use proper sling angles and load distribution
   - Secure load to prevent movement during lifting
   - Test lift at low height before full lift
   - Maintain communication throughout operation

### 7.7 Maintenance and Care
1. **Daily Maintenance**
   - Perform daily inspection and lubrication
   - Check fluid levels and add as needed
   - Clean equipment and remove debris
   - Document maintenance activities
   - Report any issues or concerns immediately

2. **Scheduled Maintenance**
   - Follow manufacturer maintenance schedules
   - Replace filters, belts, and wear items
   - Perform system calibrations and adjustments
   - Update maintenance records and certifications
   - Plan for major overhauls and rebuilds

### 7.8 Emergency Procedures
1. **Equipment Failure Response**
   - Implement immediate shutdown procedures
   - Secure area and evacuate personnel if needed
   - Assess damage and safety implications
   - Document incident and notify management
   - Plan for equipment replacement or repair

2. **Weather Emergency Procedures**
   - Monitor weather conditions continuously
   - Implement shutdown procedures for severe weather
   - Secure equipment against wind and storm damage
   - Protect electrical systems from water damage
   - Document emergency response actions

### 7.9 Quality and Performance Monitoring
1. **Performance Tracking**
   - Monitor equipment productivity and efficiency
   - Track fuel consumption and operating costs
   - Document downtime and maintenance requirements
   - Analyze performance trends and issues
   - Plan for equipment upgrades and replacements

2. **Quality Assurance**
   - Verify equipment meets quality standards
   - Monitor work quality produced by equipment
   - Track customer satisfaction with equipment performance
   - Document quality issues and improvements
   - Implement continuous improvement programs

## 8. Quality Standards
- Safety compliance: 100% compliance with manufacturer and OSHA requirements
- Equipment availability: Minimum 95% operational availability
- Maintenance compliance: 100% compliance with scheduled maintenance
- Operator certification: All operators properly certified and trained
- Documentation: Complete and accurate equipment records

## 9. Safety Requirements
- Comprehensive equipment operator training and certification
- Daily safety inspections before operation
- Proper personal protective equipment for all operators
- Emergency shutdown and response procedures
- Regular safety training updates and refreshers

## 10. Environmental Considerations
- Proper disposal of fluids and maintenance materials
- Noise control measures for community compliance
- Emission control and monitoring where applicable
- Fuel efficiency optimization
- Environmental impact minimization

## 11. Documentation

- Maintenance schedules and completion documentation
- Safety incident and near-miss reports
- Equipment certification and compliance records

## 12. Training Requirements

- Safety training for equipment operation
- Maintenance procedures and requirements
- Emergency response and shutdown procedures
- Ongoing training for new equipment and updates

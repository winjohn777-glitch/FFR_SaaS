# SOP-2088: Solar Panel Mounting Systems

## 1. Purpose
Establish comprehensive procedures for installing solar panel mounting systems on various roof types, ensuring structural integrity, weatherproofing, and electrical safety while maintaining roofing system warranties and Florida hurricane compliance.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all field crews, electrical technicians, and project managers involved in solar panel mounting system installation for residential and commercial roofing projects, including ballasted, penetrating, and specialty mounting systems.

## 3. Definitions
- **Ballasted System**: Non-penetrating mounting system using weight for retention
- **Penetrating Mount**: System secured through roof membrane with sealed attachments
- **Rail System**: Aluminum framework supporting solar panel arrays
- **Microinverter**: Individual inverter attached to each solar panel
- **String Inverter**: Central inverter managing multiple solar panels
- **MLPE**: Module Level Power Electronics (optimizers and microinverters)

## 4. Responsibilities
- **Project Manager**: Overall coordination, permit compliance, utility coordination
- **Solar Installation Specialist**: Mounting system design and installation
- **Roofing Technician**: Waterproofing integration, flashing installation
- **Electrical Technician**: Grounding, wiring, and electrical connections
- **Structural Engineer**: Load calculations and mounting point verification

## 5. Prerequisites
- Structural engineering analysis for roof loading
- Electrical permits and utility interconnection approval
- Solar panel and mounting system delivery and inspection
- Roof condition assessment and any necessary repairs
- Safety systems and equipment verification

## 6. Materials and Equipment
### Materials:
- Solar mounting rails and hardware
- Roof attachment points (bolts, screws, clamps)
- Waterproofing components (flashings, sealants)
- Grounding equipment and conductors
- Electrical conduit and wiring components
- Equipment labels and placards

### Equipment:
- Drill with appropriate bits
- Torque wrenches and calibrated tools
- Electrical testing equipment
- Sealant application tools
- Material hoists and safety equipment

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Pre-Installation Assessment
1. **Structural Verification**
   - Verify roof structure can support additional loads
   - Identify optimal mounting point locations
   - Check for structural members and spacing
   - Document any structural concerns
   - Obtain structural engineer approval if required

2. **Roof Condition Evaluation**
   - Inspect roof membrane for damage or wear
   - Verify remaining roof warranty terms
   - Identify areas requiring repair before installation
   - Check roof slope and drainage patterns
   - Document existing conditions with photos

### 7.2 System Layout and Design
1. **Panel Array Planning**
   - Optimize panel placement for solar exposure
   - Plan for proper spacing and access
   - Consider shading from HVAC equipment
   - Account for fire setback requirements
   - Design for aesthetic considerations

2. **Mounting Point Layout**
   - Mark mounting locations according to plan
   - Verify structural member alignment
   - Plan cable routing and management
   - Consider maintenance access requirements
   - Account for thermal expansion

### 7.3 Mounting System Installation
1. **Ballasted Systems (Flat Roofs)**
   - Install protection pads under ballast blocks
   - Position mounting bases according to layout
   - Add specified ballast weight for wind uplift
   - Verify system stability and level
   - Document ballast distribution

2. **Penetrating Systems (Sloped Roofs)**
   - Drill pilot holes at marked locations
   - Install appropriate lag bolts or screws
   - Apply structural sealant at penetrations
   - Install flashing and weatherproofing
   - Torque fasteners to specification

3. **Rail Installation**
   - Install mounting rails on attachment points
   - Verify proper alignment and spacing
   - Check for level and straight installation
   - Secure all connections to specification
   - Install end caps and safety features

### 7.4 Waterproofing Integration
1. **Penetration Sealing**
   - Install appropriate flashing systems
   - Apply compatible sealants around penetrations
   - Integrate with existing roof membrane
   - Verify proper water shedding
   - Test for water penetration

2. **Drainage Considerations**
   - Maintain proper roof drainage patterns
   - Install cricket or diverters as needed
   - Check for potential ponding areas
   - Plan for increased snow/debris loads
   - Monitor drainage performance

### 7.5 Florida Hurricane Compliance
1. **Wind Load Calculations**
   - Verify system meets Florida wind load requirements
   - Use hurricane-rated mounting hardware
   - Calculate uplift forces and resistance
   - Document engineering compliance
   - Plan for extreme weather events

2. **Attachment Requirements**
   - Use corrosion-resistant fasteners
   - Meet minimum penetration depths
   - Install specified fastener patterns
   - Verify attachment to structural members
   - Document installation compliance

3. **System Integration**
   - Coordinate with existing roof equipment
   - Plan for potential impact damage
   - Design accessible mounting points
   - Consider post-storm inspection needs
   - Document emergency shutdown procedures

### 7.6 Electrical Installation
1. **Grounding System**
   - Install equipment grounding conductors
   - Connect all metallic components
   - Verify grounding electrode connections
   - Test grounding system resistance
   - Document electrical compliance

2. **DC Wiring Installation**
   - Route wiring through proper raceways
   - Secure cables to prevent movement
   - Install proper connectors and fittings
   - Label all circuits and equipment
   - Test for proper polarity and continuity

3. **Safety and Shutdown Systems**
   - Install rapid shutdown devices as required
   - Label all DC circuits and equipment
   - Install arc fault protection
   - Verify emergency shutdown operation
   - Document safety system functionality

### 7.7 Quality Verification and Testing
1. **Mechanical Inspection**
   - Verify all fasteners are properly torqued
   - Check mounting system alignment
   - Inspect all flashing and sealants
   - Test system stability and attachment
   - Document mechanical compliance

2. **Electrical Testing**
   - Measure grounding system resistance
   - Test circuit continuity and polarity
   - Verify proper voltage and current readings
   - Test safety shutdown systems
   - Document electrical performance

## 8. Quality Standards
- Structural attachment: Meet Florida wind load requirements
- Waterproofing: Zero leaks at all penetrations
- Electrical safety: All circuits properly grounded and protected
- System performance: Meet manufacturer specifications
- Code compliance: 100% compliance with electrical and building codes

## 9. Safety Requirements
- Fall protection for all elevated work
- Electrical safety procedures and lockout/tagout
- Proper lifting techniques for heavy components
- Personal protective equipment for all tasks
- Emergency response procedures for electrical incidents

## 10. Environmental Considerations

- Use recyclable mounting materials when possible
- Proper disposal of packaging and waste materials
- Integration with building energy management
- Long-term environmental benefits documentation

# SOP-0025: Safety Protocols and OSHA Compliance

## Purpose
Establish comprehensive safety protocols to protect workers, customers, and property while maintaining full OSHA compliance and Florida-specific safety requirements.

## Scope
Applies to all field personnel, supervisors, contractors, and any personnel accessing roof or elevated work areas.

## General Safety Requirements

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
#### Mandatory PPE for All Roof Work:
- **Fall Protection Harness** (ANSI Z359.11 compliant)
  - Full-body harness with D-rings
  - 6-foot shock-absorbing lanyard minimum
  - Fall arrestor for steep slope work
  - Inspection and certification current

- **Footwear Requirements**
  - Non-slip, rubber-soled work boots
  - Ankle support mandatory
  - Steel toe protection for material handling
  - Weather-appropriate tread patterns

- **Head and Eye Protection**
  - Hard hats (ANSI Z89.1 Class C minimum)
  - Safety glasses with side shields
  - Sun protection (hat/visor) for extended exposure
  - Face shields for debris removal

- **Hand and Body Protection**
  - Cut-resistant work gloves (ANSI A3 minimum)
  - Weather-appropriate protective clothing
  - High-visibility vests for ground personnel
  - Sun protection clothing for UV exposure

### Pre-Work Safety Assessment

#### Weather Conditions (Mandatory Check)
- **Prohibited Conditions:**
  - Wind speeds exceeding 25 mph sustained
  - Active precipitation (rain, snow, ice)
  - Lightning within 10 miles of worksite
  - Temperature below 40°F or above 105°F
  - Wet or icy roof conditions

- **Marginal Conditions (Enhanced Precautions):**
  - Wind speeds 15-25 mph
  - Humidity above 85% with heat index over 95°F
  - Overcast conditions with precipitation forecast
  - Dawn/dusk lighting conditions

#### Roof Condition Assessment
- **Structural Integrity Check:**
  - Visual inspection of decking condition
  - Load-bearing capacity evaluation
  - Identification of weak or damaged areas
  - Temporary support requirements assessment

- **Surface Condition Evaluation:**
  - Slip hazard identification
  - Debris and obstacle documentation
  - Edge condition assessment
  - Access route planning and marking

### Fall Protection Systems

#### Roof Pitch Classifications
- **Low Slope (0-4/12 pitch):**
  - Safety monitors and warning lines acceptable
  - Guardrails required within 6 feet of edges
  - Fall arrest systems for edge work
  - Travel restraint systems preferred

- **Steep Slope (4/12+ pitch):**
  - Fall arrest or guardrail systems mandatory
  - Catch platforms for material loading areas
  - Safety nets for large crew operations
  - Temporary roof anchors as needed

#### Anchor Point Requirements
- **Permanent Anchor Points:**
  - Certified for 5,000 lb minimum load capacity
  - Annual inspection and certification required
  - GPS mapping and identification numbering
  - Load testing documentation maintained

- **Temporary Anchor Points:**
  - Engineered for specific roof structure
  - 5,000 lb load capacity minimum
  - Installation by certified personnel only
  - Daily inspection and documentation

### Equipment Safety Standards

#### Ladder Safety Protocol
- **Setup Requirements:**
  - 4:1 ratio (height to base distance)
  - Secure top and bottom anchoring
  - Three-point contact rule enforcement
  - Maximum 30-foot single ladder height

- **Usage Standards:**
  - Face ladder when ascending/descending
  - Maintain three-point contact always
  - Tool belt or rope for tool lifting
  - One person per ladder maximum

#### Power Tool Safety
- **Electrical Safety:**
  - GFCI protection for all equipment
  - Regular electrical inspection schedules
  - Proper extension cord ratings and conditions
  - Lockout/tagout procedures for maintenance

- **Pneumatic and Hydraulic Tools:**
  - Daily pressure system checks
  - Proper hose routing and protection
  - Emergency shutoff accessibility
  - Regular maintenance scheduling

### Site Safety Management

#### Traffic and Public Safety
- **Perimeter Protection:**
  - Barrier tape minimum 10 feet from drop zones
  - Warning signs in English and Spanish
  - Dedicated spotter for material movement
  - Public access route protection

- **Material Handling Safety:**
  - Crane operations per OSHA 1926 Subpart CC
  - Material hoist safety inspections
  - Ground personnel hard hat requirements
  - Established material drop zones

#### Emergency Procedures
- **Fall Incident Response (5-minute protocol):**
  1. Immediate medical assessment
  2. Incident scene preservation
  3. Emergency services notification (911)
  4. Company safety officer notification
  5. Incident documentation initiation

- **Weather Emergency Response:**
  - Lightning strike procedures
  - High wind shutdown protocols
  - Severe weather evacuation plans
  - Equipment securing requirements

### Hot Weather Safety Protocol

#### Heat Illness Prevention
- **Work/Rest Schedules (Temperature-based):**
  - 80-85°F: 15-minute breaks every 2 hours
  - 86-90°F: 15-minute breaks every hour
  - 91-95°F: 20-minute breaks every hour
  - 96°F+: 30-minute breaks every hour + enhanced monitoring

- **Hydration Requirements:**
  - Minimum 8 oz water every 20 minutes
  - Electrolyte replacement for extended work
  - Cool water availability on-site
  - Ice packs for cooling stations

#### Heat Stress Monitoring
- **Buddy System Implementation:**
  - Paired worker monitoring
  - Heat stress symptom recognition training
  - Communication check intervals (every 30 minutes)
  - Immediate removal protocol for symptoms

### Cold Weather Safety Protocol

#### Hypothermia and Frostbite Prevention
- **Temperature-Based Protocols:**
  - Below 40°F: Enhanced layering requirements
  - Below 32°F: 15-minute warming breaks every hour
  - Below 20°F: Work restrictions and enhanced monitoring
  - Below 10°F: Work suspension consideration

- **Equipment Modifications:**
  - Insulated hand protection
  - Ice-removal equipment availability
  - Heated shelter provision
  - Tool winterization procedures

### Safety Training and Certification

#### Initial Safety Training (40-hour minimum)
- **OSHA 30-Hour Construction Training**
- **Fall Protection Competent Person Training**
- **First Aid/CPR Certification**
- **Company-Specific Safety Protocols**

#### Ongoing Training Requirements
- **Monthly Safety Meetings (2 hours minimum)**
  - Incident review and lessons learned
  - Seasonal safety topic focus
  - New equipment or procedure training
  - Regulatory update communications

- **Annual Recertification:**
  - Fall protection system training
  - Equipment inspection certification
  - Emergency response drill participation
  - Safety knowledge assessment

  - 29 CFR 1926 Subpart M (Fall Protection)
  - 29 CFR 1926 Subpart X (Stairways and Ladders)
  - 29 CFR 1926.95 (Personal Protective Equipment)
  - 29 CFR 1926.151 (Fire Protection)

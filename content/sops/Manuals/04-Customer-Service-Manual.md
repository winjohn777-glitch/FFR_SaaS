## 3. INITIAL CUSTOMER CONTACT {#initial-contact}

### 3.1 Lead Intake Process

#### First Impression Excellence
The initial contact sets the tone for the entire customer relationship. Every interaction should demonstrate professionalism, competence, and genuine care for the customer's needs.

#### Phone Intake Procedure
1. **Professional Greeting**: "Good [morning/afternoon], Florida First Roofing, this is [Name]. How may I help you today?"
2. **Active Listening**: Listen carefully to customer needs and concerns
3. **Information Gathering**: Collect essential customer and project information
4. **Scheduling**: Schedule inspection or consultation appointment
5. **Confirmation**: Confirm appointment details and contact information

### 3.2 Customer Information Collection

#### Essential Information
- **Contact Information**: Name, address, phone, email
- **Property Information**: Property type, age, roof details
- **Service Needs**: Type of service required (repair, replacement, inspection)
- **Urgency Level**: Emergency, urgent, or routine service
- **Preferred Communication**: Communication preferences and timing

#### Qualifying Questions
- "What type of roofing issue are you experiencing?"
- "When did you first notice the problem?"
- "Have you had any recent storms or weather events?"
- "What is your timeline for completing this project?"
- "Do you have any insurance claims pending?"

### 3.3 Emergency Response

#### Emergency Identification
- **Water Intrusion**: Active leaks or water damage
- **Structural Damage**: Compromised roof structure
- **Safety Hazards**: Falling debris or dangerous conditions
- **Storm Damage**: Recent storm-related damage

#### Emergency Response Protocol
1. **Immediate Assessment**: Assess emergency severity
2. **Safety First**: Ensure customer and property safety
3. **Rapid Dispatch**: Schedule emergency response within 2 hours
4. **Temporary Solutions**: Provide temporary protection if needed
5. **Follow-up Plan**: Establish plan for permanent repairs

### 3.4 Appointment Scheduling

#### Scheduling Best Practices
- **Customer Convenience**: Schedule at customer's convenience
- **Accurate Timing**: Provide realistic time estimates
- **Confirmation**: Confirm appointments 24 hours in advance
- **Backup Plans**: Have contingency plans for weather delays
- **Professional Arrival**: Arrive on time and professionally dressed

#### Preparation for Appointment
- **Customer File Review**: Review customer information before visit
- **Equipment Preparation**: Ensure all necessary equipment is ready
- **Route Planning**: Plan efficient travel routes
- **Communication**: Confirm appointment and arrival time
- **Materials**: Bring necessary forms, contracts, and materials
